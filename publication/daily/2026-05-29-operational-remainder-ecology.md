# 2026-05-29: Operational Remainder Ecology

Source observation: `observations/codex/2026-05-26-operational-remainder-ecology.md`
Promotion stage: Public Claim

## Finding

A contemplative path is shaped by three interacting factors: the operational capacities it requires, the residue policy it applies after negation, and the training ecology that calibrates those demands in lived practice. The operational profile names what must remain usable for the method to work, such as attention, memory, effort, reflexivity, receptivity, trust, or surrender. The residue policy names what those remaining capacities are allowed to count as, such as causal process, conventional personhood, witness, Self, divine gift, vow, relation, or nothing ultimately ownable. The practice ecology names the teacher, community, intensity, duration, screening, interpretation, and support conditions that determine whether the operational demand is stabilizing or destabilizing. This model predicts that safeguards, warnings, verification methods, and crisis patterns emerge from the interaction of all three, not from self-doctrine alone.

## Current Critique

The candidate synthesis may still overformalize living traditions. Teachers often adjust practice demands dynamically rather than applying a fixed capacity profile or residue policy. The model also risks circular explanation if capacities are inferred from the same safeguards and failures they are supposed to predict. Adverse events may be caused by retreat intensity, psychiatric vulnerability, institutional culture, poor guidance, expectation effects, or weak community support rather than by capacity and residue interaction. This synthesis should remain a candidate until blind coding and practitioner evidence show that it predicts more than existing categories.
