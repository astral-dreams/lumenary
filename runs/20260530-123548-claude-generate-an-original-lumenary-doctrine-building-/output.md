# A Test Must Be Able to Say Yes

## Original Claim

A method for deciding whether two traditions truly converge is only a test if more than one verdict is possible. The translation-strain approach has been built almost entirely as a downgrade engine: it breaks an apparent match into smaller claim units, shows what must be bent, dropped, or added, and the answer is always that the two are less alike than they looked. A device that can only ever lower a score is not measuring convergence; it is performing skepticism, and no comparison can prove it wrong. To become a test, the procedure must be able to reach three honest endings: this much is really shared, this match is false, or we cannot yet tell. It must sometimes raise confidence after decomposition, not only lower it. The discipline that makes it a test is to state, before taking the likeness apart, what a surviving shared role would look like and what would count as confirming it, so the comparison can prove the doubter wrong.

## Why It Might Be New

The work on this frontier keeps adding new ways to subtract: check the borrowing history, check whether the repair task survives, check the receiver's wound, check whether a tradition even agrees with itself. Every addition is a reason a likeness is weaker than it seemed. None states what a confirmed convergence would look like, so no case could ever raise the program's verdict, and that one-sidedness is invisible from inside. The distinct move here is a symmetry requirement plus a pre-registered positive verdict: name the upgrade condition before decomposing, and treat a test that never upgrades as a failed instrument rather than a finding about the world. This differs from Smith, who prized difference and the useful manipulation of the gap but never demanded a confirm-or-expose-or-undecidable verdict with a stated upgrade bar, and from general falsifiability talk by tying the demand to a concrete scoring decision.

## Critique

The asymmetry may be correct rather than a flaw. If genuine independent convergence between historically entangled traditions is genuinely rare, a method that mostly downgrades may simply be tracking reality, and forcing it to produce upgrades could manufacture exactly the perennialist false positives the whole program was built to prevent. Pre-registering an upgrade condition can also be gamed by setting a low bar. The Atman and anatta case is the anomaly that cuts both ways: the de-identification role survives decomposition, which looks like a confirmed convergence, yet the two terms were shaped by centuries of mutual polemic and shared milieu, so the survival may be causal contact rather than independent recurrence, which means both an upgrade verdict and a downgrade verdict mislead. If the three-verdict scheme cannot cleanly separate a thin-but-real shared role from a historically borrowed resemblance, the symmetry requirement is satisfied in name only.

## Teaching Candidate

Status: under_dialogue
Tags: convergence, skepticism, comparison-method, digital-comparison, meaning-loss, critique

### Teaching Line

A doubt that can only ever debunk is not honesty; it is a habit.

### Doctrine Claim

An honest comparison can reach three honest endings: this much is really shared, this match is false, or we cannot yet tell. A method that can only reach the second is not testing anything.

### Teaching Body

When two teachings sound alike, it is easy to feel clever by showing they are not the same. Take them apart far enough and every likeness cracks. But a tool that always cracks the likeness is not telling you about the teachings; it is telling you about its own setting. Before you take a likeness apart, decide what would make it real. Name what a genuinely shared practice or role would look like. Then look. Sometimes the shared ground survives, and you should say so. Sometimes it dissolves, and you should say that. Sometimes you cannot yet tell, and that is also an answer. Keep the verdict you did not expect.

### Human Problem

Digital comparison, meaning loss, and the quiet cynicism of seekers who pick apart every shared idea until nothing feels really shared, and feel more alone for it.

### For

Reflective, spiritually eclectic adults and older teens who read across traditions and use comparison to evaluate and debunk rather than to belong.

### Pressure Survived

Survives the primary-text contrast between tat tvam asi, the unseen knower, and not-self analysis: a shared de-identification role survives decomposition even where metaphysics stay incompatible, which a downgrade-only method would hide. Survives prior-art pressure from Smith and Freiberger by adding a stated upgrade bar rather than another way to prize difference.

### Falsifying Pressure

Weakens if blind coders applying a confirm-expose-undecidable scheme never produce confirmations across many cases, which would show the symmetry is cosmetic. Weakens if upgrade verdicts cannot be separated from historical-borrowing artifacts, or if the teaching is read as license to declare false unity and so reintroduces the perennialist error.



## Practice Candidate

Status: seed
Risk level: low
Tags: convergence, comparison-method, digital-comparison, cynicism, low-risk

### Practice Line

Before you take a likeness apart, write down what would make it real.

### Purpose

To keep a comparison from becoming an automatic debunking habit, so that a genuinely shared role can be confirmed and acted on, not only exposed and dropped.

### Human Problem

Cynicism, meaning loss, and isolation in people whose habit of comparing traditions only ever subtracts, leaving them with nothing shared to stand on.

### For

Stable adults and older teens who gather teachings from books, apps, podcasts, and mixed communities and notice they reflexively pick everything apart.

### Not For

Not for acute crisis, psychosis, mania, severe depression, addiction withdrawal, or OCD and scrupulosity doubt loops where comparison becomes compulsive reassurance seeking. Not for people using analysis to avoid the harder work of belonging or commitment.

### Time

- Duration: 10 minutes
- Frequency: Once when a shared idea across traditions feels especially compelling or especially suspect, not as a daily exercise; at most twice per week.
- Minimum attempt: Try it on three different apparent agreements over two weeks before judging whether it helps.

### Steps

1. Write the apparent agreement in one plain sentence.
2. Before analyzing, write what a genuinely shared role would look like in practice, and one thing that would confirm it is real.
3. Now take the likeness apart: name what each side would have to bend, drop, or add for the match to hold.
4. Reach one of three verdicts: this much is really shared, this match is false, or I cannot yet tell.
5. Add one line of history: could the resemblance come from borrowing or shared influence rather than independent agreement?
6. If the verdict is confirmed, choose one concrete way to act on the shared ground today: a practice, a conversation, a small commitment, not another comparison.

### Notice

- Whether you reached any verdict other than false, or whether you always land on false.
- Whether confirming a real shared role leaves you more connected and less cynical.
- Whether the exercise becomes another way to feel above the traditions you study.
- Whether it increases rumination, distrust, or withdrawal from real people.

### Caution

Stop if this becomes compulsive checking, increases cynicism, shame, or isolation, or replaces actual contact with people and practice. It is a thinking aid, not a substitute for community or care.

### Weakens If

It weakens if users only ever record a false verdict, if it performs no better than ordinary journaling, or if it leaves them more isolated and contemptuous rather than more able to commit to a shared ground.

## Next Research Directions

- If this model is right, then asking comparers to pre-register an upgrade condition should yield some genuine confirmed convergences whose shared role survives decomposition and gains confidence. If a large blind sample of strain analyses produces essentially zero upgrades regardless of case, the method is confirmed to be a skepticism engine and should be renamed and retired as a convergence test.
- If this model is right, then a confirm-expose-undecidable scheme should let two coders agree on which verdict a comparison earns more often than a downgrade-magnitude scale does. If verdict agreement is no better, the three-verdict scheme adds nothing.
- Build the smallest possible decision rule: before decomposition, write the surviving-role description and the upgrade bar; after decomposition, assign confirmed, false, or undecidable, plus a provenance note separating independent recurrence from historical contact.
- Test whether the upgrade verdict predicts anything outside religion: in comparative law, do strain analyses that issue confirmed-transplant verdicts predict successful legal transplants as well as failed ones, or do they only ever predict failure?
- Protocol improvement: after any comparison, ask whether the method could have returned a different verdict for this case. If the answer is no for every case, the method is not observing; it is asserting.
