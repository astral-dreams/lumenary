## Original Claim

The family of models built around this frontier shares one move that should now be challenged: the claim that continuity, custody, or support is always held somewhere, and that what a path negates predicts where it migrates. This move quietly fuses two different claims. The first is constitutive and trivially true: a transforming person is always sustained by some body, habit, teacher, text, vow, or community, so support can always be found 'relocated' after the fact. This half is inherited from extended-mind reasoning and predicts nothing, because no possible case can contradict it. The second is differential and worth testing: what a path negates predicts where support migrates and how it fails. Only the differential half is a finding, and it has purchase only where a search-and-negation sequence actually runs. In search-refusing paths, where the seeking that generates remainder pressure is itself forbidden (Huangbo), denied a before-and-after (Dogen), or replaced by direct recognition (Dzogchen), there is no remainder to locate; redescribing those traditions' teachers, vows, and communities as 'relocated continuity' is not a confirmation of the model but the visible symptom of its unfalsifiability. Lumenary should therefore narrow the whole continuity-and-remainder apparatus to search-sequence paths, separate the constitutive claim from the differential claim in every record, and refuse to count a model as confirmed unless it names an observation that would show it false in a specific case.

## Why It Might Be New

The frontier has repeatedly listed Dogen, Huangbo, and Dzogchen as anomalies, but each prior model absorbs them by saying the support simply moved to teacher, vow, or present act. The new move is to treat that absorption itself as the diagnostic failure rather than a successful extension: a model whose central concept can swallow every counterexample has stopped doing work. The specific contribution is to name the inherited source of that generosity (extended-mind constitutivity), to separate the trivially true claim from the testable one, and to restrict the testable claim to paths that actually run the negation sequence. This is a falsifiability critique of a model family, not another variable added to it, which is what distinguishes it from the recent question-permission and support-holder findings it otherwise agrees with.

## Critique

The critique may prove too much. Almost every rich interpretive framework looks unfalsifiable from outside, and the frontier brief itself asks that this work remain a comparative and practitioner-facing heuristic, not a predictive science; demanding a prohibited observation may impose a natural-science standard where it does not belong. A second strain: the differential claim might retain purchase even in search-refusing paths, because those paths show their own characteristic failures (Pure Land warnings against self-power calculation, Dzogchen warnings against reifying recognition). If those failures cluster predictably with what each path refuses, then a support-and-failure frame earns predictive status there too, and my scope-restriction to search-sequence paths is weakened. A third strain: this critique is itself close to Lumenary's own progress-gate and duplicate-production findings, so its novelty is partly recursive, and it should be scored modestly.

## Teaching Candidate

Status: under_dialogue
Tags: falsifiability, spiritual-bypass, loneliness, meaning-loss, self-negation, critique-mode

### Teaching Line

If nothing could show a comfort to be false, it cannot guide you when you most need guidance.

### Doctrine Claim

An explanation that survives every possible outcome has not explained your situation; before you lean your life on a reassurance, name what would show it untrue.

### Teaching Body

Some sayings feel deep because they can never be wrong. 'You are always held.' 'Nothing is ever truly lacking.' 'It is all unfolding as it should.' These can steady a hard hour, and that is a real use. But a claim that fits every outcome cannot tell you anything about this outcome. When you are deciding what to do, ask a smaller question: what would I expect to see if this were false? If you can name it, the saying is teaching you something. If nothing could ever count against it, treat it as comfort, not counsel, and then go check the plain facts: is there a person you could actually call, a duty you are actually neglecting, a help you are actually avoiding?

### Human Problem

Loneliness and meaning loss soothed by totalizing reassurances that can never be wrong, which feel like understanding while quietly delaying the concrete, awkward act of reaching for real support.

### For

Reflective people who calm distress with all-encompassing spiritual or psychological frames, especially the isolated and the over-managing who prefer a clean explanation to a phone call.

### Pressure Survived

Survives the search-refusing anomalies (Huangbo, Dogen, Dzogchen) and the extended-mind near-neighbor by separating what is always true from what tells you about your own case.

### Falsifying Pressure

Weakens if naming a disproof reliably increases anxiety or strips comfort that a person in acute crisis genuinely needs to stay safe, or if the reassurance was already producing concrete good conduct without any audit. In those cases comfort should not be examined.



## Practice Candidate

Status: seed
Risk level: low
Tags: falsifiability, spiritual-bypass, loneliness, low-risk, contact-first

### Practice Line

When a reassurance steadies you, name one thing that would show it false, then check whether you actually have concrete support.

### Purpose

To tell apart a comfort that is guiding you from a comfort that can never be wrong and is therefore quietly substituting for action.

### Human Problem

Loneliness, meaning loss, and the habit of resolving distress with totalizing claims that cannot fail and so cannot prompt a real next step.

### For

Stable, reflective adults who reach for sweeping spiritual or psychological reassurances when alone or under pressure, and who tend to prefer interpretation to contact.

### Not For

Not for acute crisis, fresh grief that needs comfort rather than scrutiny, OCD or scrupulosity where it would become compulsive doubt-checking, psychosis, mania, severe depression, or anyone whose reassurance is the thin thread keeping them safe right now. Not a substitute for clinical care, and not for people already supported by a trusted teacher or community handling this well.

### Time

- Duration: 6 minutes
- Frequency: Only when you notice yourself leaning hard on a sweeping reassurance, no more than twice per week.
- Minimum attempt: Try it on three different reassurances over two weeks before judging it, stopping earlier if it increases anxiety or doubt-spiraling.

### Steps

1. Write the reassuring sentence you are leaning on in plain words.
2. Ask: what would I expect to see, or not see, if this were false? Write one concrete observation.
3. If you can name one, the saying is doing real work; keep it and act on what it implies.
4. If nothing could ever count against it, mark it as comfort, not counsel, and do not build a decision on it.
5. Either way, name one concrete support you actually have access to today: a person to call, a task to finish, a request for help, or a place to go.
6. Do that one concrete thing, then stop. Do not turn the exercise into an audit of all your beliefs.

### Notice

- Whether the reassurance leads you toward a real person or duty, or only back into your own head.
- Whether naming a disproof loosens or tightens the chest.
- Whether you are using the saying to avoid an awkward but available act of contact or repair.

### Caution

Stop if this becomes compulsive doubt-checking, increases shame or panic, or strips a comfort you need to stay safe. Comfort in a crisis is not the target; substitution for action is. Seek human or clinical support when distress is high.

### Weakens If

It weakens if it increases rumination or anxious certainty-seeking, if it does no better than an ordinary 'who can I reach out to' prompt, or if people use it to dismiss reassurances that were already steadying them into good conduct.

## Next Research Directions

- If this critique is right, then asking coders to mark, for each continuity model, an observation that would falsify it in a specific case should leave many of the current records with no such observation; those should be retired or downgraded. If coders can readily supply prohibited observations, the family is healthier than claimed and the critique is weakened.
- If remainder pressure is produced by the search sequence rather than by self-negation as such, then practitioners moved from a search-and-negation method into a search-refusing method should report the felt question 'what remains / what holds this' fading rather than relocating. If the question persists unchanged across the switch, the sequence-dependence claim is weakened.
- Test the scope-restriction directly: code Huangbo, Dogen, and Dzogchen sources for whether their warnings and failures cluster predictably with what each path refuses. If they do, the differential claim has purchase even without a search sequence, and the restriction must be relaxed.
- Run a blind distinct-content test against Continuity Ecology Under Negation, Distributed Continuity Architecture, and Operational Remainder Ecology: can readers state what each predicts that the others do not? Records that cannot be distinguished should be merged.
- Protocol improvement: in every future comparative model, write the trivially-true constitutive version and the testable differential version as separate sentences before scoring, and refuse novelty credit to the constitutive half.
