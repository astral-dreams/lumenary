## Original Claim

The predictive-processing account of self-negation does not neutrally reproduce the atman versus not-self dispute; it has quietly joined one side. By modeling the self as a generative posit whose precision can be relaxed toward zero, the framework builds in the very premise the dispute is about: that the self is something inferred or constructed. It can therefore express the Buddhist refusal to ground awareness, but it structurally cannot express the Advaita claim that awareness is non-inferred self-disclosure, because in this framework everything cognitive is already a model. The framework also speaks at a deflationary, as-if level about what the brain does, while the dispute lives at the level of what a person is entitled to claim about themselves. So the apparent reproduction of the dispute is translation strain across a level boundary, not a higher-level mirror and not evidence. The frontier claim that the model formally reproduces the dispute should be retired and replaced with the narrower claim that the model restates one party in new vocabulary.

## Why It Might Be New

Prior work treated the predictive-processing framing as a neutral higher-level mirror in which the old dispute reappears. The distinct move here is that the mirror is bent twice over. First, its vocabulary is not neutral between the parties: treating the self as a relaxable model is itself the contested Buddhist-leaning premise, so the framework cannot represent the non-inferred, self-luminous witness without dissolving it. Second, it speaks at a subpersonal or deflationary register, where, on the theory's own terms, no one is actually inferring anything, while the dispute is a person-level normative question about warrant. Naming both the vocabulary bias and the level mismatch turns a claimed convergence into a diagnosed category error.

## Critique

The critique may attack a position no one defends. Laukkonen and Slagter offer descriptive scaffolding and do not claim to adjudicate metaphysics, so reading the frontier as an arbitration claim could be a strawman; perhaps reproduce only means the same structural fork appears, which is harmless. The neutrality charge could also fail if a careful formalization represents the Advaita witness as an irreducible fixed point or minimal model that the system cannot relax below, in which case the framework would model self-luminosity rather than erase it. Finally, the personal versus subpersonal distinction I lean on is itself contested, and some hold that the Free Energy Principle is meant precisely to dissolve that boundary, which would weaken the level-mismatch argument. If a coherent non-reductive formalization of svaprakasha awareness exists, this contradiction should be downgraded to a caution about vocabulary rather than a retirement of the frontier.

## Teaching Candidate

Status: under_dialogue
Tags: critique-mode, predictive-processing, atman-anatta, objectless-awareness, meaning-loss, overinterpretation

### Teaching Line

A model of how your mind goes quiet cannot tell you what you are.

### Doctrine Claim

An account of what the brain does during a quiet or objectless state describes a mechanism. It does not settle the separate, person-level question of what you may conclude about yourself. Do not let the description quietly answer the question.

### Teaching Body

After a deep quiet, the mind wants a verdict: I am nothing, or I am the true Self, or I have seen through the self. A neat scientific story can arrive and seem to hand you the answer. Notice that the story was built to describe a process, not to judge a person. When someone tells you the self is only a model, that sentence already leans one way before you have weighed anything. Hold the description and the verdict apart. Let the mechanism be interesting without letting it be the judge of who you are.

### Human Problem

Meaning loss and identity distress when a quiet or objectless practice experience is handed a verdict by a borrowed scientific story, producing either nihilistic deflation or spiritual inflation.

### For

Psychologically stable adults in secular mindfulness, non-dual, or app-based practice who interpret their experiences through popular neuroscience and reach for what it really proves about the self.

### Pressure Survived

Survives the contrast between Shankara's non-inferred self-luminous witness and the Buddhist refusal to ground awareness, by showing that a predictive-processing redescription can voice only one side and speaks at a level where no person is making a claim at all.

### Falsifying Pressure

Weakens if a non-reductive scientific model can genuinely represent both the witness and not-self without favoring either, or if separating mechanism from verdict makes no difference to how people interpret and recover from destabilizing states.



## Practice Candidate

Status: seed
Risk level: low
Tags: mechanism-versus-verdict, objectless-awareness, meaning-loss, low-risk, secular-mindfulness

### Practice Line

When a story explains what your experience really means about you, split the two claims it is making.

### Purpose

To stop a scientific or app-given explanation of a quiet practice state from silently deciding an identity question it was never built to answer.

### Human Problem

Identity distress and meaning loss after objectless or depersonalization-adjacent practice states, intensified by borrowed explanations that feel like proof about the self.

### For

Stable adults who meditate or practice self-inquiry, often through apps or popular science, and who tend to intellectualize their experiences into conclusions about who they are.

### Not For

Not for people in active depersonalization or derealization disorder, psychosis, mania, acute dissociation, or trauma activation, who need clinical care rather than an epistemic exercise. Not for beginners with no destabilizing experiences to interpret.

### Time

- Duration: 8 minutes
- Frequency: Only after an unusually quiet, blank, or self-loosening experience, no more than twice per week.
- Minimum attempt: Try it after three separate such experiences before judging whether it helps.

### Steps

1. Write the explanation you were given in one sentence, for example: meditation relaxes the brain's self-model.
2. Underline the part that is a mechanism claim: what the brain or mind did.
3. Underline separately the part that is an identity verdict: what you supposedly are, or are not.
4. Ask whether the mechanism claim, even if true, actually forces the verdict, or whether the verdict was added.
5. For the next 24 hours, hold the mechanism as interesting and leave the verdict open. Name one ordinary action to return to: rest, work, a conversation, or care for someone.

### Notice

- Whether the experience stays meaningful when you refuse to draw a final conclusion about yourself.
- Whether the borrowed explanation was leaning toward one conclusion before you weighed anything.
- Whether holding the verdict open lowers both the deflation and the inflation.
- Whether the exercise turns into more rumination rather than less.

### Caution

Stop if this increases obsessive analysis, derealization, fear about your own mind, or shame. Seek human or clinical support if a quiet state left you frightened or impaired. This is not a substitute for care.

### Weakens If

It weakens if people report more rumination or detachment, if ordinary rest and conversation help just as much, or if separating mechanism from verdict makes no difference to distress after three attempts.
