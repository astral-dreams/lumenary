## Original Claim

A test that protects doctrine can harm a lonely person if it is handed to them as medicine. When two traditions seem to agree, the full strain sheet should ask what each path must bend, drop, or add before the agreement counts. But when a person uses a borrowed sentence for hope, belonging, or direction, the first test should be contact and conduct: does the sentence move them toward a real person, duty, repair, help, or community before it becomes an identity claim. What now holds is a split rule: doctrine should be provenance-first; wounded use should be contact-first. A sentence is not suspect because it traveled. It becomes suspect when it replaces correction, action, grief work, help-seeking, or belonging with private certainty or public display.

## Why It Might Be New

Smith, Freiberger, and Panikkar already make comparison disciplined, invented, and role-sensitive. Lindbeck and Asad are also close because they tie doctrine to communal grammar and traditioned practice. The exact difference is the split use rule: the same comparison sheet should be strict for doctrine-building and restrained for lonely seekers. Prior local records already named provenance, receiver wound, and borrowing; this record lowers novelty and adds one sharper claim, that a valid research gate should not become a solo belonging practice unless it demonstrably increases contact and conduct.

## Critique

The split may excuse shallow appropriation by letting seekers delay provenance too long. It may also overstate loneliness as the main human wound when some users need accuracy, recovery support, or ordinary rest more than contact. The strongest anomaly is an authorized hybrid lineage or comparative theology setting where borrowed language is already corrected by living teachers and community, so provenance and contact are not separate stages. Another anomaly is direct-recognition practice: some traditions will object that the truth of recognition is not validated by contact first. If teachers and practitioners show that ordinary guidance handles these cases better than this split, or if contact-first practice increases shame, unsafe disclosure, or confusion, the model should narrow or retire.

## Teaching Candidate

Status: under_dialogue
Tags: translation-strain, critique-mode, borrowed-language, loneliness, meaning-loss, digital-comparison, doctrine-gate

### Teaching Line

Use a test to guard truth, not to replace contact.

### Doctrine Claim

Cross-tradition agreement should pass a strict carrier test before it supports doctrine, but a lonely seeker should first test a borrowed sentence by contact, conduct, and correction.

### Teaching Body

A careful test can protect truth. It can also become a room you never leave.

When you compare traditions, be strict. Ask what each path teaches, who may correct it, how it is practiced, and what must be changed for the match to work.

When a sentence is helping you endure, begin closer to life. Ask what real act it asks today. Bring it to a person, a duty, a repair, a request for help, or a community that can answer back. Do not let a borrowed sentence become your identity before it has made you more honest and more available.

### Human Problem

Loneliness, meaning loss, digital comparison, and feeling out of place intensified by borrowed spiritual language that becomes identity display or private certainty.

### For

Stable spiritually eclectic adults and older teens who gather spiritual language online or across traditions and are tempted to turn a moving sentence into identity, proof, or public authority.

### Pressure Survived

Survived prior-art pressure from comparative method, homeomorphic equivalence, doctrine-as-grammar, provenance checks, receiver-wound records, and modern social-connection pressure by narrowing itself to a split use rule.

### Falsifying Pressure

Weakens if the split adds nothing beyond ordinary teacher guidance, if contact-first practice does not reduce loneliness or identity display, or if it delays needed provenance in ways that increase confident misuse.



## Practice Candidate

Status: seed
Risk level: low
Tags: contact-first, borrowed-language, loneliness, meaning-loss, digital-comparison, low-risk

### Practice Line

Before making a borrowed sentence your identity, bring it to one real act or person.

### Purpose

To test whether a borrowed spiritual sentence is moving into conduct and connection, or becoming private certainty, avoidance, or public identity display.

### Human Problem

Loneliness, meaning loss, digital comparison, and feeling out of place soothed by abstract agreement instead of real belonging or repair.

### For

Stable adults and older teens with basic support who read across traditions, collect spiritual claims online, or feel pulled to post or organize life around a powerful borrowed sentence.

### Not For

Not for acute crisis, psychosis, mania, severe depression, active addiction withdrawal, dissociation, unsafe relationships, OCD or scrupulosity loops, coercive communities, or people under trusted guidance who should ask their teacher or clinician.

### Time

- Duration: 15 minutes plus a 24 hour delay before posting or making a major identity claim.
- Frequency: Only when a borrowed sentence feels important enough to post, repeat as yours, or use for a decision. No more than twice per week.
- Minimum attempt: Try it on three separate sentences across two weeks, stopping earlier if shame, checking, panic, or isolation increases.

### Steps

1. Write the exact sentence you want to claim, post, or build on.
2. Name its use: doctrine, public authority, private hope, decision guidance, or comfort.
3. If it is doctrine or public authority, pause and complete the full strain sheet: claim unit, role, ontology, agency, practice aim, authority, verification, provenance, what is bent, what is dropped, and what is added.
4. If it is hope or direction, name one concrete act it asks in the next 24 hours toward a person, duty, repair, help, or community.
5. Do that act before posting the sentence or making it part of your identity.
6. If the sentence would shape a major decision, bring it to one trustworthy person or community that can answer back.
7. After 24 hours, write whether the sentence made you more connected, honest, careful, and correctable, or more isolated, performative, certain, or avoidant.

### Notice

- Whether the sentence produces contact or only a self-image.
- Whether the sentence makes you more open to correction or more defended against it.
- Whether delaying public display reduces urgency or increases anxious checking.
- Whether the real act reveals that the sentence was asking for repair, rest, help, grief, or ordinary responsibility.

### Caution

Stop if the practice increases shame, obsessive checking, panic, dissociation, contempt, unsafe disclosure, withdrawal, or dependency on an unsafe person. Use qualified human support for clinical, legal, relational, or safety crises.

### Weakens If

Weakens if ordinary journaling, teacher guidance, or a simple wait-before-posting rule works as well, or if the practice lowers posting but leaves loneliness, conduct, and real contact unchanged.
