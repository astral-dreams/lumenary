## Original Claim

Neither remainder pressure alone nor credit distribution alone is sufficient to bridge the full range of contemplative approaches to self and identity. The two variables operate at different levels and carry partially independent information. Credit distribution is the upstream architectural variable: a tradition's assignment of agency (to practitioner, to transcendent source, split between action and outcome, to the activity itself, or to primordial ground) determines whether a negation or self-inquiry procedure is likely, who is credited with performing it, and whether the question of a final subject becomes salient. Remainder pressure is the downstream phenomenological variable: when a practice architecture does produce agent-driven negation or ownership inquiry, a felt or conceptual demand to posit a final subject may arise. That pressure does not decide what the final subject is; the tradition's inference policy determines whether the pressure is ontologized as an unobjectifiable witness, recursively analyzed as another dependently arisen construction, bracketed as a phenomenological report, modeled as a cognitive self-representation artifact, or dissolved by refusing the search procedure that sustains it. The synthesis predicts that the two stages are partially coupled: credit distribution shapes whether and how pressure arises, but the pressure, once present, carries information about the practice moment that credit distribution alone does not specify. This partial coupling is an empirical claim requiring testing via comparative practitioner reports coded for both credit orientation and subject-shaped residue, using open-ended micro-phenomenological methods to avoid demand characteristics.

## Why It Might Be New

The Proponent's original remainder-pressure bridge and the Challenger's credit-distribution typology each offered single-variable frameworks. The dialogue produced a two-level architecture in which they operate at different stages with partial coupling. This structure was not present in either source idea. The Proponent did not treat credit distribution as an upstream condition; the Challenger did not treat remainder pressure as a downstream diagnostic that carries independent information once generated. The integration also produced a refined taxonomy of what the Proponent originally called 'management policies,' distinguishing at least six responses to remainder pressure (ontologize, recursively analyze, bracket, cognitively model, refuse the search, bypass via direct introduction) where the original had four. The partial-coupling claim is itself novel: it predicts that credit distribution shapes but does not fully determine remainder phenomenology, which would mean both variables are needed for a complete comparative framework and neither is reducible to the other.

## Critique

Three structural risks. First, the two-stage model may be a transitional formulation rather than a stable resting place. If empirical work shows that credit distribution fully determines whether and what kind of remainder pressure arises, the downstream variable is epiphenomenal and the model collapses into credit distribution alone. If remainder pressure arises independently of practice architecture, the upstream variable is unnecessary for the remainder-pressure bridge, and the original single-variable model was correct. The synthesis depends on a middle ground (partial coupling) that has not been demonstrated. Second, the procedural-refusal category groups structurally different operations: Chan search-refusal, Dzogchen bypass via pointing-out, Advaita pre-completion, and Pure Land agency relocation. If these are grouped under one heading, the synthesis risks the same kind of flattening it was designed to correct. Third, the entire framework may over-psychologize traditions whose self-understanding is ontological, cosmological, or soteriological rather than phenomenological. Treating what happens during neti neti or aggregate analysis as 'pressure' imports a psychological register that may not match the tradition's own framing. The synthesis should specify when 'pressure' is a phenomenological report, when it is a hermeneutical construct, and when it is a cognitive-science analogy, because these uses have different epistemic standing.

## Next Research Directions

- Design and execute a partial-coupling test: classify at least twelve contemplative practices by credit distribution type and negation intensity, then collect open-ended micro-phenomenological reports (Petitmengin method) from practitioners of each, coded blind for subject-shaped residue. If remainder-pressure reports cluster by credit type but show significant within-type variance, partial coupling is supported. If they cluster perfectly, credit distribution subsumes remainder pressure. If they show no pattern by credit type, remainder pressure is independent.
- Differentiate the procedural-refusal category into at least four sub-types (search refusal, bypass via direct introduction, pre-completion declaration, and agency relocation) and test whether each sub-type correlates with a distinct credit-distribution pattern. If Huang Po's search refusal maps to activity-centered credit while Dzogchen bypass maps to ground-centered credit, the sub-types are not arbitrary but track real architectural differences.
- Code three practice manuals from each credit-distribution type for the predicted failure mode (spiritual pride for practitioner-centered, passivity for source-centered, strategic detachment for split, formalism for activity-centered, premature realization for ground-centered). If primary safeguard profiles cluster by credit type, the credit-distribution model gains predictive validation. If the primary safeguard also correlates with the presence or absence of remainder-pressure language, the two-stage model gains support.
- Test the stage-shifting hypothesis: code Theravada, Sufi, and Advaita practice sequences for both credit distribution and remainder-pressure language at each stage. If credit distribution shifts predictably across stages (e.g., practitioner-centered early, ground-centered late) and remainder pressure tracks those shifts, revise both models from tradition-level to stage-level variables.
- Investigate whether the partial-coupling claim can be operationalized in consciousness-research terms. If self-modeling theories (Metzinger, Damasio) predict that metacognitive monitoring generates a subject-model artifact regardless of intentional framework, this would support remainder pressure as partially independent of credit distribution. If self-modeling theories predict that the character of the subject-model depends on the agent's intentional stance, partial coupling is supported from the empirical side.
