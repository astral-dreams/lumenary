## Original Claim

After a state that cannot be inspected while it happens (contemplative cessation, deep sleep, grief numbness, burnout blankness, addiction aftermath, anesthesia, or dissociation), the first report is not neutral memory. How that report distributes evidential weight is shaped by two layers that operate in sequence but influence each other. The upstream layer is the method's self-relationship at completion: a self-confirming method (Advaita discriminative inquiry, Mandukya analysis) produces subject-weighted reports because its logic requires a witness that persists; a self-canceling method (Madhyamaka dialectic, Zen koan exhaustion, Eckhartian Gelassenheit) produces absence-and-transition-weighted reports because its logic requires that no owner survive the gap; a self-dissolving method (Zhuangzi's zuowang, Dzogchen rang grol, Sufi fana) produces aftereffect-or-relation-weighted reports because the practitioner has matured past the frame in which subject-talk applies. The downstream layer is the grammar of the report itself: the specific clause that a person or tradition treats as evidence (subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, relation, teacher recognition, embodied fruit). This grammar is usually shaped by the upstream method-type, but it is not inert. Grammar is trained, taught, corrected by community, and it trains back: the admissibility rule a person learns to apply after silence shapes what they trust, what they remember as salient, and how they act on return. The grammar thus has reciprocal force on the practitioner's subsequent self-interpretation and conduct. The practical rule is restrained: after a gap, do not let the first clause of the report own the whole event. Ask which clause is being weighted, and ask which method, community, wound, or fear has trained that weighting. For ordinary life, this means that people emerging from grief, numbness, burnout, or dreamless sleep can resist premature identity verdicts ('I felt nothing, so I am broken'; 'I returned, so the old self is secure') by noticing that their report has a grammar, and that the grammar is not the whole truth of the gap. Critical constraint: the ordinary-life application requires stage-awareness (Herman's safety, remembrance, reconnection framework); applying grammar coding to someone in acute crisis or early protective numbness could misread a necessary phase as a problem to analyze. The model's tested audience is scholars, spiritual directors, and trained therapists; its reach to stable ordinary people is a hypothesis that requires empirical testing against simpler non-taxonomic reflection.

## Why It Might Be New

Neither source idea contained the two-layer architecture. The Gap Report model treated grammar as a primary comparison variable; the Method's Reckoning treated method-type as the primary variable and did not develop the grammar layer. The synthesis positions them in an upstream-downstream relationship with a specific reciprocal-force hypothesis: grammar is usually predicted by method-type, but grammar also trains back by shaping what the practitioner trusts on return. The ordinary-life extension (grief, burnout, numbness, addiction) with stage-awareness from Herman's trauma recovery model is also new; neither source idea made this clinical connection. The three-arm research design (dual-trained practitioner interviews, five-clause protocol for ordinary participants, and simpler-prompt control) is a novel experimental contribution that tests both the contemplative and ordinary-life claims.

## Critique

Four vulnerabilities require attention. First, the reciprocal-force hypothesis (grammar trains back, shaping what is trusted on return) is plausible but untested. If the proposed five-clause protocol shows no effect on later self-interpretation or conduct, the grammar layer loses its claim to active force and becomes a passive trace. Second, the ordinary-life extension is the most ambitious and least supported part of the synthesis. The analogy between contemplative cessation and grief numbness may flatten crucial differences: neurobiological, relational, contextual. If the analogy fails under empirical pressure, the model's audience shrinks to the specialist cohort. Third, Herman's stage model warns that grammar coding without clinical training could misread protective numbness as a problem to analyze; the safety constraint is honest but it also limits the model's practical reach to clinically trained intermediaries, not directly to 'stable ordinary people.' Fourth, the three-type method classification (confirming, canceling, dissolving) may not be stable: many traditions mix types across training stages (Advaita's 'thorn removes thorn,' the Buddhist raft parable, Rinzai's staged curriculum), which could force the model toward a stage-dependent typology rather than a tradition-level one. If method-type is stage-dependent, the upstream layer becomes significantly more complex to operationalize.

## Next Research Directions

- Test the reciprocal-force hypothesis: give dual-trained practitioners (e.g., Advaita self-inquiry plus Zen koan practice) a single objectless experience and ask them to report under each method-frame. If report grammar shifts with method-frame, the upstream layer is confirmed. Then ask whether the shifted grammar changes what the practitioner trusts about the experience afterward; if it does, reciprocal force is demonstrated.
- Run a three-arm study with non-advanced participants emerging from grief numbness, burnout blankness, or ordinary dreamless sleep: (a) five-clause grammar-coding reflection protocol, (b) simpler non-taxonomic prompt ('What did the gap leave behind, and what did it take away?'), (c) unstructured free narration. Measure premature identity verdicts, rumination, and shame at one week and one month. If the grammar protocol outperforms the simpler prompt, the instrument has practical force. If the simpler prompt matches it, the specialist vocabulary is dispensable for ordinary use.
- Map method-type by training stage across six traditions (Advaita, Theravada, Rinzai Zen, Dzogchen, Sufism, Eckhartian mysticism) to determine whether the three-type classification is tradition-level or stage-level. If most traditions mix all three types at different stages, the upstream layer needs a stage-dependent formulation.
- Collaborate with a clinical psychologist to pressure-test the boundary between 'stable ordinary grief' and destabilizing dissociation. Develop inclusion and exclusion criteria for the ordinary-life protocol that are operationally safe, not just disclaimed.
- Close-read the primary texts (Mandukya 7, Heart Sutra negation series, MN 44, Brihadaranyaka 4.3.23, Zhuangzi ch. 6) in original languages to verify that the limit-move and report grammar are visible in the source-language grammar, not only in translation choices.
