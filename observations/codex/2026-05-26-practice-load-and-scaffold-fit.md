## Original Claim

Self-transcending paths should be assessed by the fit among four variables: the destabilizing load of the practice, the scaffold location, the quality of containment, and the practitioner's current self-coherence. Scaffold location can be intrapersonal, relational, institutional, divine, environmental, or mixed. The minimum-self tension is not a universal explanation of all paths, but a high-risk special case: practices that depend heavily on the practitioner's own attention, memory, agency, and reflexivity while also weakening ordinary self-reference. The practical prediction is that adverse outcomes should rise when high destabilizing intensity and high intrapersonal load meet low external containment, especially for practitioners whose self-structure is already fragile through trauma, dissociation, chronic shame, unstable attachment, achievement-contingent identity, or identity exhaustion under digital comparison.

## Why It Might Be New

The candidate synthesis is not just the proponent's minimum-self model and not just the challenger's translation-strain method. It adds a practical risk model: apparent cross-tradition convergence around self-dissolution should be translated into scaffold, load, containment, and cohort variables. Its possible novelty is the combination of comparative contemplative architecture with adverse-experience prediction and practice design. It treats metaphysical disagreement as useful pressure for identifying where support is located and what kind of harm a practice can produce.

## Critique

The model may still over-systematize living traditions and teacher discernment. It risks treating diverse wounds, trauma, dissociation, anxiety, shame, grief, burnout, loneliness, and meaning loss, as one fragile-self cohort when they may require different supports. External scaffolds are not automatically safer, since teachers, communities, vows, and surrender frameworks can produce dependency, coercion, shame, or delayed help-seeking. The residual minimum-self language may also collapse into a truism if it means only that a person must be able to hear or respond. The candidate needs empirical pressure from adverse-event data, teacher screening practices, practitioner reports, and failed cases before it can become a teaching or practice protocol.

## Next Research Directions

- Create a coding rubric with four axes: intrapersonal load, destabilizing intensity, external containment, and baseline self-coherence.
- Test the rubric against manuals, retreat rules, teacher screening protocols, and adverse-event reports across vipassana, koan practice, self-inquiry, Pure Land, Sufi teacher-student paths, Christian contemplative prayer, and monastic sangha structures.
- Separate modern cohorts instead of merging them: trauma and dissociation, chronic shame, achievement-contingent identity, grief, burnout, loneliness, and meaning loss may require different scaffold designs.
- Audit whether scaffold-poverty predicts adverse outcomes after controlling for retreat duration, intensity, sleep disruption, social isolation, prior vulnerability, teacher competence, and substance use.
- Develop a low-risk practice-design question rather than a path recommendation: what support must be built before this practice weakens ordinary self-reference?
