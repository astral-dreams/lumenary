## Original Claim

Some paths do not move support away from the self after the self is questioned. They expose that the self was never the first support. A teacher, vow, community, ritual form, present act, or given trust may already be carrying the beginning before the practitioner can own a beginning. The live question is earlier than, what remains after me: what was holding me before I tried to be the holder? This matters for lonely and achievement-trained people who turn every help into proof of self-sufficiency. The narrowed doctrine should teach receiving help without converting it into private achievement.

## Why It Might Be New

Close prior work already shows that religious thought and practice are formed through bodies, tools, texts, institutions, and communal rules. The sharper difference here is the timing test: in self-emptying paths, ask whether support is built by practice, revealed as already present, received from beyond private effort, or enacted as the present act itself. That timing distinction predicts a different modern failure: turning received support into proof that one has finally mastered surrender.

## Critique

The model may still smuggle a support story into traditions that reject support-seeking. Huangbo's search-refusal, Dogen's practice-realization, and early Buddhist not-self may not be variants of prior support; they may be instructions to stop reifying practice time, ownership, and attainment. Shinran's Other Power can also become a theological claim that should not be translated into a general human lesson. The modern practice may harm people whose real need is agency, safety, boundaries, or clinical support. This should remain under dialogue until lineage readers and practitioner reports confirm that already-carried support changes diagnosis rather than redescribes ordinary belonging.

## Teaching Candidate

Status: under_dialogue
Tags: critique-mode, self-negation, support, loneliness, achievement-pressure, doctrine-seed

### Teaching Line

Before asking how to carry yourself, ask what is already helping you stand.

### Doctrine Claim

A person is not made free by pretending to be self-made. Some paths work by revealing supports already present before private effort can claim them.

### Teaching Body

Some people try to make even help into proof of strength. They take a teacher, practice, community, or good advice, then turn it into another private achievement.

This teaching asks for a smaller honesty. Notice what is carrying part of your life before you take credit for standing. Receive it without performance, then answer it with one act that helps another person or keeps a real promise.

### Human Problem

Loneliness, feeling unneeded, and achievement-contingent self-worth that turns help into private proof.

### For

Achievement-driven adults, isolated practitioners, founders, students, caregivers, and professionals who can receive support but habitually translate support into self-sufficiency.

### Pressure Survived

Critique mode pressure from Dogen practice-realization, Shinran Other Power, Huangbo search-refusal, SN 22.59 not-self, extended religious mind prior art, discursive-tradition prior art, and modern loneliness and perfectionism sources.

### Falsifying Pressure

Weaken if practitioners in no-gap, not-self, or Other Power traditions reject the prior-support reading as distortion; or if pilots show that support-noticing increases passivity, dependence, shame, or self-audit.



## Practice Candidate

Status: seed
Risk level: low
Tags: support-inventory, achievement-pressure, loneliness, low-risk, practice-seed

### Practice Line

Before a hard task, name what is already helping you stand.

### Purpose

Test whether noticing prior support reduces self-made pressure and increases concrete reciprocity.

### Human Problem

Loneliness, feeling unneeded, and achievement-contingent self-worth.

### For

People who over-own success, hide dependence, or turn help into proof that they are finally self-sufficient.

### Not For

Not for acute crisis, abusive or controlling relationships, severe depression, addiction withdrawal, psychosis, dissociation, people whose main need is stronger boundaries, or compulsive self-monitors who turn every exercise into a worth test.

### Time

- Duration: 7 minutes before one demanding task.
- Frequency: Three times per week for two weeks.
- Minimum attempt: Four attempts, then review whether it helped or worsened pressure.

### Steps

1. Choose one demanding task or decision.
2. Write three supports already present: a person, place, routine, tool, promise, teaching, or body condition.
3. After each support, write: I did not create all of this alone.
4. Choose one small answer to one support: thank someone, maintain a tool, keep a promise, ask for help, or make the next step easier for another person.
5. Do the task for ten minutes without using the list to judge your worth.
6. At the end, record whether pressure to prove yourself rose, fell, or stayed the same.

### Notice

- Whether your body tightens or softens when help is named.
- Whether you feel gratitude, debt, shame, relief, or resistance.
- Whether you try to turn receiving help into another achievement.
- Whether one concrete act of reciprocity becomes easier.

### Caution

Stop if the exercise increases shame, rumination, passivity, dependence on unsafe people, or pressure to perform gratitude. This is not a substitute for therapy, medical care, or crisis support.

### Weakens If

After four attempts, self-monitoring, shame, passivity, or worth-pressure rises; or the exercise produces no concrete act of reciprocity or responsible action.

## Next Research Directions

- If this model is right, then traditions that deny self-generated entry should show visible early supports before explicit doctrinal explanation, such as teacher, vow, liturgy, community, repeated form, or given trust. If manuals instead rely mostly on private effort and only later add outside support, the model is weakened.
- Close-read Dogen's Bendowa and Uji, Shinran's Kyogyoshinsho and Tannisho, Huangbo's Transmission of Mind, and SN 22.59 for timing verbs: begin, receive, practice, realize, seek, entrust, abandon, enact.
- Ask practitioners and teachers: when students fail, is the failure usually isolation, striving, passivity, pride in surrender, loss of ordinary responsibility, or loss of communal support?
- Protocol improvement: after using de-identification to remove false ownership, run a support inventory before naming any remainder; otherwise inquiry can mistake not mine for unsupported.
