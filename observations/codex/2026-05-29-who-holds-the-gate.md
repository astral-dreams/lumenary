## Original Claim

A practice-routing model must decide three things before recommending a return test: whether the practice permits result language, whether the practitioner is presently bypassing or self-scoring, and who should hold the diagnostic gate. Self-administered return attention may help the bypasser who uses insight to avoid conduct. For the compulsive scorer, the gate should be externally held by a teacher, peer, clinician, fixed rule, or replaced by resultless practice. For oscillators, the assignment must be state-level and revisable, not a stable identity label.

## Why It Might Be New

The source ideas contained the return test and the instrumental versus autotelic scope gate, but neither original source idea made gate custody the decisive safety variable. The dialogue added a new operational distinction: the same diagnostic can be medicine or harm depending on who holds it. This may be a publishable Lumenary candidate only after originality audit, since therapy, teacher guidance, clinical triage, and spiritual direction may already contain close prior versions.

## Source Basis

- Proponent revision: the return test survives only as a conditional diagnostic for practices that produce a result and for practitioners who use that result as identity, avoidance, or self-defense.
- Challenger revision pressure: non-instrumental traditions such as Dogen practice-realization and Shinran no-calculation require a scope gate before result custody language is applied.
- Final challenger turn: the remaining safety variable is who operates the gate, since a compulsive self-measurer may turn even the gate into self-measurement.
- Modern human-condition pressure: achievement-contingent self-worth, perfectionism, and burnout make self-monitoring risk central rather than peripheral.
- Empirical-adjacent safety constraint: Mor and Winquist on self-focused attention and negative affect pressures any repeated introspective check for ruminative or perfectionistic users.

- Parent ideas: The Test Is How You Return; Not Every Practice Is a Tool

## Critique

The synthesis may overstate the availability and reliability of external gate holders. It could also create authority dependence, or move the scoring problem from self-evaluation to approval-seeking. The bypasser, scorer, and oscillator categories may be too coarse unless treated as momentary states. The empirical support is adjacent rather than direct, and the model needs pilot evidence before becoming a public practice recommendation.
