## Original Claim

A spiritual path does not merely require more or less capacity from a practitioner. It requires capacities with direction. Receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration recur across traditions as functional roles, but each role must be described as a vector with magnitude, direction, locus, stage, safeguards, and failure mode. A path can lower or relocate a load only through a directionally compatible capacity, or through an explicit reorientation practice that changes the grain of attention, trust, memory, agency, or integration. The next explanatory layer is soteriological: the direction of each capacity may be generated by the tradition's account of what the self is, what liberation is, and what remains after transformation.

## Why It Might Be New

Neither source idea alone fully held this combined structure. The proponent supplied the five-role ledger and conservation insight. The challenger supplied directional grain and cross-tradition resistance. The dialogue produced a revised synthesis that treats each capacity as vector-valued while also adding a new caution: direction may be derivative of whole-path soteriology rather than an independent cell-level property. That makes the candidate more precise and more testable than either scalar capacity scoring or a general grain thesis.

## Critique

The synthesis may become too elaborate to compress the phenomena it studies. If every tradition requires bespoke direction, stage, safeguard, and failure language, the ledger may become a catalog rather than a model. The conservation rule is also weakened unless reorientation cost can be operationalized. Pure Land and Dzogchen remain pressure cases because entrusting and recognition may be relational, event-like, or field-dependent rather than practitioner-owned capacities. The model should not claim originality or promotion until an originality audit tests its proximity to constructivist mysticism, contemplative-development theory, Buddhist attention studies, and comparative soteriology.

## Next Research Directions

- Build a vector ledger rubric where each cell records capacity, magnitude, direction, locus, stage, safeguard, failure mode, and evidence.
- Operationalize reorientation cost using practitioner reports, teacher instructions, switching difficulty, relapse into prior attentional habits, and needed bridging practices.
- Test whether direction is cell-level or soteriology-level by comparing traditions with similar liberation accounts but different practice architectures.
- Audit close prior art in Katz, Ferrer, Sharf, Engler, Ganeri, Siderits and Katsura, contemplative adverse-effects research, and comparative theology of grace and effort.
- Code adverse-practice reports for grain mismatch versus missing prerequisite to see whether direction predicts the kind of failure better than magnitude alone.
