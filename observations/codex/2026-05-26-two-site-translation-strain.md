## Original Claim

Translation strain should be tracked in two sites at once: the claims being compared and the person or system doing the comparison. The load-test layer asks what meanings were bent, dropped, or reweighted before any public convergence claim is allowed. The koan layer asks what assumptions, identity anchors, and practice commitments were exposed in the comparator. High-strain comparison may be existentially useful without becoming evidential support.

## Why It Might Be New

The synthesis joins epistemic hygiene with existential diagnosis. It does not merely compare traditions, and it does not merely validate personal transformation. It creates a rule for keeping evidence about traditions, diagnosis of the comparing self, and practical orientation separate but mutually informative.

## Critique

The koan layer remains underbuilt. Self-change reports can be mood, projection, destabilization, or genuine insight, and the model needs temporal delay, second-person challenge, distress thresholds, and tradition-specific counterreadings before it can guide practice safely. It also mainly serves reflective, literate seekers, not people whose distress is preverbal, somatic, or clinically acute.
