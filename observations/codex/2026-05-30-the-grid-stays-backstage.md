# The Grid Stays Backstage

## Original Claim

For achievement-driven people, the safest teaching is not a recurring self-audit about practice. The diagnostic grid belongs mostly to teachers, designers, clinicians, communities, or one-time setup. The user-facing form should be a brief transition into observable conduct: name the tool lightly, stop making it proof of worth, and do one ordinary act that shows re-entry. After-use reflection is appropriate only when the path is staged, the person is not trapped in compulsive checking, and the check does not increase rumination, shame, or tool-performance anxiety.

## Why It Might Be New

Neither source idea fully contained this delivery distinction. The proponent supplied the after-use grid and modern residue problem. The challenger supplied the path precondition and stop-audit warning. The dialogue added a third layer: administrability. It distinguishes backstage diagnostic structure from frontstage user practice, and proposes that the same insight may be healthy as a teacher's lens but harmful as a self-administered worksheet for the target cohort.

## Critique

This synthesis may be mostly a practical safety refinement rather than a new doctrine. It still needs originality audit against clinical work on decentering, behavioral activation, scrupulosity, spiritual bypassing, pastoral direction, Buddhist teacher-student safeguards, and self-monitoring research. It may also become too cautious or dependent on external support, limiting usefulness for people without teachers or clinicians. The behavior-only variant must be tested for avoidance, since doing an ordinary act can become a way to evade genuine inquiry.

## Next Research Directions

- Run the proposed stratified pilot with four arms: After-Use Check, behavior-only re-entry, maxim-plus-act with no self-rating, and usual practice.
- Measure whether disposition self-screening alone raises rumination, shame, tool-performance anxiety, or reassurance-seeking among over-auditors.
- Ask Soto, Shinran, Zen, Advaita, and clinical mindfulness teachers whether keep-release-embody is a faithful translation, useful pastoral adaptation, or doctrinal distortion.
- Audit prior art in MBCT, ACT, behavioral activation, scrupulosity treatment, spiritual direction, and Buddhist pastoral guidance for the backstage diagnostic versus frontstage conduct distinction.
- Develop a low-risk user-facing practice that contains no recurring self-score and weakens immediately if it increases avoidance or checking.
