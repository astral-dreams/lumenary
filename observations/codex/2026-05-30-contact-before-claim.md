# Contact Before Claim

## Original Claim

A borrowed spiritual sentence should be tested differently according to its use. If it is being used as doctrine, evidence, public authority, or cross-tradition convergence, it needs provenance, carrier conditions, correction, and regeneration. If it is being used by a lonely seeker as hope or orientation, its first test should be contact and conduct: say it, live it, or bring it to a real person, duty, community, or repair before making it an identity claim. Borrowing becomes dangerous when it replaces action, contact, correction, grief work, or help-seeking, not simply because it is borrowed.

## Why It Might Be New

The components are close to existing comparative method, seeker spirituality, practice-realization, anti-rumination psychology, and social connection research. The potentially new move is the operational split by user and wound: doctrine receives provenance-first scrutiny, while the seeker receives contact-first fruit testing. Novelty is moderate and unproven until originality audit checks pastoral discernment traditions, comparative theology, bricolage spirituality, and therapeutic anti-checking protocols.

## Critique

The synthesis may still overgeneralize several wounds under one practice. Contact can be unsafe in abusive, coercive, or unstable relationships, so the practice needs a strong non-fit and safety protocol. Delaying provenance may protect lonely seekers, but it could also permit shallow appropriation or confident misuse if public authority is claimed too soon. The original challenger idea on time remains mostly unused, so this is not yet a true synthesis of both source ideas. The candidate should weaken if contact-first practice lowers posting but does not reduce loneliness, increases shame or checking, or proves indistinguishable from ordinary journaling plus social support.

## Next Research Directions

- Run an originality audit against comparative method, pastoral discernment, seeker spirituality, Dogen practice-realization, therapeutic anti-checking models, and social connection interventions.
- Revise the practice into separate versions: a doctrine-scoring regeneration sheet, a contact-first seeker practice, and a non-fit safety note for people seeking ordinary comfort or in unsafe relationships.
- Test four arms with the named cohort: full provenance gate, solo carry-first, contact-first carry-first, and ordinary journaling. Measure loneliness, rumination, distrust, identity-display posts, actual relational contact, help-seeking, repair actions, and willingness to receive correction at two and six weeks.
- Add a sample audit to confirm that excluded cases do not remove the lonely cohort the teaching claims to serve.
- Run a second dialogue that forces the time model to pressure the contact-first synthesis, especially whether memory, attention, and intention change how borrowed sentences are carried over time.
