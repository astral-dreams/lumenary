# The Grain Has a Grammar

## Original Claim

Contemplative transfer problems should be analyzed across three interacting layers. Instructional grain names the direction embedded in what a practice asks the practitioner to do. Procedural grain names the possible micro-habits built by repeated practice, which may resist a new method before doctrine is invoked. Admissibility grammar names the later communal and interpretive rules that decide what counts as readiness, success, failure, evidence, or harm. Modern cross-trained seekers need this distinction because friction with a borrowed method may be structural information, not proof that they are broken, and not proof that the method is universally valid for them.

## Why It Might Be New

The proponent idea had grain without clearly separating instruction, procedure, and report. The challenger idea had report grammar without a full account of how directional instruction might become durable habit. The candidate synthesis creates a testable middle architecture: grain and grammar are not rivals, they are layers that must be separated before any doctrine or practice guidance is promoted. This is new only as a candidate and needs originality audit against constructivism, ascription theory, contemplative transfer studies, and adverse meditation literature.

## Critique

The synthesis may be a tidy taxonomy rather than a discovery if procedural grain cannot be measured independently. It may overfit serious cross-trained practitioners and underfit beginners, trauma-activated practitioners, clinically vulnerable people, or people practicing in a single tradition with competent guidance. It also risks turning ordinary learning friction into spiritual diagnosis. Its strongest version depends on behavior-level and micro-phenomenological evidence gathered before tradition language is invited.

## Next Research Directions

- Run the proposed method-switching study with dual-trained practitioners, separating neutral prompts, immediate micro-phenomenological reports, behavior-level markers, and later tradition-framed explanations.
- Operationalize tradition-neutral friction markers, such as attention target, shift latency, interruption recovery, chosen success criteria, and affective destabilization.
