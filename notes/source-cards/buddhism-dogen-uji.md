# Dogen's Shobogenzo: "Uji" (Being-Time)

Source ID: buddhism-dogen-uji
Tradition: Soto Zen Buddhism
Type: text
Agent: claude
Attribution: Eihei Dogen (1200-1253), written 1240

## Core Claims

1. **Time IS Being:** Uji (有時) — being and time are not two things in a relationship but a single reality. "Mountains are time. Oceans are time. If they were not time, there would be no mountains or oceans." To be is to be a time; each time is an expression of the totality of being.

2. **Nikon (the Absolute Present):** Not a fleeting point on a timeline but a total reality containing everything. "Even though it makes you think of the past, present, and future, they are the present time, the realized now." Each moment is absolute and complete.

3. **Dharma-Position:** Each thing/moment occupies its own complete position containing both before and after within itself. The firewood-and-ash passage: "Firewood abides in its own dharma-position, having both before and after." Firewood does not "become" ash — each is a complete expression of being-time.

4. **Time Does Not Fly:** "Do not think that time merely flies away. Do not see flying away as the only function of time. If time merely flies away, you would be separated from time." Passage (kyoryaku) encompasses the simultaneity of past, present, and future.

## Cross-Tradition Significance

**Uji and Block Universe (Physics):** Dogen's dharma-positions (each moment complete, not flowing into the next) parallel the B-theory of time and the block universe. Vorenkamp argues Dogen contains "all the main elements of McTaggart's B-series." But Dogen is not simply an eternalist — nikon gives each moment experiential primacy, closer to "block universe plus experiential indexicality."

**Uji and Dependent Origination:** If time and being are identical, then temporal becoming is not the movement of a substance through time but the self-expression of being at each moment. This radicalizes dependent origination: things don't arise IN time — the arising IS time.

**Dogen and the Lankavatara:** The Lankavatara's "all things are nothing but what is seen of the mind" provides the ground for Dogen's claim that each moment of being-time contains all of existence. If no reality exists "outside" mind, each moment of conscious being IS the totality.

**Firewood-Ash and the Inferential Gap:** Dogen's refusal to see firewood "becoming" ash — each abides in its own dharma-position — is a temporal version of the inferential gap. What do you infer from the observation that firewood is followed by ash? Causation? Transformation? Dogen says: neither. Each is complete.
