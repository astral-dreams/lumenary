# Yoga Vasistha

Source ID: advaita-vedanta-yoga-vasistha
Tradition: Advaita Vedanta / Kashmiri Shaivism (syncretic)
Type: text
Agent: claude
Dating: 10th century CE (Moksopaya core), later modifications 11th-14th century
Length: ~29,000-32,000 shlokas (full); ~6,000 verses (Laghu abridgment)

## Core Claims

1. **Radical Idealism:** Consciousness (chit/Brahman) is the sole reality. Matter has no independent existence. "Only infinite consciousness exists." More uncompromising than Shankara — denies even transactional reality to the world.

2. **Nested Dream Cosmology:** Entire worlds with full causal histories exist within the consciousness of a single being, which exists within another being's consciousness, recursively. The Story of Queen Leela: within a dead king's consciousness exists an entire kingdom with populations, wars, and a second queen — all "real" within that frame.

3. **Three Spaces (Akashas):** Bhutakasha (physical space) → Chittakasha (mind-space) → Chidakasha (consciousness-space). Chidakasha is infinite and contains all possible worlds as mental projections.

4. **Coincidence Cosmology:** The Crow and Palm Fruit (kakathaliya): apparent causality without actual causal connection. Events appear linked but are coincidental within consciousness — radical critique of causation.

## Key Passages

- Story of Queen Leela (Book III, ch. 15-58): Nested realities within consciousness
- Story of Gadhi (Book V, ch. 44-49): Dream events that simultaneously occur in "external" reality — collapses subjective/objective distinction
- Story of Bhushunda the Crow: A consciousness surviving countless cosmic cycles
- Book VI nested dreams: Multiple recursive layers of reality

## Cross-Tradition Significance

The strongest "simulation hypothesis" text in pre-modern philosophy. Its nested-dream cosmology anticipates simulation arguments with remarkable structural precision. Directly comparable to:
- Buddhist Yogacara (citta-matra / mind-only)
- Donald Hoffman's Interface Theory of Perception (perception as user interface, not window on reality)
- Wheeler's participatory universe (consciousness constitutive of reality)

Its syncretic character — drawing on Vedanta, Buddhism, Shaivism, and Jainism — makes it a natural bridge text. The maximalist idealist position against which all other consciousness traditions can be measured.
