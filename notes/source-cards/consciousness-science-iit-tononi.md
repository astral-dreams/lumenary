## Core Claims

1. **Five Axioms → Postulates:** Starting from what experience IS like (intrinsicality, information, integration, exclusion, composition), IIT derives physical requirements. To exist intrinsically = to have cause-effect power upon itself.

2. **Phi (Φ):** Integrated information measures how much a system's cause-effect structure is irreducible. Consciousness = Φ > 0. The specific experience corresponds to the cause-effect structure (the "quale").

3. **Constitutive Panpsychism:** Any system with Φ > 0 has some experience. Graded, structural; tied to physical organization, not biological substance. Simple systems (photodiodes) have minimal Φ; feedforward networks (many current AIs) have Φ = 0.

4. **2025 Adversarial Collaboration (Nature):** IIT's prediction that posterior cortical areas suffice for consciousness was corroborated (n=256, three neuroimaging modalities). GNWT's prefrontal cortex prediction was not supported.

## Cross-Tradition Significance

**IIT and Vedanta:** Both claim consciousness is fundamental, not emergent. But IIT is pluralistic (many systems, many Φ values) while Vedanta is monistic (one Brahman). IIT grounds consciousness in physical causal structure; Vedanta treats consciousness as ontologically prior to the physical.

**IIT and Buddhism:** IIT's axiom of intrinsicality (consciousness has inherent existence) is in direct tension with Buddhist sunyata (nothing has svabhava/inherent existence). This is a genuine philosophical disagreement, not a translation issue.

**Strongest Criticisms:** Unfalsifiability charge (124-scholar letter, 2023); computational intractability (NP-hard for real brains); "unwieldy panpsychism" objection.
