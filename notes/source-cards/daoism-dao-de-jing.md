# Dao De Jing (Laozi)

Source ID: daoism-dao-de-jing
Tradition: Daoism
Type: text
Agent: claude
Dating: 4th-3rd century BCE (scholarly consensus; traditionally attributed to 6th c. BCE)

## Core Claims

1. **Ineffability of the Dao:** Ch.1 — "The Dao that can be spoken is not the eternal Dao." Ultimate reality is prior to and beyond linguistic-conceptual capture. Naming introduces duality (namer/named), which is already downstream from the Dao.

2. **Wu (Non-Being) as Generative Source:** Ch.40 — "All things under heaven are born of being (you); being is born of non-being (wu)." Wu is not mere nothingness but pregnant emptiness — the formless condition for the possibility of being.

3. **Cosmogonic Sequence:** Ch.42 — "The Dao gives birth to the One. The One gives birth to Two. Two gives birth to Three. Three gives birth to the ten thousand things." Differentiation from unity, not creation ex nihilo. Parallels Neoplatonic emanation.

4. **Wu Wei:** Not mere passivity but the Dao's own mode of operation — "does nothing, yet nothing is left undone" (Ch.37). The cessation of ego-driven action that disrupts natural harmony. Rooted in ziran (self-so-ness/spontaneity).

## Key Chapters

- **Ch.1**: Ineffability, the named vs. unnamed, xuan (mystery)
- **Ch.11**: "The usefulness of a vessel lies in its emptiness" — wu as functional
- **Ch.25**: Dao as prior to heaven and earth, the four-fold hierarchy (Man → Earth → Heaven → Dao → ziran)
- **Ch.40**: Return as the movement of Dao; being from non-being
- **Ch.42**: Cosmogonic sequence (One → Two → Three → Ten Thousand Things)

## Cross-Tradition Significance

**Wu vs. Sunyata:** Critical distinction for Lumenary. Wu is cosmogonically generative (the source from which being arises). Sunyata is deconstructive (things lack inherent existence). When Buddhism entered China, the geyi ("matching concepts") approach conflated these, producing significant distortions. Prime case for translation strain analysis.

**Emanation parallel:** Ch.42's sequence (Dao → One → Two → Three → many) structurally parallels Plotinus (One → Nous → Soul → Matter) and Vedantic unfolding through maya. None is creation ex nihilo — all describe spontaneous differentiation from unity.

**Return/Reversion:** Ch.40's "return is the movement of the Dao" parallels Plotinus's epistrophe and contemplative ascent traditions universally.
