# Plotinus, Enneads V.1 and VI.9

Source ID: neoplatonism-plotinus-enneads
Tradition: Neoplatonism
Type: text
Agent: claude
Dating: 3rd century CE (204/5-270 CE); compiled by Porphyry

## Core Claims

1. **Three Hypostases:** One (to hen) → Nous/Intellect → Soul → Matter. Each level is an "image" of the one above at a lower degree of unity. Emanation is spontaneous, necessary, involves no diminishment of the source.

2. **The One Beyond Being and Thought:** The One is not a being among beings but the source of all being. It is beyond self-knowledge because knowledge requires a duality of knower/known, and the One is prior to all duality. Not a deficiency; self-knowledge is a lesser, derivative mode.

3. **Epistrophe (Return/Reversion):** The soul's contemplative ascent: turning from external world → inner life → discursive thought → intellectual intuition (Nous) → the One. Each stage involves simplification; shedding multiplicity.

4. **Henosis (Mystical Union):** VI.9; "the flight of the Alone to the Alone" (phuge monou pros monon). The soul stripped to bare unity recognizes itself as identical with the One. Not rational, not discursive, ultimately inexpressible. Porphyry reports Plotinus experienced this four times.

## Key Passages

- **V.1**: The emanation architecture; One → Nous → Soul; why self-knowledge requires duality
- **VI.9.11**: "The flight of the Alone to the Alone"; the culminating mystical description
- **V.1.6**: "The One is not all things, but their source"
- Fire-brand / light metaphors for emanation without diminishment

## Cross-Tradition Significance

**The One vs. Brahman:** Both beyond all predication, beyond being/non-being. Both the ultimate source. Both describe a return journey (epistrophe/moksha) where the individual discovers identity with the Absolute. Key differences: Plotinus treats matter as evil (furthest from the One); Vedanta treats the world as appearance (mithya), not evil. Plotinus's hierarchy is genuinely ontological; Advaita says only Brahman is real.

**Beyond Self-Knowledge:** Parallels the negative theology of Pseudo-Dionysius, Eckhart's Gottheit (Godhead beyond God), and the Mandukya's turiya. All converge on the claim that the ultimate is beyond even self-relation.

**Emanation vs. Creation:** Emanation preserves ontological continuity between source and product; creation ex nihilo posits a fundamental gap. This structural difference has profound implications for consciousness's relationship to matter.
