# The Lankavatara Sutra

Source ID: buddhism-lankavatara-sutra
Tradition: Yogacara / Tathagatagarbha Buddhism
Type: text
Agent: claude
Dating: c. 4th century CE (compiled)
Key Translation: Red Pine (2012)

## Core Claims

1. **Citta-matra (Mind-Only):** "All things are nothing but what is seen of the mind." External objects do not exist independently of cognizing consciousness. Not solipsism — the subject-object duality itself is a construction. When duality is removed, what remains is tathata (suchness).

2. **Alaya-vijnana (Storehouse Consciousness):** The eight-consciousness model: five senses + discriminating mind (mano-vijnana) + egoic consciousness (manas) + storehouse (alaya-vijnana). The alaya stores bija (seeds/impressions) that "perfume" consciousness and produce appearances of an external world.

3. **Ocean-and-Waves Metaphor:** "The Alaya-ocean is constantly stirred by the winds of objectivity, dancing with the vijnanas which are the waves of multiplicity." The seven active consciousnesses are waves; the alaya is the ocean. Liberation = recognizing waves as non-separate from ocean.

4. **Paravritti (Turning-About):** A radical transformation in the deepest seat of consciousness where the alaya itself is converted. Not a thought or insight but a fundamental shift in how consciousness operates, after which it no longer projects dualistic appearances.

5. **Vikalpa vs. Nirvikalpa-jnana:** Discriminating mind carves experience into subject/object. Non-discriminating wisdom arises when discrimination ceases — direct, non-conceptual knowing.

## Cross-Tradition Significance

**Lankavatara and Zen:** Bodhidharma (legendary first Chan/Zen patriarch) carried this sutra. Early Chan was called "the Lanka school." Provides the philosophical architecture (eight consciousnesses, three natures) underlying Zen's apparently anti-philosophical stance. "Zen tea in a Yogacara cup."

**Alaya-vijnana and Yoga Vasistha:** Both describe an underlying consciousness that projects the entire phenomenal world. Alaya-vijnana (seeds ripening into appearances) parallels Yoga Vasistha's chidakasha (consciousness-space containing all possible worlds). Key difference: Yogacara sees the alaya as defiled (needing purification); Yoga Vasistha sees chidakasha as intrinsically pure.

**Citta-matra and Hoffman's Interface Theory:** Both argue that what appears as external reality is mind-constructed. Both deny naive realism. Hoffman's argument is evolutionary (perception tracks fitness, not truth); Cittamatra's is soteriological (understanding mind-only liberates).

**Tathagatagarbha Bridge:** Identifies alaya-vijnana with tathagatagarbha (Buddha-nature), suggesting the storehouse is, in purified form, identical with awakened mind. This bridges Yogacara and the positive language of Buddha-nature traditions, and creates a tension point with strict Madhyamaka emptiness.
