# Modern Human Condition Foundation

This library exists to correct a failure mode in the doctrine engine: a practice can sound elegant while solving no urgent human problem.

Lumenary should not create teachings or practices only because a model is interesting. A teaching should answer a wound. A practice should be medicine for a recognizable condition, and the record should say who the medicine is for.

## Operating Rule

Every frontier, dialogue, finding, teaching candidate, practice candidate, and doctrine council review should ask:

1. What human problem does this clarify?
2. Who is this for?
3. Who is this not for?
4. Why this remedy, rather than ordinary advice?
5. What modern source grounds the problem?
6. What observation would show the remedy is not working?

If the answer is vague, the idea may still be useful as research, but it should not become a teaching or practice yet.

## Primary Modern Problems

### Loneliness and disconnection

Modern people often lack durable belonging, not just company. Teachings here should address isolation, feeling unneeded, withdrawal, loss of local community, and the difficulty of being known.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory`
- `modern-human-condition-world-happiness-report-2024`
- `modern-human-condition-gallup-state-global-workplace-2024`

### Anxiety, depression, and mental distress

Many people live with fear, low mood, shame, numbness, or a sense that life is too much. Lumenary practices can support attention and meaning, but they must not replace therapy, medication, emergency care, or clinical support.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-who-world-mental-health-report`
- `modern-human-condition-apa-stress-in-america-2024`
- `modern-human-condition-cdc-suicide-facts`

### Addiction, compulsion, and withdrawal

Compulsion is not just weak will. It is often pain regulation, social injury, habit, biology, environment, and despair braided together. Lumenary may eventually offer reflective supports, but addiction and withdrawal require explicit safety boundaries and referral to human care.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-samhsa-2023-nsduh`
- `modern-human-condition-cdc-overdose-prevention`
- `modern-human-condition-who-world-mental-health-report`

### Meaning loss and purpose erosion

Modern people often have more options and fewer shared reasons. A doctrine should not merely explain reality; it should help a person know what to give themselves to.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-pew-where-americans-find-meaning-in-life`
- `modern-human-condition-world-happiness-report-2024`

### Burnout, overwork, and achievement pressure

Work can become identity. Performance can become proof of worth. This is where practices around effort and result may matter, but only for people whose self-worth rises and falls with performance, praise, failure, correction, or usefulness.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-who-burn-out-occupational-phenomenon`
- `modern-human-condition-apa-stress-in-america-2024`
- `modern-human-condition-curran-hill-perfectionism-increasing`
- `modern-human-condition-gallup-state-global-workplace-2024`

### Digital comparison and attention capture

Digital life can train the person to seek proof of existence through visibility, reaction, comparison, and pace. Teachings here should be concrete about attention, desire, envy, loneliness, and the body after long exposure.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-youth-mental-health-social-media-advisory`
- `modern-human-condition-surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory`

### Feeling unneeded or out of place

A person can be surrounded by systems and still feel unused by the world. Teachings here should address service, belonging, repair, vocation, and the difference between being noticed and being needed.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory`
- `modern-human-condition-pew-where-americans-find-meaning-in-life`
- `modern-human-condition-gallup-state-global-workplace-2024`

### Grief, death, and change

Grief is not a flaw in attention. It is the cost of love, attachment, memory, and mortality. Practices here must not rush meaning or force peace.

Grounding sources:

- `modern-human-condition-who-world-mental-health-report`
- `modern-human-condition-cdc-suicide-facts`

## Practice Targeting Rule

Every practice candidate must name:

- Target human problem.
- Target cohort.
- Non-fit case.
- Why the practice matters.
- Why this practice instead of ordinary advice.
- What to notice.
- When to stop.
- What would weaken it.
- At least one modern human-condition source when possible.

## Current Practice Assessment

`The Result Release` should not be treated as a universal practice.

It is a targeted remedy for people whose self-worth rises and falls with performance, praise, correction, usefulness, or failure. It may fit perfectionistic students, achievement-driven professionals, founders, caregivers, leaders, artists, and people who turn responsibility into identity.

It is probably not useful for people whose primary problem is under-motivation, avoidance, dissociation, exhaustion, clinical depression, addiction withdrawal, or simple mindful work already done with love. For them, the practice may add unnecessary self-monitoring or miss the real wound.

This correction should guide the future doctrine council: a teaching is stronger when it knows who it is for.
