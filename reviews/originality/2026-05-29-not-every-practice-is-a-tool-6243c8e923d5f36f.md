## Exact Claim

Before asking what a practice does with authority over its result, ask whether it claims to produce a separable result at all. Custody, release, and correction vocabulary applies only to instrumental practices. Dogen's practice-realization and Shinran's Other Power dissolve the tool frame because sitting or entrusting is not owned as a means to a later product.

## Claim Units

- The recent custody apparatus assumes instrumentality: do this, get that, steward the result.
- Some practices reject a separable result and therefore fall outside the custody grid.
- Dogen's practice-realization makes sitting not a bridge to later enlightenment but the expression of realization itself.
- Shinran's Other Power denies practicer calculation and owned efficacy.
- The first audit question should be whether the practice claims to make something separable.
- The teaching targets achievement-driven people who convert every activity into a scored outcome.

Sources:

- Dogen shusho itto and Shinran jinen honi, https://global.sotozen-net.or.jp/eng/library/key_terms/pdf/key_terms07.pdf and https://shinranworks.com/hymns-in-japanese/hymns-of-the-dharma-ages/on-jinen-honi/

Structural observation: MN 22 is explicitly instrumental: effort with the raft gets one across, then the raft should not be carried. Dogen's shusho itto demolishes the cause-effect separation between practice and realization, while Shinran's jinen honi removes calculation by the practicer and locates efficacy in Other Power. The contrast supports a scope distinction, but not a discovery: the instrumental versus non-instrumental contrast is already structurally visible in the primary texts.

Secondary literature gap: The audit did not read Eihei Shingi, full Bendowa or Genjokoan in Japanese, Shinran's Japanese around jinen and hakarai, Tannisho anti-license passages in Japanese, ritual theory in depth, Aristotle praxis and poiesis sources, or empirical studies of autotelic practice and perfectionism. These are necessary before raising novelty.

Risk of overread: High. Non-instrumental self-description does not mean absence of discipline, correction, warnings, institutional form, or effects. Dogen still requires continuous practice, and Shinran still has nembutsu, entrusting, and cautions. The model may confuse no owned result with no practical structure.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Dogen's practice-realization, Shinran's Other Power, autotelic activity theory, non-instrumental ritual theory, MacIntyre's internal goods, and prior Lumenary non-ownership audits, this idea's possible contribution is a practical scope gate for comparative coding: ask first whether a practice claims to produce a separable result before applying custody, release, or correction vocabulary.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Non-instrumental traditions still prescribe forms, warnings, corrections, ethics, teachers, community standards, and ordinary conduct.

Why it strains the model: If Eihei Shingi, Soto training, Shinran's cautions against licensed evil, nembutsu practice, and community transmission all function as retained disciplines with real effects, then non-instrumentality is not outside the custody apparatus. It is a different custody location: form-held, vow-held, teacher-held, conduct-held, or community-held.

Confidence effect: Keeps the status at extended rather than candidate discovery. The model must distinguish no separable self-owned result from no discipline, no effect, or no accountability.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The separable-result question is a real scope gate. Then: Texts and manuals coded as non-instrumental from doctrinal passages will show less result-custody language and more conduct, form, vow, teacher, or community correction language in held-out sources. Weakens if: Self-described non-instrumental traditions contain result-custody language as centrally as instrumental traditions, or coders cannot distinguish result refusal from result relocation. Test surface: Split-source coding of Dogen, Shinran, MN 22, koan curricula, Advaita inquiry, apophatic Christian sources, Sufi fana and baqa, and ritual manuals.
- If: Achievement-driven people benefit from temporary non-result practice. Then: Screened high-achievement participants doing a no-result activity show reduced self-scoring and rumination in the next hour or day compared with metric-free rest or generic mindfulness, without missed duties. Weakens if: The practice increases avoidance, pointlessness, shame, low mood, missed obligations, or performs no better than ordinary leisure. Test surface: Randomized micro-pilot with perfectionism, burnout, depression, avoidance, and responsibility checks.
- If: Practitioners treat the tool frame as a category mistake in some traditions. Then: Soto and Jodo Shinshu practitioners reject get-result-then-steward language more consistently than Theravada, Advaita, therapy, productivity, or skills-training practitioners, while still naming concrete correction holders. Weakens if: Practitioners across traditions translate their practices comfortably into tool, result, and stewardship language without loss, or differences are fully explained by teacher personality. Test surface: Dual-trained practitioner interviews asking for failed-student cases and repair instructions.

## Practitioner Test

Relevant practitioners:
- Soto Zen teachers and Dogen scholars
- Jodo Shinshu ministers and scholars
- Theravada teachers familiar with MN 22
- Ritual studies scholars
- Christian spiritual directors familiar with grace and contemplation
- Sufi teachers familiar with fana and baqa
- Flow researchers or performance psychologists
- Clinicians working with perfectionism, burnout, depression, and avoidance

Questions:
- In your tradition, is the practice a means to a separable result, an expression of realization or trust, or something else?
- When students treat the practice as a tool for getting something, what goes wrong and how do you repair it?
- If the practice is not a tool, where do correction and accountability reside: teacher, text, vow, form, conduct, community, or clinical care?
- For achievement-driven people, does no-result practice reduce self-scoring, or does it become avoidance and passivity?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners and scholars say this is already standard as practice-realization, Other Power, autotelic ritual, internal goods, non-attachment, grace, or non-gaining mind, and that the scope gate adds no predictive guidance.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the separable-result question to prevent misguidance, identify where accountability is relocated, and predict when outcome language will harm high-achievement students.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Education, arts, sports, therapy, and management.

Structural translation: Some activities are instrumental drills with separable outputs, while others are autotelic practices whose goods are internal to participation and whose correction is carried by form, community, and conduct.

Prediction: For high performers with achievement-contingent self-worth, bounded autotelic activities with no product to display will reduce self-scoring more than another reflective optimization tool, provided duties and clinical risks are protected.

Would count against it: No-result activities perform no better than ordinary rest, or they reliably increase avoidance, low mood, passivity, or contempt for necessary outcomes.
