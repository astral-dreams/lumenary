## Exact Claim

Some forms of help are distorted when the practitioner converts them into another private management task. An after-use review should be prescribed only if three fit conditions hold: the path permits provisional method use, the path names a later moment where a result can be handled, and the person has enough spare agency for review to help rather than add burden. If any condition fails, help may need to be carried by practice, community, vow, rest, ordinary conduct, teacher, or external support without another private audit.

## Claim Units

- Help can be damaged by being turned into a project of private management.
- Practice-ending or after-use review has preconditions: provisional method ownership, completion temporality, and available agency.
- Where these preconditions fail, custody should be relocated to forms such as community, vow, conduct, rest, teacher, or ordinary support.
- The main human-condition fit is depleted over-managers with burnout, compulsive self-monitoring, or achievement-contingent self-worth.
- The idea is a scoped contraindication to recent Lumenary after-use and method-authority practices, not a universal doctrine.

Structural observation: MN 22 supports a tool-use and non-clinging pattern: a teaching can be used for crossing and then not possessed. The Heart Sutra strains a simple release rule because it denies attainment while preserving reliance on prajnaparamita. Dogen-style practice-realization strains it further because practice is not merely an instrument awaiting later review. Shinran Other Power also resists practitioner-owned handling because merit and final efficacy are not primarily self-managed. The candidate's strongest move is not method-release, which is old, but the fit gate that asks whether ownership, temporality, and available agency exist before prescribing review.

Secondary literature gap: The audit still needs direct term-level work on Shinran's jinen honi and hakarai, Dogen's shusho-itto or practice-realization, apophatic unsaying, spiritual direction ethics, and ACT or CBT literature on self-monitoring. Source reliability is adequate for lowering novelty, not for promoting the model as a discovery.

Risk of overread: High. Some people harmed by over-management still need structure, accountability, clinical care, concrete help, or community obligations. A tradition may reject self-owned merit while retaining concrete forms of practice, conduct, teacher correction, and institutional discipline. Calling this non-management can hide a transfer of management to another holder.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Pargament's agency styles, ACT's critique of internal control, Buddhist non-grasping, apophatic unsaying, Cheetah House safety routing, and prior Lumenary correction-holder records, this idea says that after-use review itself should be blocked unless three fit conditions are present: provisional ownership, a permitted after, and spare agency. That is useful, but mostly an extension of known parts.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: A depleted person with achievement-contingent self-worth who improves only after a structured review, external accountability, or clinician-supported rule, while a no-audit prompt increases avoidance or helplessness.

Why it strains the model: The idea assumes that private review is often the burden for depleted over-managers. If structure and review are the relieving container, then the model confuses over-management with the absence of safe holding. It also fails if Other Power, Dogen practice-realization, or community-held practice includes rigorous review, conduct correction, and formal repetition without harming agency.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from moderate to cautious. The claim should remain a fit rule until pilots and practitioner interviews distinguish burdening review from supportive structure.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Ownership, completion temporality, and spare agency are real fit variables for after-use practices. Then: Split-source coding of primary manuals will show that traditions without practitioner-owned result or discrete after have different warning, verification, and repair patterns than traditions that permit method-use and later handling. Weakens if: Coders cleanly apply keep, release, and embody axes across Shinran, Dogen, Heart Sutra, MN 22, and apophatic sources without distortion, and the three fit variables do not predict held-out warnings or repairs. Test surface: Pre-registered comparative codebook with held-out manual warnings and practitioner interviews.
- If: No-audit permission helps depleted over-managers more than ordinary after-use review. Then: In a two-week pilot, screened over-managers using the No-Audit Check show lower rumination, lower self-surveillance, and no increase in missed duties compared with a structured after-use review group. Weakens if: No-audit increases avoidance, helplessness, shame, missed obligations, or performs no better than simple rest or planning. Test surface: Randomized low-risk pilot with burnout, rumination, self-worth contingency, action completion, and adverse-effect measures.
- If: The practice is cohort-specific, not universal. Then: Participants with high reflective compulsion benefit more than under-reflectors, grievers, people needing accountability, or people under material overload. Weakens if: Benefits are not moderated by over-management, or the practice harms the target cohort as much as non-fit groups. Test surface: Moderator analysis in pilot data plus practitioner triage interviews.
- If: Custody relocation is distinguishable from refusal of management. Then: Manuals that deny self-ownership still name alternative holders such as vow, grace, teacher, conduct, sangha, scripture, ritual, or ordinary duty, and those holders predict repair advice. Weakens if: No stable custody-holder patterns appear, or practitioner repairs do not track holder type. Test surface: Manual coding and concrete failed-student interview cases.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Knowledge work and creative production

Structural translation: A tool that helped produce work should not automatically become another self-management loop. Before a retrospective, postmortem, or optimization review, check whether the worker owns the lever, whether there is a real after-action moment, and whether the person has enough agency for the review to improve action rather than increase self-surveillance.

Prediction: For burned-out high performers, skipping or externally holding some retrospectives will reduce redundant self-critique and improve follow-through compared with mandatory self-review, while teams with clear accountability needs will still benefit from structured review.

Would count against it: Ordinary workload, leadership quality, psychological safety, and planning explain all effects, or no-audit policies mainly increase drift, missed obligations, and uncorrected errors.
