## Exact Claim

A path that dissolves, denies, or transcends selfhood still requires a minimum functioning self, agency, memory, reflexivity, and continuity, and each tradition's strategy for managing that requirement predicts its practice architecture.

## Claim Units

- Self-dissolving practice presupposes capacities for hearing instruction, effort, memory, error recognition, and self-observation.
- SN 22.59 dissolves the person while preserving the causal process of liberation.
- Chandogya 6.8.7 preserves the deepest Self while dissolving the reality of the student's apparent process.
- Causal continuity, ontological identity, temporal collapse, and divine restoration are proposed as management strategies.
- The management strategy should predict effort theory, verification, warnings, and post-practice fruit.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- SN 22.59 Anattalakkhana Sutta, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN22_59.html
- Chandogya Upanishad 6.8.7 through 6.16.3, https://shlokam.org/texts/Chandogya-6-8-7 and https://www.wisdomlib.org/hinduism/book/chandogya-upanishad-english/d/doc239333.html

Structural observation: SN 22.59 requires monks who can listen, answer, inspect each aggregate, and become disenchanted, yet it denies that any aggregate, including consciousness, is fit to be self. Its closing grammar says the monks' minds were released, preserving process without owner. Chandogya repeatedly addresses Shvetaketu as a learner asking for more explanation, yet the repeated teaching identifies him with the Self of all this. This supports the proposed tension, but it is not new by itself. It is close to classical Buddhist debates over personhood and to Advaita pedagogy around recognizing the already present Self.

Secondary literature gap: I did not find an exact four-strategy cross-tradition typology that predicts architecture across Buddhism, Advaita, Zen, and Sufism. But the main pressure is heavily precedented in Buddhist personhood debates, Pudgalavada arguments, Collins on selfless persons, Engler on ego strength before no-self, functional-self accounts in Buddhist psychology, and contemporary self-pattern theories of meditation.

Risk of overread: High. The model may turn a researcher's philosophical tension into a practitioner's lived problem. It also risks forcing mixed traditions into clean boxes. Theravada, Zen, Advaita, Sufism, Dzogchen, and Christian grace traditions often combine effort, surrender, recognition, teacher relation, and ethical transformation across stages.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Engler, Pudgalavada debates, Collins, or functional-self Buddhist psychology, this idea treats the minimum self as a comparative architecture variable across traditions and claims it predicts effort, verification, warnings, and fruit.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Other-power and direct-introduction traditions, such as Jodo Shinshu entrusting and Dzogchen pointing-out, appear to minimize self-powered agency while still functioning as paths.

Why it strains the model: If a tradition can produce stable transformation through vow, grace, devotion, direct introduction, or teacher-field participation with very little reflexive self-management, then the minimum self may not be an architectural driver. The model would need a scale of required self-capacities rather than a universal requirement.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the universal form of the claim. It supports a revised model where paths require some functional receiving capacity, but the amount and role vary sharply.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The stronger a path's final self-dissolution claim, the more explicit its safeguards should be against premature or distorted self-loss. Then: Manuals and teachers should warn against nihilism, dissociation, spiritual bypassing, false attainment, antinomianism, or ego-inflation in ways that match the path's self-management strategy. Weakens if: Warnings show no relationship to self-teaching and track only institution, teacher personality, or general ethics. Test surface: Coded comparison of Theravada, Zen, Dzogchen, Advaita, Sufi, Christian apophatic, and Pure Land training materials.
- If: Long, effortful paths should develop more elaborate continuity mechanisms than sudden or other-power paths. Then: Theravada stages, Sufi stations, and Advaita preparation should specify capacities, sequence, and error correction; sudden methods should shift burden to teacher recognition, pointing-out, vows, or post-recognition integration. Weakens if: Practice length and self-continuity machinery are unrelated, or sudden paths have equally elaborate minimum-self architecture before recognition. Test surface: Comparative coding of practice sequences, teacher-student protocols, and attainment verification.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report different kinds of permitted selfhood when switching methods. Then: Vipassana permits causal observation without ownership; Advaita permits witness or Self recognition; Soto Zen discourages gaining selfhood; Sufi practice permits surrendering agency into relation with God. Weakens if: Practitioners report no stable shift, or the shifts are better explained by temperament and teacher style. Test surface: Structured interviews with practitioners trained in two or more lineages.
- If: The point where a method consumes its prerequisite should be specially marked as difficult, risky, or decisive. Then: Texts and practitioners should mark investigation of the investigator, hearing tat tvam asi, fana, kensho, pointing-out, or surrender as a crisis or verification point. Weakens if: These moments are not specially marked, or transformation is described as ordinary continuity without any self-prerequisite tension. Test surface: Primary-text close reading plus practitioner interviews and retreat-report coding.

- Would this rubric change how you screen students, sequence practices, verify insight, or warn against errors?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners identify the idea as already standard: conventional person versus ultimate no-self, somebody before nobody, sadhana before recognition, practice-realization, fana and baqa, or other-power entrusting.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners find that the rubric predicts real training differences and clarifies why premature self-dissolution, clinging to witness, bypassing, or false surrender fail differently across paths.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and transformative education

Structural translation: Any method that aims to dissolve or radically transform the learner's starting identity must preserve enough agency, memory, trust, and reflexivity for the method to work.

Prediction: Therapies or pedagogies that deconstruct identity, such as trauma therapy, exposure therapy, psychedelic therapy, contemplative psychotherapy, or critical pedagogy, should have stronger screening, scaffolding, integration, and teacher-client safeguards than methods that merely add skills.

Would count against it: If deconstructive methods require no more scaffolding than ordinary skills training, or if safeguards do not track the degree of identity transformation, the structural claim is weakened.
