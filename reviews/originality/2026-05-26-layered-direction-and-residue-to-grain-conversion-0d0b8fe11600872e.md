## Exact Claim

Contemplative traditions install direction through layered mechanisms: procedural instructions, residue authorization after negation, and social or linguistic report norms. With repetition, authorized residue can become embodied grain. Early mismatch should appear as confusion about what may be found or claimed, while mature mismatch should appear as switching costs, attentional resistance, or specific distortions in practice.

## Claim Units

- Direction in contemplative practice is layered rather than unitary.
- Procedure directs the practitioner from the beginning by selecting what to notice, devalue, seek, or refuse.
- Residue authorization directs the practitioner by defining what may remain after negation.
- Teacher expectations and report norms carry social and linguistic direction.
- Repeated residue authorization can become embodied attentional grain.
- Early mismatch should show up as confusion about what may be found or claimed.
- Mature mismatch should show up as switching costs, attentional resistance, or practice-specific distortions.
- The model adds a temporal sequence: procedural direction can operate early, while residue direction may become embodied later.

Structural observation: MN 10 gives clear procedural direction: attention is not generic noticing, but a guided sequence through body, feeling, mind, mental qualities, hindrances, aggregates, arising, passing, abandoning, and cultivation. SN 22.59 supplies a residue policy for aggregates, including consciousness, by refusing ownership language and ending in disenchantment and release. Vivekachudamani vv. 17-30 gives the opposite directional texture: prerequisites are already aimed toward Advaita recognition through discrimination between permanent and impermanent, dispassion, restraint, faith in scripture and guru, concentration on God, and desire for liberation. Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 authorizes an unseen seer and knower as inner controller. Close-reading supports the claim that procedure and residue policy direct practice, but it does not by itself prove that residue becomes embodied grain. That requires longitudinal practitioner evidence.

Secondary literature gap: Search found close near-neighbors in Foucault on technologies of the self, Hadot on spiritual exercises, Bourdieu and Wacquant on habitus and hysteresis, Saba Mahmood on pious habitus, Lutz, Dunne, Jha, and Saron on the phenomenological matrix, Dunne on Buddhist mindfulness styles, Lindahl and Britton's VCE work, Katz and Sharf on constructed or rhetorically framed experience, and Luhrmann on attentional learning. This audit did not review full books by Foucault, Hadot, Bourdieu, Mahmood, Asad, Lindbeck, or Katz, nor original-language Pali, Sanskrit, Tibetan, Japanese, Arabic, or Persian sources, nor actual practitioner interviews.

Risk of overread: High. The model risks treating texts, teacher norms, report vocabulary, actual phenomenology, and embodied habit as one process. It also risks making every difference count as grain and every doctrinal sentence count as residue policy. Traditions such as Soto practice-realization, Dzogchen direct introduction, Pure Land Other Power, inherited practice, and apophatic silence may reject the developmental sequence or the analyst's residue vocabulary.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Foucault, Hadot, Bourdieu, Wacquant, Mahmood, Lutz, Dunne, Saron, Lindahl, Britton, Katz, Sharf, Luhrmann, and prior Lumenary Grain and Residue records, this idea's distinct claim is a testable temporal bridge: residue authorization is not only a doctrinal policy or report norm, it may become embodied grain through repeated practice, while procedural direction can install grain earlier. The difference is useful, but thin unless longitudinal and split-source tests show predictive lift beyond habitus, practice-style, report-vocabulary, and VCE-style factor baselines.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Successful dual-trained or hybrid practitioners who switch methods smoothly after clear instruction, plus traditions that refuse the analyst's developmental sequence, especially Soto practice-realization, Dzogchen direct introduction, Pure Land Other Power, inherited practice, and apophatic silence.

Why it strains the model: If practitioners can move from vipassana to Advaita, from self-inquiry to noting, from non-attachment to devotion, or from devotional prayer to deconstructive practice without old attentional habits persisting, then grain may be current ecology and vocabulary rather than embodied residue. If Pure Land or grace-held practice keeps efficacy outside practitioner-owned capacity, residue may not become the practitioner's grain at all. If Soto practice-realization collapses means and fruit, early and mature mismatch may not separate cleanly.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality confidence from candidate discovery to extended. The model remains promising only if it predicts stage-specific and direction-specific switching failures beyond teacher style, vocabulary, social ecology, practice intensity, and clinical risk.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Split-source coding of manuals should identify where direction first enters, then predict held-out warnings and repair instructions. Then: Procedural-direction texts should predict immediate attentional errors, such as tracking the wrong object or applying judgment when suspended. Residue-authorization texts should predict claim-confusion warnings, such as reifying awareness, collapsing into nihilism, self-crediting, or refusing responsibility. Social-report norms should predict acceptable testimony and teacher correction patterns. Weakens if: Held-out warnings are predicted as well or better by tradition identity, teacher style, institution, practice intensity, social ecology, or generic beginner problems, with no incremental value from direction-layer coding. Test surface: Pre-registered corpus coding across MN 10, SN 22.59, Vivekachudamani, Brihadaranyaka, one Dzogchen or Mahamudra manual, one Soto source, one Pure Land source, one Sufi source, and one Christian apophatic source.
- If: Early and mature mismatch should differ phenomenologically and behaviorally. Then: Beginners crossing traditions should mostly ask what is allowed to be found, named, trusted, or claimed. Mature practitioners should report automatic attentional pull, such as noting when asked to rest, witness-seeking when asked to deconstruct, affective restraint when asked to surrender, or devotional personalization when asked to bracket relation. Weakens if: Beginners and mature practitioners show the same generic confusion, or mature resistance disappears after one clear explanation of the new method. Test surface: Longitudinal micro-phenomenological interviews with beginner, intermediate, and advanced dual-trained practitioners, coded blind to tradition and stage.
- If: Residue-to-grain conversion should produce nonverbal or low-vocabulary markers that survive report-language controls. Then: Prior training should predict attentional target selection, recovery after distraction, affective valence toward arising phenomena, observer stabilization or dissolution, and response latency when switching methods, even when participants are not invited to use tradition-specific language. Weakens if: All apparent grain signal disappears when report vocabulary, teacher framing, and community identity are controlled. Test surface: Mixed-method study using interviews, timed practice prompts, experience sampling, teacher-blind coding, and lexical controls.
- If: Adverse effects should show different profiles for early authorization confusion and mature embodied distortion. Then: Early mismatch should cluster around uncertainty, permission, fear of doing the practice wrong, and forbidden claimant confusion. Mature mismatch should cluster around witness reification, nihilistic flattening, devotional rupture, compulsive deconstruction, or inability to stop a trained attentional operation. Weakens if: VCE-style variables such as retreat intensity, sleep disruption, trauma history, psychiatric vulnerability, weak teacher support, or social isolation explain the adverse pattern without residue-to-grain variables. Test surface: Re-code VCE-style adverse-event interviews and teacher case consultations for prior training, current practice, direction layer, stage, warning, repair, and outcome.
- If: The bridge claim should outperform its parent ideas separately. Then: A model using both residue policy and grain-stage variables should predict switching difficulty and repair better than grain-only, residue-only, practice-technique-only, or social-ecology-only models. Weakens if: The interaction term adds no predictive value, or grain-only and VCE-factor baselines fully absorb the effect. Test surface: Inter-rater coded dataset of cross-training cases with preregistered comparison models.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and professional retraining

Structural translation: Procedure becomes the method's explicit operations. Residue authorization becomes what remains trusted after critique of the old self-model, such as agency, bodily signal, cognition, parts, relationship, or surrender. Social and linguistic direction becomes supervision, case language, and acceptable progress reports. Grain becomes the automatic trained operation that persists after the person enters a new method.

Prediction: CBT-trained clients entering psychodynamic therapy should initially turn free association into problem solving. Somatic-therapy clients entering cognitive restructuring should over-prioritize body signal. IFS-trained clients entering exposure work should over-personify processes. Experienced clinicians switching modalities should show mature switching costs that differ from beginner vocabulary confusion. Explicit re-graining should improve integrative therapy more than simply adding new instructions.

Would count against it: Switching costs are fully explained by diagnosis, alliance, therapist skill, session dose, client preference, or vocabulary familiarity, and prior-method grain adds no prediction of failure shape or repair.
