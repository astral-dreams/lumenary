## Exact Claim

Advice to use a method, release it, and not own the result is not a universal account of practice completion. It is medicine for active-voice, self-power practitioners who believe effort earns results and convert those results into worth. Other-power, practice-realization, and no-attainment sources strain the release frame because they deny the practitioner as holder, the method as completed possession, or the linear after in which release would occur.

## Claim Units

- The validate-versus-undermine and retain-abandon-embody grid is mostly prior art, not discovery.
- Dogen, Shinran, and the Heart Sutra break the grid by rejecting an active owner who finishes and assigns custody.
- The better variable may be grammatical voice of completion-talk: active, middle, passive, or mixed.
- Release teaching fits people who believed they held or earned the result.
- The modern human problem is achievement-contingent self-worth and burnout, not spiritual completion in general.

Structural observation: MN 22 assumes a practitioner can grasp the Dhamma rightly, use it for crossing, and then not hold it as a possession. Shinran's jinen-honi and Tannisho relocate efficacy to Other Power and repeatedly reject practicer calculation, making even discussion of no working liable to become another working. This is a strong primary-text basis for narrowing release advice by agency grammar. But it also shows the candidate's central insight is not new inside Shinran: one cannot release as self-power what was never self-power to begin with.

Secondary literature gap: This audit did not read Japanese, Pali, Sanskrit, Chinese, or commentarial originals. It did not read full Lewin on middle voice, full Heidegger and Eckhart scholarship, full Pargament, full Hori, full Dogen scholarship, or Jodo Shinshu scholarship beyond accessible translations and summaries. No Shin, Soto, Rinzai, or Christian grace practitioners were interviewed.

Risk of overread: High. 'Middle voice' is an analyst metaphor that may not map cleanly onto Japanese, Sanskrit, Pali, or Chinese practice grammar. The active versus middle distinction may become a renamed self-power versus Other Power distinction, or it may hide stage shifts where effort and surrender coexist.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Lewin on middle voice, Shinran on Other Power, Dogen on practice-realization, Pargament on religious coping styles, Heart Sutra no-attainment reliance, Smith, Sells, and prior Lumenary completion models, this idea's possible contribution is a testable fit rule: give release-the-method advice only to practitioners whose own language shows active earning and result-ownership, then test whether the advice helps them more than it helps other-power, nondual, or practice-realization practitioners.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Stage-mixed traditions and practitioners, where active effort, surrender, grace, teacher recognition, ordinary conduct, and no-attainment are all present at different moments.

Why it strains the model: If most actual paths shift voices by stage, role, and context, then active versus middle voice will not sort traditions or predict guidance. Pure Land practitioners still say the nembutsu, Zen practitioners still sit, Christian grace traditions still discipline attention, and high-striving nondual practitioners may still benefit from release advice despite no-owner doctrine.

Confidence effect: Keeps the idea at extended. It can only rise if voice coding predicts warnings, confusion, and repair better than self-power, Other Power, Pargament agency style, teacher style, and clinical-risk baselines.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Release teaching is cohort-specific medicine for active earning and result-ownership. Then: High-striving practitioners who describe results as earned will show reduced pride, shame, comparison, and burnout after the Earning Check, while other-power or practice-realization practitioners will show little benefit or increased self-monitoring from the same instruction. Weakens if: All cohorts benefit equally, or the instruction mainly licenses avoidance, passivity, or contempt for ordinary responsibility. Test surface: Randomized pilot stratified by self-power language, perfectionism, tradition background, and achievement-contingent self-worth.
- If: Grammatical voice predicts practice guidance better than custody-grid coding. Then: Blind coding of completion-talk as active, middle, passive, mixed, or no-voice predicts held-out warnings, repairs, and supervision needs better than retain, release, embody categories alone. Weakens if: Custody values, tradition identity, teacher style, institution type, or Pargament agency styles predict guidance as well as or better than voice coding. Test surface: Split-source codebook tested on MN 22, Tannisho, Dogen, Heart Sutra, Rinzai koan curriculum, Christian grace texts, and practitioner interviews.
- If: The embodied-without-ownership cell is too elastic. Then: Coders will overuse embodied-without-ownership for Dogen, Shinran, Heart Sutra, Dzogchen, apophasis, and other hard cases, with low inter-rater reliability and weak prediction of warnings. Weakens if: The cell has clear boundaries, high inter-rater reliability, and predicts concrete repairs better than voice coding. Test surface: Inter-rater trial comparing the old two-axis grid against active, middle, passive, and mixed voice labels.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Education and workplace performance coaching

Structural translation: Advice to let go of outcomes helps people who over-own results as proof of worth. It can misfire for people whose outcomes are mainly constrained by systems, teams, luck, injury, caregiving load, discrimination, or externally assigned roles.

Prediction: Coaches who screen for perceived result ownership before giving detachment advice will reduce perfectionistic rumination and burnout better than coaches who give universal 'let go of the outcome' advice.

Would count against it: Screened and universal advice have equal effects, or material constraints, workload, sleep, discrimination, team support, and clinical variables fully explain outcomes with no added effect from ownership-language fit.
