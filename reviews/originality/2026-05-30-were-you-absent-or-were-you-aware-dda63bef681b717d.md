## Exact Claim

Before applying post-gap evidence gates, first distinguish a memoryless absence from a remembered bare awareness, because the two report types carry different evidential risks and produce different identity errors.

## Claim Units

- The word blank hides at least two report types: no-memory absence and remembered bare awareness.
- The absence-or-awareness fork is prior to correction, residue, and report-clause gates.
- Collapsing the two reports feeds opposite errors: I vanished, or I found an indestructible self.
- The proposed practice asks stable practitioners to sort the blank and suspend identity verdicts.
- The human-condition fit is discontinuity anxiety, fear of sleep or death, and solitary inflation of empty states.

Structural observation: Brihadaranyaka 4.3.23 protects an unlost seer when no second object appears, which is not simply a remembered in-gap experience. It is closer to a doctrinal and inferential witness claim. MN 44 describes cessation through prior training and emergence, not through a private self-report of awareness inside the gap. AN 9.34 treats cessation as known through discernment and fruit. SN 22.59 blocks turning consciousness into owner. A three-way codebook, inferred-from-absence, claimed-in-gap awareness, and verified-by-training or fruit, fits the sources better than the candidate's two-way fork.

Risk of overread: High. The fork may be an artifact of interview prompts, memory reconstruction, or doctrinal vocabulary. Advaita also strains the model because absence itself can be used to argue for presence. The practice can worsen derealization, fear of sleep, obsessive self-monitoring, or dissociation if applied outside the narrow stable cohort.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Thompson, Windt, Alcaraz-Sanchez, classical dreamless-sleep debates, and prior Lumenary gap-report work, this idea says the first practitioner-facing audit question should be: was this a no-memory absence or a remembered bare awareness? That simplification is useful, but the structure is mostly renamed prior art.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Advaita-style inference from absence, where the absence of objects in deep sleep is used to argue for unlost witnessing, rather than being sorted as either absence or remembered in-gap awareness.

Why it strains the model: It shows that the binary is too clean. A report can be phenomenologically opaque but doctrinally used as evidence for presence. That means the prior variable may be not absence versus awareness, but report source: edge inference, in-gap memory claim, or training-fruit verification.

Confidence effect: Lowers novelty and lowers confidence in the two-way fork. Raises confidence in a three-way coding protocol.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The absence-or-awareness fork is an independently usable prior variable. Then: Neutral interviewers can sort reports into no-memory absence and remembered bare awareness with acceptable inter-rater reliability before doctrinal labels are introduced. Weakens if: Reports collapse into mixed, indeterminate, or interviewer-shaped categories, or coders need doctrinal outcome passages to classify them. Test surface: Blind-coded micro-phenomenological interviews with Advaita, Tibetan sleep-yoga, Theravada, and untrained participants.
- If: Remembered bare-awareness reports are not just waking-edge reconstruction. Then: A stable subgroup of trained practitioners reports sparse but consistent in-gap awareness features under serial awakenings and careful follow-up. Weakens if: The reports map mainly to sleep-state misperception, prompt effects, white dreams, memory reconstruction, or subtle ordinary content. Test surface: Sleep-lab serial awakening study with polysomnography, immediate reports, and blinded phenomenological coding.
- If: The fork helps the target human problem rather than intensifying rumination. Then: Screened adults with discontinuity anxiety show fewer catastrophic or grand identity verdicts after the Name the Blank practice than after ordinary journaling or sleep education alone. Weakens if: The practice increases derealization, sleep fear, obsessive checking, or performs no better than ordinary reassurance. Test surface: Low-risk pilot excluding dissociation, trauma amnesia, panic disorder, psychosis, mania, substance blackout, and unexplained loss of consciousness.
- If: Primary texts support a prior routing distinction. Then: Blind coders distinguish inferred-from-absence, claimed-in-gap awareness, and verified-by-training or fruit across Brihadaranyaka, Yoga, Tibetan, MN 44, AN 9.34, and SN 22.59 sources. Weakens if: The two-way or three-way categories cannot be coded without later commentary or tradition labels. Test surface: Split-source textual coding using multiple translations and original-language checks.
