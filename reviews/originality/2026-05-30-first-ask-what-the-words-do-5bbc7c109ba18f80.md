## Exact Claim

After deep quiet or objectless awareness, do not ask what a sentence proves until its speech role has been named. Post-silence language may be instruction, warning, defense, report, repair, scientific description, direct assertion, or identity verdict. No post-silence conclusion should become doctrine or a verdict on a person until the role of the words and the fruit of using them have been observed.

## Claim Units

- Post-negation or post-silence sentences do different kinds of work, even when they look like rival conclusions about one state.
- A sentence-role classification should precede inference coding or doctrinal comparison.
- Religious, scientific, and safety language can be mistaken for identity verdicts when the listener is isolated or anxious.
- The practice value is narrow: reduce unsupported interpretation, meaning loss, isolation, and achievement-contingent self-worth in stable solo practitioners.
- The claim weakens if role coding is unreliable, obvious to practitioners, or no better than ordinary discernment, conversation, rest, or journaling.

Structural observation: Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 appears in a debate frame and names the self as the unobjectified knower, unseen but seeing, with a strongly declarative and revelatory function. SN 22.59 uses a repeated question sequence on form, feeling, perception, fabrications, and consciousness, then trains the listener not to regard them as mine, self, or what I am. SN 22.95 supplies a practitioner method of seeing, observing, and examining until the aggregate appears empty of substance. The candidate correctly notices that these passages do not merely assert competing propositions. They also authorize different practices of attention, identity, and non-clinging. The originality risk is that this is exactly what speech-act theory, religious-language analysis, and discursive-tradition analysis already train readers to notice.

Risk of overread: High. Role coding can become a way to soften or evade direct doctrinal claims. A passage can be both assertion and instruction, or both warning and ontology. English translations may hide genre and grammar. Direct-recognition, practice-realization, and Other Power cases may refuse clean separation among word, act, gift, recognition, and result. The human-condition use is plausible only for stable overinterpreters, not for acute panic, psychosis, mania, dissociation, trauma amnesia, active addiction withdrawal, medical blackouts, OCD or scrupulosity loops, unsafe authority settings, or people already under competent guidance.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Austin, religious-language theory, Asad, Sharf, VCE, and prior Lumenary authorization records, this candidate says the first coding operation after quiet states should be sentence role, before doctrinal inference, neuroscience interpretation, or identity verdict. That is a useful bounded extension, not a standalone discovery.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Direct-recognition and practice-realization lineages, such as Dzogchen or Mahamudra pointing-out, Dogen practice-realization, and Pure Land Other Power.

Why it strains the model: In these cases, a phrase may not merely describe, instruct, warn, or report. It may be treated as the performative event of recognition, entrusting, or practice itself. If word, act, authority, and result are not separable, sentence-role coding may impose an analytic grid that practitioners would reject.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence and requires analyst-frame-refused or inseparable-role values before public use.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Blind coders can classify post-negation sentences by role before coding the conclusion they license. Then: Usable inter-rater agreement on roles such as assertion, instruction, warning, defense, report, repair, praise, direct recognition, and analyst-frame-refused across Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23, SN 22.59, SN 22.95, Udana 1.10, Heart Sutra, Dogen, and one pointing-out manual. Weakens if: Low agreement, or agreement only after coders already know the tradition and doctrinal conclusion. Test surface: Split-source coding study with primary passages and commentaries withheld until after first-pass role coding.
- If: Sentence-role coding changes later interpretation and counsel. Then: Dual-trained practitioners say the same quiet-state report receives different counsel when the explanation is framed as warning, instruction, report, direct assertion, scientific mechanism, or identity verdict. Weakens if: Practitioners say this distinction is obvious and does not alter counsel, or conclusions stay unchanged across sentence roles. Test surface: Practitioner interviews with Advaita, Theravada, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Zen, spiritual direction, and contemplative-science practitioners.
- If: The practice reduces harmful private verdicts for the named cohort. Then: Screened stable solo practitioners using the Sentence Role Check show lower identity-verdict urgency, shame, rumination, and isolation than ordinary journaling after quiet states. Weakens if: Ordinary rest, conversation, or journaling works as well or better, or the role check increases self-surveillance and checking. Test surface: Small four-week practice pilot with exclusions for crisis, dissociation, OCD or scrupulosity loops, unsafe authority, and medical blackout concerns.
- If: Science explanations can be prevented from becoming covert identity verdicts by role labeling. Then: Participants given a predictive-processing explanation of quiet states make fewer self-worth or self-existence verdicts when first asked to label the explanation as mechanism, not verdict. Weakens if: Role labeling has no effect on identity conclusions, or increases anxiety and checking. Test surface: Matched vignette experiment using spiritual instruction, neuroscience mechanism, safety warning, and identity-verdict framings.

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is standard speech-act discernment, source criticism, spiritual direction, or teacherly common sense, and that the new labels do not change counsel or prediction.

Answers that support contribution: They identify concrete misinterpretation cases where role classification prevents harm or predicts repair better than doctrine-only, experience-only, or support-holder coding.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Clinical and scientific explanation after unusual mental states

Structural translation: A sentence about an event can function as diagnosis, safety instruction, mechanism, reassurance, prognosis, identity verdict, or repair plan. Treating all such sentences as evidence for identity creates harm.

Prediction: In psychoeducation for panic, dissociation-adjacent states, sleep paralysis, or burnout blankness, role labeling should reduce catastrophizing and identity conclusions more than content explanation alone.

Would count against it: If role labeling does not reduce catastrophizing or makes reassurance seeking worse, the structure does not travel cleanly outside contemplative interpretation.
