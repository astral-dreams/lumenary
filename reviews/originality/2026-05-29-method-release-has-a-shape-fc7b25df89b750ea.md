## Exact Claim

A mature practice is not finished when it produces insight. It also teaches its post-use relation: whether the method validates or undermines its own way of seeing, and whether the practice should be retained, released, or embodied as ordinary conduct.

## Claim Units

- Methods train the practitioner's post-use relation to the method.
- The prior confirming, canceling, dissolving model misses possessability and aftermath.
- A two-axis model, validates versus undermines, and retained versus abandoned versus embodied, explains post-insight risks and training patterns.
- Achievement pressure can turn a released method into a trophy or an embodied method into an endless checklist.
- A mature practice includes aftermath instructions.

Structural observation: MN 22 is a direct release template: the Dhamma is used for crossing and then not carried. The Heart Sutra complicates release because it denies path and attainment while still relying on Prajnaparamita, mantra, recitation, and teacher-mediated practice. Dogen complicates both retention and abandonment because practice and realization are described as one, so there is no clean after-stage where custody is decided.

Secondary literature gap: Before any public claim, read fuller work on upaya, raft and ladder metaphors, Wittgenstein's ladder, Nagarjuna's no-thesis claim, Hori on koan pedagogy, Sells on apophasis, Hadot on spiritual exercises, Dogen scholarship, Shinran scholarship, and Pargament on religious agency styles. Japanese, Pali, Sanskrit, and Chinese terms were not checked here.

Risk of overread: High. Retained, released, and embodied may be analyst categories imposed on traditions whose native grammar is vow, grace, ritual, teacher correction, lineage, or practice-realization. The model may also mistake a single tradition's stage-specific instructions for a stable method type.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 22, Wittgenstein's ladder, Smith's Nagarjuna-Wittgenstein therapy, Sells's apophatic unsaying, Dogen's practice-realization, and prior Lumenary after-use models, this idea's possible contribution is a bounded coding rubric that tests whether method type predicts held-out warnings, repairs, and post-insight temptations in achievement-pressured practitioners.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen practice-realization, Shinran Other Power, and the Heart Sutra's no-attainment-with-reliance pattern.

Why it strains the model: These cases do not merely choose keep, release, or embody. They challenge the assumed sequence: method first, result next, custody afterward. They may relocate custody to practice, vow, text, teacher, sangha, or conduct rather than grant a practitioner a post-use choice.

Confidence effect: Substantially lowers confidence in universal scope. The model can survive as a heuristic only if it first codes whether a tradition permits a separable result and practitioner custody.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Method shape predicts aftermath instruction. Then: Blind coders can code validates versus undermines and retained versus abandoned versus embodied from instruction passages, then predict held-out warnings, repairs, and verification rules better than tradition label alone. Weakens if: Coders cannot agree on the axes, or the axes do not predict held-out warnings better than generic variables such as teacher style, institution, practice intensity, or tradition identity. Test surface: Split-source corpus coding across MN 22, Heart Sutra, Mandukya, Dogen, Shinran, apophatic Christian, Sufi, Advaita, and koan-training sources.
- If: Different method shapes create different post-practice temptations. Then: Dual-trained practitioners report trophy insight after confirming methods, nihilistic dismissal or premature release after undermining methods, and endless self-performance after embodied methods. Weakens if: Practitioners report the same temptation profile across method types, or differences are better explained by personality, trauma history, teacher style, or institutional culture. Test surface: Semi-structured practitioner interviews asking for concrete failure and repair cases, not agreement with the model.
- If: The After-Use Check helps a narrow high-achievement cohort. Then: Screened perfectionistic practitioners show lower self-scoring and rumination after a brief keep, release, ask, or embody check than after generic journaling, without increased avoidance of duties. Weakens if: The check increases rumination, scrupulosity, superiority, avoidance, or workload denial, or generic rest performs equally well. Test surface: Small randomized micro-pilot with measures for perfectionism, burnout, rumination, responsibility avoidance, and ordinary conduct.

Questions:
- Do you actually distinguish practices that should be kept, released, or embodied, or is this outsider vocabulary?
- Can you name concrete cases where carrying a method after it helped became harmful, and what repair was given?
- Does this rubric predict student warnings or repairs better than your existing terms such as upaya, non-attachment, surrender, practice-realization, integration, or ordinary supervision?
- For high-achievement practitioners, does an After-Use Check reduce worth-pressure or create another performance ritual?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is standard raft teaching, upaya, integration, surrender, practice-realization, or supervision in new language, and that the rubric does not change sequencing or repair.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the rubric to distinguish actual failure modes, choose different repairs, and prevent achievement-driven method ownership in cases where older vocabulary was too broad.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Education, therapy, coaching, and organizational training.

Structural translation: Learning methods also have aftermaths: some drills must be retained, some scaffolds should be removed, and some lessons should become ordinary conduct rather than identity.

Prediction: Programs that explicitly teach after-use relations for their methods will show better transfer and less dependency, performance identity, or ritualized self-monitoring than programs that only teach the method.

Would count against it: After-use instructions add no predictive value over ordinary practice frequency, teacher quality, or workload conditions, or they increase rumination and dependency.
