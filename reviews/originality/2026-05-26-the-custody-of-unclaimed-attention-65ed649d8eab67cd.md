## Exact Claim

After object and self-story loosen, traditions differ by where the freed attention is allowed to belong: no owner, witnessing Self, or God.

## Claim Units

- The practical movement is loosening attention from objects and ordinary self-story.
- Udana 1.10 makes the sense fields sufficient and denies any locatable you there.
- Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 3.7.23 makes the unseen seer the sole knower and inner Self.
- The Cloud of Unknowing directs thought and will away from creatures toward God through naked intent.
- The model predicts that traditions train a post-negation custody rule, not only a doctrine of self or no-self.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Udana 1.10, Bahiya Sutta, Thanissaro translation, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/KN/Ud/ud1_10.html
- Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 3.7.23, https://www.brhat.in/openlibrary/special/brihadaranyaka-upanishad/3-7-23

Structural observation: The primary contrast is real but narrower than the proposed model. Udana 1.10 uses the same seeing, hearing, sensing, cognizing sequence and then removes locatable self-reference. Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 uses seeing, hearing, perceiving, knowing to identify the sole inner ruler and Self. This supports a strong no-owner versus hidden-seer contrast. The Christian leg is less exact: The Cloud of Unknowing chapter 3 begins with love and intent toward God, rather than first freeing neutral attention and then assigning it. Pseudo-Dionysius further strains the model because Mystical Theology chapter 5 places the Cause beyond both affirmation and negation, so even God as a stable recipient is apophatically unstable. Cloud source: https://sacred-texts.com/chr/cou/cou08.htm. Dionysius source: https://hekhal.org/texts/mystical-theology-dionysius/

Secondary literature gap: I did not find the exact phrase or structure of attention custody across Buddhist no-self, Advaita witness, and Christian apophatic prayer. However, close prior work already distinguishes common mystical experience, culturally shaped interpretation, monistic versus theistic typologies, witness-consciousness, and pure awareness. The originality is therefore a reframing of a known comparison around practice-level ownership language, not a standalone discovery.

Risk of overread: High. The term attention imports modern psychology into texts that speak about clinging, Self, inner ruler, will, love, and God. Bahiya may be about ending clinging rather than leaving attention unowned. Brihadaranyaka may be ontological rather than attentional. The Cloud may intensify desire rather than suspend ownership.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike common-core or constructivist accounts, this idea says the decisive variable is not only whether an experience is the same or differently interpreted, but whether training assigns loosened attention to no owner, the witnessing Self, or God.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dzogchen or Mahamudra luminous selfless awareness, plus Pseudo-Dionysius's claim that the divine Cause is beyond both affirmation and negation.

Why it strains the model: Luminous selfless awareness can look neither ownerless in a simple Bahiya sense, nor owned by an Atman, nor addressed to a devotional recipient. Dionysius also makes the Christian recipient unstable, because the final move refuses both affirmation and denial of God as a graspable object of intent.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence and pushes the idea away from candidate discovery toward extended synthesis.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Practitioners trained in Bahiya-style sense instruction, Advaita self-inquiry, and Christian apophatic prayer should diverge most in ownership and recipient language at the moment ordinary self-reference drops. Then: Interview transcripts should cluster around no one there, witnessing Self, and God or beloved as recipient, even when sensory content and quietness descriptions overlap. Weakens if: Reports cluster only by phenomenological texture, emotional tone, or teacher charisma, with no stable ownership or recipient pattern. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with trained Theravada, Advaita, and apophatic practitioners.
- If: Changing the instruction at the handoff point should change later interpretation more than immediate perceptual content. Then: A practitioner given no-location language, witness language, or God-directed language after similar objectless calm should later describe different belonging or authorization, while reporting similar quiet or contentlessness. Weakens if: The initial phenomenology changes before doctrinal instruction, or the ownership language remains random after instruction. Test surface: Controlled meditation instruction study with post-session interview coding.
- If: Primary manuals should contain explicit or implicit custody rules near passages that loosen ordinary selfing. Then: Texts should pair negation of objects or self-story with instructions such as do not locate a self, know the unseen seer, or lift the heart to God. Weakens if: The relevant manuals loosen objects without any subsequent owner, Self, or recipient language, or use all three interchangeably. Test surface: Close-reading corpus of Theravada, Advaita, Christian apophatic, Dzogchen, and Mahamudra manuals.

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is just the standard experience versus interpretation debate, or say attention is the wrong category and the model misses liberation, ontology, grace, or devotion.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners independently identify a decisive handoff point and report that no-owner, witness, and God-directed instructions change practice understanding.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and secular attention training

Structural translation: De-identification practices loosen ordinary ownership of thoughts, then assign the released stance to no-self mindfulness, observing self, values, therapist relationship, or a higher power.

Would count against it: If de-identification outcomes and reports are unaffected by ownership or recipient framing, the custody structure is probably only a theological gloss.
