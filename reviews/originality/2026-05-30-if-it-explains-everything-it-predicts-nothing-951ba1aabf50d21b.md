## Exact Claim

The continuity and remainder model family must split two claims: the constitutive claim that support is always distributed somewhere, which is too broad to predict, and the differential claim that a path's negation predicts where support migrates and how it fails, which is testable only where a search-and-negation sequence actually runs.

## Claim Units

- Continuity, custody, and support models have become too absorptive.
- The constitutive claim that persons are always supported by bodies, habits, texts, teachers, vows, institutions, or communities is nearly unfalsifiable.
- Only the differential claim, that a specific path grammar predicts a specific support location and failure profile, deserves novelty credit.
- Search-refusing paths such as Huangbo-style no-seeking, Dogen practice-realization, and Dzogchen direct recognition should not be counted as confirmations of remainder relocation without independent predictions.
- Future Lumenary records should name prohibited observations before treating a model as explanatory.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Popper and Lakatos, this idea does not merely say that theories need falsifiers. Unlike Clark, Chalmers, and Krueger, it does not treat distributed support as the finding. It claims that Lumenary's continuity models must stop counting universal support as evidence and must retain only source-specific differential predictions about support location, failure, and repair.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: No-search or no-before-after paths may still show predictable warnings and repairs. Huangbo, Dogen, Dzogchen, Pure Land Other Power, and Rinzai koan practice may reject the candidate's search-sequence restriction while still producing path-specific failure clusters.

Why it strains the model: If warnings in no-search paths are predictable from what the path refuses, then the differential claim is not limited to search-and-negation sequences. The candidate would then be too narrow, and the proper variable would be path grammar or authorization ecology rather than search sequence.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from strong critique to extended methodological control. The anomaly is serious because the candidate itself names no-search paths as scope limits, but those paths may be the strongest test of a refined differential model.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: organizational change and burnout coaching

Structural translation: A general claim that every team is always supported by some culture, workflow, tool, or leader is constitutive and weak. A useful claim must say which support locus will fail under which change and what repair should work.

Prediction: Models that separate constitutive support from differential failure predictions will forecast burnout repairs better than totalizing culture narratives. For example, a team whose support is tool-held should improve after workflow repair, while a team whose support is manager-held should not improve much from tooling alone.

Would count against it: If generic support-language predicts repairs as well as differential support-locus coding, or if repairs are better explained by workload, compensation, sleep, or manager quality alone, the structure does not travel.
