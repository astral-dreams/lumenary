## Exact Claim

Transformative practice begins through layered authorization: empirical affordances make participation possible, institutions select and intensify those affordances, first-break grammar authorizes or forbids agency claims, and handoff density tracks repeated thresholds where capacity is scaffolded, claimed, forbidden, transferred, repaired, or reauthorized.

## Claim Units

- Beginning is not a single event but a layered process.
- Family, crisis, ritual, teacher trust, imitation, community, and bodily practice function as empirical affordances that make participation possible.
- Institutions select which affordances matter and how intensely they are applied.
- A tradition's first-break grammar regulates which agency may be praised, corrected, disowned, suspended, or confirmed.
- Repeated thresholds should be coded for scaffolding, transfer, repair, and reauthorization, not only the initial conversion moment.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Rambo's conversion stages, Lindbeck's doctrine-as-grammar, Pargament's religious agency styles, Lave and Wenger's situated learning, liminality theory, scaffolding theory, Shinran's Other Power grammar, and prior Lumenary entry-claimant work, this idea says beginning should be coded as four separable layers: empirical affordance, institutional selection, normative agency authorization, and repeated handoff density. Its possible contribution is not that beginnings are layered, but that these layers can be split into independent corpora and tested for prediction of warnings, repair instructions, and reauthorization episodes.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Inherited practice and Dogen-style practice-realization, where there may be no discrete beginning, no transfer point, and no allowed separation between method and fulfillment.

Why it strains the model: If a tradition treats practice itself as realization or treats participation as inherited social life rather than a chosen threshold, first-break grammar and handoff density may be analyst-imposed. The model may still code affordances and institutions, but its central authorization claim weakens.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from discovery-level to extension-level. The model needs explicit values for no-threshold, practice-realization, inherited participation, continuous authorization, and analyst-frame-refused.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Entry grammar can be coded from doctrinal or initiation texts independently of pastoral manuals. Then: Blind coders using only entry texts should predict later warning profiles, such as pride warnings in self-effort paths, passivity warnings in grace or Other Power paths, and teacher-dependence warnings in recognition-based paths, above baseline models. Weakens if: Warnings are predicted equally well or better by institutional role, teacher style, social context, or conversion-stage variables, with no added value from agency authorization grammar. Test surface: Split-corpus coding across Zen, Jodo Shinshu, Calvinist, Wesleyan, Dzogchen, Gelug, and Sufi entry materials.
- If: Handoff density is a real variable rather than a retrospective label. Then: Traditions with more repeated transfer or reauthorization points should show more explicit correction episodes, verification rituals, vows, teacher confirmations, or repair scripts than traditions with low handoff density. Weakens if: Correction and repair frequency tracks institution size, textual genre, monastic discipline, or teacher personality instead of handoff density. Test surface: Manual coding of sermons, training manuals, interviews, and failure narratives separated from entry texts.
- If: Institutional selection mediates which empirical affordances count as legitimate entry supports. Then: The same affordance, such as crisis, family inheritance, teacher trust, or bodily discipline, will be praised in one tradition, downgraded in another, and treated as dangerous in a third according to that tradition's entry grammar. Weakens if: Affordance valuation is mostly explained by local culture, class, literacy, recruitment context, or institutional survival needs rather than doctrinal authorization. Test surface: Paired traditions in similar social contexts but different doctrinal entry grammars.
- If: Wrong-claimant failures should be a distinct class of beginner failure. Then: Practitioner interviews should produce failure cases where repair changes who or what is allowed to claim progress, not merely effort level, belief, emotion, or compliance. Weakens if: Teachers describe repairs in ordinary terms: more practice, less practice, clearer instruction, trauma care, or community fit, without a distinct claimant dimension. Test surface: Semi-structured teacher and spiritual-director interviews, coded blind against religious coping, conversion, and pedagogy models.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Clinical psychotherapy intake and supervision

Structural translation: Client change begins through empirical affordances, such as crisis and referral; institutional selection, such as clinic intake and modality; authorization grammar, such as whether insight belongs to client, therapist, alliance, medication, or protocol; and repeated handoffs, such as homework, exposure, relapse repair, and termination.

Prediction: Therapies with different authorization grammars should produce different beginner warnings and repair scripts even for similar symptoms: cognitive therapies should warn against passive insight without practice, psychodynamic therapies against premature technique ownership or enactment, and medication-centered care against misattributing all change to willpower.

Would count against it: Repair scripts and dropout patterns are explained as well by diagnosis, alliance quality, socioeconomic access, clinician skill, and modality intensity, with no independent effect from authorization grammar or handoff density.
