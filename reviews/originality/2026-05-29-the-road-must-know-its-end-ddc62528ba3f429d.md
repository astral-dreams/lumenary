## Exact Claim

A practice is not trustworthy merely because it produces a strong insight. It must also give after-use guidance: whether its result is validated or possession of the result is undermined, and whether the practice should then be retained, set down, or embodied in ordinary conduct. The modern use case is preventing achievement-driven practitioners from turning methods, insights, therapy, meditation, or productivity systems into proof of worth.

## Claim Units

- Completion is not sufficient; a practice needs guidance for its own after-use.
- The proposed structure has two variables: proof relation, validating versus undermining possession, and carrying relation, retained, released, or embodied.
- Hybrid cases such as MN 22, the Heart Sutra, Rinzai koan curriculum, and Dogen's practice-realization expose the weakness of a simple confirm-or-cancel typology.
- The practical target is achievement-contingent self-worth, burnout, compulsive self-improvement, and spiritual identity built from once-helpful tools.
- The After-Use Check asks what to keep, what to set down, and what to live.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 22, upaya, Sells, Smith, Dogen scholarship, and prior Lumenary custody models, this idea's possible contribution is a bounded two-variable after-use rubric that predicts when a practitioner should keep a method, set it down, or let it appear as conduct, especially for achievement-driven people who turn tools into proof of worth.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen's practice-realization identity and Shinran's Other Power.

Why it strains the model: Dogen strains the premise that a road first leads to an end and then receives an after-use instruction. Shinran strains the premise that the practitioner owns the road, trust, or result enough to retain, release, or embody it. These cases may sit outside the grid's conditions of askability.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in originality. The model should first code ownership and temporal completion as scope conditions.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Mature practice traditions vary systematically in after-use guidance. Then: Split-source coding will find distinct markers for retain, release, embody, no-threshold, practice-realization, and no-owner cases, and those markers will predict held-out warnings, verification rules, and repair practices. Weakens if: Coders cannot agree on the categories, or tradition identity, teacher style, institution type, and general agency models predict warnings just as well. Test surface: Inter-rater coding of MN 22, Heart Sutra, Dogen Bendowa or Genjokoan, Tannisho, Rinzai koan curriculum sources, apophatic Christian texts, and Sufi fana or baqa sources.
- If: The After-Use Check helps the intended modern cohort. Then: Achievement-driven participants with high self-worth contingency report less rumination, comparison, and method-identity after using the check, without reduced responsibility. Weakens if: The check increases self-monitoring, shame, avoidance, or tool-performance pressure, or performs no better than generic journaling or rest-and-plan prompts. Test surface: Two-week randomized pilot stratified by perfectionism, burnout, self-worth contingency, and clinical-risk screen.

Questions:
- Is keep, release, or embody an actual distinction you use in guidance, or an analyst vocabulary imposed from outside?
- Can you name concrete cases where carrying a method after insight caused harm, and what repair was given?
- Does this rubric predict warnings or student repairs better than existing language such as upaya, non-attachment, grace, surrender, practice-realization, or ordinary integration?
- For burned-out high achievers, does the After-Use Check reduce worth-pressure, or does it create another task to perform correctly?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is simply upaya, raft teaching, practice-realization, surrender, integration, or ordinary supervision in new language, and that it does not change sequencing, warnings, or repairs.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the two-variable rubric to identify mismatched guidance, prevent method-identity, and predict different repairs for clinging, premature release, hollow insight, and depleted over-management.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Therapy, coaching, productivity systems, and professional training.

Structural translation: A method that produces benefit needs an after-use instruction: continue as a protocol, retire as scaffolding, or translate into ordinary conduct.

Prediction: Manuals for effective training should differ by after-use relation: retained methods require routine and supervision, released scaffolds require stopping rules, and embodied methods require observable conduct markers. High achievers should benefit from explicit after-use boundaries more than from open-ended self-improvement tracking.

Would count against it: Training outcomes are explained by dosage, accountability, teacher quality, and baseline mental health, with no added predictive value from after-use categories.
