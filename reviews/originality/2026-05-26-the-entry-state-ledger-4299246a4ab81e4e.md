## Exact Claim

A path begins when enough entry-state capacity is held somewhere in the person-world field, then a tradition's grammar of beginning disciplines that threshold by reallocating what the practitioner may claim, trust, repeat, fear, and verify. The real comparative unit is the entry-state ledger: which capacities are required before conscious practice can begin, where those capacities are held, and which grammar disciplines them after the threshold.

## Claim Units

- Entry requires threshold capacities before conscious practice can begin.
- Those capacities may be distributed across practitioner, teacher, community, text, ritual, crisis, vow, institution, or latent awareness.
- Traditions apply a grammar of beginning that selects which threshold factor counts as decisive.
- That grammar is not merely retrospective, it disciplines later practice through warning, trust, repetition, fear, and verification.
- The better unit of comparison is not effort versus gift or first-break alone, but entry capacity locus plus post-threshold grammar.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike conversion-process theory, religious coping theory, upaya pedagogy, situated learning, liminality theory, Lindbeck's doctrine-as-grammar model, Engler's capacity prerequisites, and Shinran's Other Power grammar, this idea claims that path entry can be coded as a pre-practice distributed ledger plus a normative beginning grammar, and that this pairing should predict later safeguards, warnings, verification practices, and failure modes better than either entry ecology or doctrine alone.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: No-threshold entry: inherited practice and Dogen's practice-realization grammar, especially Bendowa's claim that beginner practice is already original realization and that there is no beginning in practice.

Why it strains the model: If a path is entered by gradual socialization, inherited belonging, or a practice-realization grammar that refuses a boundary between practice and realization, then no discrete entry-state capacity may be identifiable. The ledger may become a total life-history description rather than a threshold model. It also becomes hard to distinguish grammar from institution, family, habit, and ordinary enculturation.

Confidence effect: This lowers confidence substantially. The model should add no-threshold, inherited-practice, gradual-entry, and analyst-frame-refused values, and it should predict weakly unless a later recommitment, crisis, initiation, vow, or teacher-recognition event creates a second-order threshold.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Entry grammar adds predictive value beyond social ecology. Then: In paired communities with similar diaspora context, demographics, institutional density, and teacher access, Other Power or grace grammars will produce more warnings against self-powered calculation, more trust and receiving language, and different repair instructions than effort-centered traditions. Weakens if: If safeguards and repairs track institution, teacher style, class, ethnicity, or convert versus cradle status better than coded grammar, the ledger loses explanatory force. Test surface: Blind paired-case coding of Jodo Shinshu and Theravada, or evangelical and contemplative Catholic entry materials, in the same region.
- If: The grammar can be coded from early entry texts and used to predict later manual warnings. Then: Coders using only entry texts can predict which later warnings emphasize pride, calculation, despair, antinomianism, passivity, premature certainty, lack of verification, or inadequate effort above chance. Weakens if: If coders cannot predict warnings above chance, or predictions only work when the same texts are used for both coding and testing, the model is circular. Test surface: Split-source design: entry sermons, initiation liturgies, vows, or doctrinal entry passages coded first, later teacher manuals and interviews held out.
- If: Capacity locus and grammar are separable. Then: Within one tradition, sublineages or debates that share institution and culture but differ on grace, effort, vow, sudden awakening, or teacher-mediated recognition will show different threshold safeguards and different named entry failures. Weakens if: If intra-tradition differences do not change safeguards once institution and social setting are held constant, grammar may be secondary rhetoric. Test surface: Augustinian versus Pelagian disputes, jiriki versus tariki debates, gradual versus sudden Chan, or Soto practice-realization versus koan-curriculum entry.
- If: A distributed entry ledger should help explain failed beginnings and dropout. Then: Failed entry cases will show missing or misassigned ledger functions: no trusted authority, weak community holding, unresolved crisis, mismatched language, unsupported memory practice, or forbidden self-claiming in a grammar that rejects self-power. Weakens if: If dropout is better predicted by generic social support, mental health, schedule burden, cost, or leader quality with no additional contribution from ledger variables, the model is mostly renamed sociology. Test surface: Retrospective interviews and prospective intake records for seekers entering meditation centers, churches, mosques, sanghas, Pure Land temples, or initiatory groups.

Questions:
- When a person begins in your tradition, which parts of beginning must already be held by the person, teacher, community, text, ritual, vow, grace, institution, crisis, or latent awareness?
- Does your tradition's account of beginning predict concrete student mistakes and repair instructions, or is that only ordinary teacher judgment?
- Would this ledger change how you screen, welcome, warn, sequence, or verify beginners?
- Does calling entrusting, grace, recognition, inherited belonging, or practice-realization a capacity distort your tradition's own language?
- Can you name one failed beginning that this rubric predicts before you explain the usual lineage repair?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say the model is just conversion studies, upaya, religious coping, catechesis, initiation theory, lineage pedagogy, or common pastoral judgment. They cannot use it to predict failures, and they object that the ledger misnames grace, vow, inherited belonging, or teacher recognition as capacities.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the ledger to distinguish two beginners who look socially similar but require different supports, to name hidden threshold loads, to predict common failures before they occur, and to explain why entry instructions from one path can harm or confuse beginners in another.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Professional apprenticeship and clinical training

Structural translation: A training path begins when enough entry-state capacity is held by student, supervisor, cohort, institution, protocol, case exposure, crisis, licensure structure, and professional identity. The field's grammar of beginning then tells novices what to claim, trust, repeat, fear, and verify.

Prediction: Fields with similar technical content but different beginning grammars will produce different novice safeguards. Medicine, psychotherapy, law, aviation, and software incident response should differ in whether they warn mainly against overconfidence, boundary violation, protocol drift, emotional flooding, improvisation, or failure to escalate, even when entrants have similar ability.

Would count against it: If novice safeguards are explained almost entirely by objective task risk and institutional liability, with no independent effect from professional beginning grammar, the entry-state structure does not travel well.
