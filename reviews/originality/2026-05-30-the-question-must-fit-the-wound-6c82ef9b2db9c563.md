## Exact Claim

A self-negating question is teachable only when it passes three gates: the source permits the question, healthy correction can hold the answer, and the question serves the person's actual wound rather than turning practice into private performance or self-audit.

## Claim Units

- Deep questions are not automatically beneficial.
- A source or path licenses some questions and refuses or transforms others.
- Correction support is part of whether a question can be safely used.
- The person's wound or cohort fit determines whether the question helps or harms.
- The modern target is burnout, loneliness, meaning loss, anxious overinterpretation, and achievement-contingent self-worth among stable reflective practitioners.

The candidate's three gates are coherent, but each gate has strong prior art.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 2, MN 22, upaya, Collingwood, Gadamer, VCE, spiritual bypassing research, and prior Lumenary question-permission records, this idea claims that a self-negating question becomes teachable only when source permission, correction support, and human-wound fit are jointly satisfied and tested against outcomes.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Rinzai koan and huatou practice, where a destabilizing or impossible question can be the authorized medicine, plus cases where ordinary loneliness needs contact rather than a better question.

Why it strains the model: A question that looks malformed or harmful outside the lineage may be precisely the path's method inside a teacher-held container. Conversely, a question that passes textual permission can still be useless when the real wound is isolation, sleep debt, coercive work, trauma, or clinical rumination.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in any simple three-gate checklist. Supports a more complex codebook with question-as-medicine, teacher-held question, no-question frame, clinical routing, ordinary-contact routing, and undecidable.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The three gates add predictive value beyond generic good judgment. Then: Blind coders can identify source-permitted question, correction holder, and non-fit wound from source and context data before seeing warnings, and those labels predict held-out repair instructions better than tradition label, support-holder-only, or VCE-factor baselines. Weakens if: Coders cannot agree, need outcome passages to infer the gates, or the model adds no prediction beyond teacher style, social ecology, clinical risk, sleep loss, or ordinary support. Test surface: Split-source inter-rater coding across SN 22.59, MN 2, MN 22, Huangbo, Bendowa, Tannisho or Shinran letters, Dzogchen direct introduction, and Rinzai koan sources.
- If: Question-fit screening helps stable over-auditors more than another attainment review. Then: The Question Fit Check reduces proof-seeking, self-surveillance, shame, and withdrawal, and increases ordinary action and willingness to receive correction more than attainment journaling. Weakens if: Ordinary rest, conversation, behavioral activation, or social-support prompts work as well or better, or the check becomes another ritualized audit. Test surface: Four-arm pilot with screened stable practitioners: question-fit check, ordinary journaling, rest-and-plan, and social-support prompt.
- If: Different path grammars and wounds produce different failure clusters. Then: Importing a proof question into no-distance or Other Power settings predicts self-grading and defensiveness, while importing a no-question stance into investigation or repair settings predicts avoidance and missed accountability. Weakens if: Failure clusters are similar across path types, or are better explained by trauma history, isolation, coercion, unsafe teachers, sleep loss, or clinical risk. Test surface: Practitioner interviews coded for concrete failed-student cases, wrong-question import, wound, repair, and outcome.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: clinical intake and education

Structural translation: A powerful question or assessment task is helpful only when the method permits it, a competent holder can correct misreadings, and it fits the learner or patient's actual problem.

Prediction: Students or clients given a fit-gated reflection prompt will show more concrete next actions and less rumination than those given an abstractly deep prompt, especially among perfectionistic high performers.

Would count against it: If outcomes are fully explained by facilitator skill, relationship quality, workload, sleep, or baseline anxiety, and question-source-wound coding adds no measurable prediction.
