## Exact Claim

A silence after self-questioning practice becomes usable evidence only when the later report can be corrected by something the practitioner accepts, such as teacher, text, vow, community, clinician, or visible conduct.

## Claim Units

- Silence after practice is not proof of Self, no-self, or attainment by itself.
- The later story must be overridable by an accepted correction process before it is trusted.
- The key question is not only what remained, but who or what may overrule the returned interpretation.
- This is aimed at isolated practitioners who turn private silence into identity, certainty, doubt, or despair.
- Correction can also harm when the corrector is abusive, incompetent, or clinically mismatched.

Ignatian rule 8 is an especially close Christian primary neighbor: even a high consolation requires later opinions to be examined before full credit or action. The candidate's correction threshold is structurally old.

Secondary literature gap: Read Proudfoot on religious experience attribution, Sharf on experience and Buddhist modernism, Katz and Taves on interpretation, Asad and Lindbeck on tradition and grammar, Alston on doxastic practice and defeaters, VCE literature on appraisal and support, spiritual direction ethics, abuse literature, and clinical material on meditation-related adverse effects.

Risk of overread: High. The model can smuggle modern accountability into traditions that claim immediate recognition. It can also romanticize correction despite abusive teachers, doctrinal policing, dependency, scrupulosity, paranoia, and clinical crises. The human fit is loneliness and meaning loss among stable solo practitioners, not acute distress or destabilizing meditation effects.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Ignatian discernment, Jamesian fruits, Proudfoot, Sharf, Asad, Lindbeck, Alston, VCE safety work, and prior Lumenary correction-holder records, this idea says that post-self-questioning silence should not count as usable evidence unless a named accepted corrector can overrule the returned interpretation. The narrowing is useful, but the structure is mostly renamed prior art.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Valid solitude or direct recognition with stable ethical fruit, plus cases where external correction is abusive, shaming, or dependency-producing.

Why it strains the model: If some practitioners integrate silence well without a named external corrector, or if correction often makes them more dependent and ashamed, then correctability cannot be the threshold for evidential use. It becomes one possible safeguard among others.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and practice confidence. The model survives only as a routing heuristic for isolated, stable practitioners, not as a general spiritual law.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Correction holder is an independently codable variable, not just a restatement of doctrine. Then: Blind coders can identify teacher, text, vow, community, clinician, conduct, or no-corrector from one source layer and predict held-out warnings or repairs better than tradition label alone. Weakens if: Coders cannot identify correctors without outcome passages, or tradition label explains warnings and repairs equally well. Test surface: Split-source primary-text and manual coding across Upanishadic, Theravada, Zen, Tibetan, Christian, and Sufi sources.
- If: Naming an accepted corrector helps the target cohort. Then: Stable solo practitioners who use the Correction Partner Note show less identity inflation and fewer certainty-doubt swings than ordinary journaling controls. Weakens if: The practice increases shame, dependency, rumination, isolation, or performs no better than rest and ordinary conversation. Test surface: Two-week low-risk pilot with solo meditators and app users, excluding crisis, psychosis, mania, dissociation, severe depression, and destabilizing meditation effects.
- If: Traditions that claim direct recognition still retain some corrective channel. Then: Advaita, Dzogchen, Zen, and apophatic sources that speak of direct recognition still point to conduct, teacher verification, scripture, vows, lineage, compassion, or ordinary functioning as later constraints. Weakens if: Mature sources explicitly authorize private recognition with no teacher, text, conduct, or communal check, and practitioners show stable integration without such checks. Test surface: Close reading plus practitioner interviews asking for concrete misrecognition and repair cases.
- If: Support routing matters more than the initial label after difficult practice experiences. Then: In meditation-related challenge cases, outcomes improve when appraisal is paired with fitting support, not merely with a spiritual or clinical label. Weakens if: Clinical-risk variables explain outcomes completely, or correction-holder naming adds no value beyond standard triage. Test surface: Retrospective VCE-style case coding and prospective support-routing pilot.

## Practitioner Test

Relevant practitioners:
- Spiritual directors familiar with Ignatian discernment and scrupulosity
- Buddhist meditation teachers who verify or de-emphasize experiences
- Advaita and Dzogchen teachers who teach direct recognition
- Clinicians familiar with meditation-related challenges
- Pastoral counselors and chaplains
- Researchers of religious experience and contemplative science
- Survivors or advocates familiar with abusive spiritual authority

Questions:
- Is this more than standard discernment, teacher verification, fruits testing, religious-experience attribution theory, or clinical triage?
- After a deep quiet or gap, who or what can overrule the practitioner's first label in your setting?
- Can you describe a case where lack of correction caused inflation, despair, or identity fixation?
- Can you describe a case where correction itself caused shame, dependence, abuse, or spiritual bypassing?
- Would this rubric change screening, sequencing, or repair instructions, or merely rename what you already do?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is standard spiritual direction, fruits testing, teacher verification, lineage grammar, or meditation-safety triage, and that the new language adds no prediction.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the correctable-return rule to identify unsupported post-gap overclaim earlier than existing language, and it predicts concrete repair paths for solo practitioners.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Scientific and organizational decision making after private insight

Structural translation: A compelling insight, model, or diagnosis is not evidence until an accepted correction process can revise it.

Prediction: Teams that require investigators to name the review, replication, user evidence, or failure metric that can overrule a cherished insight will show fewer overclaimed conclusions and fewer stalled projects than teams relying on intuitive certainty.

Would count against it: Ordinary peer review, project maturity, or leadership quality explains all improvement, and pre-naming correctors adds no independent value.
