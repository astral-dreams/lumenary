## Exact Claim

Before coding where support is placed in a contemplative path, first determine whether the path permits a real distance between seeker and sought. In no-distance systems, supports are not bridges to a later prize but guards against rebuilding separation as private certainty, passivity, or isolation; no-distance is not no-support.

## Claim Units

- Some paths treat seeker-sought distance as real enough to cross, while others diagnose that distance as the illness.
- Continuity-ecology and support-location coding should begin with a distance field.
- In no-distance teachings, teacher, vow, posture, community, text, and conduct function as guards rather than bridges.
- Modern solo users may mistake denied metaphysical distance for permission to abandon supports.
- Adding a distance field should improve prediction of warnings, repairs, and failure clusters beyond tradition label or general support coding.

Sources:

Structural observation: Dogen strongly supports the no-distance side because practice and realization are not cleanly sequential. But his no-distance is not free-floating: it is enacted through zazen, forms, rules, teacher lineage, and conduct. Longchen Rabjam also strains any simple no-support reading: direct introduction appears, but sustaining recognition, devotion, vows, guru reliance, and conduct remain. Shinran rejects self-power calculation and places efficacy in Other Power, yet pastoral warning and entrusting are still structured. Huangbo is the hardest pressure because search itself is suspect, but the teaching still occurs inside transmission and teacher-student address. The common pattern is not absence of support but refusal to make support into a self-owned bridge to a prize.

Secondary literature gap: Before promotion, read Dogen scholarship on practice-realization and shusho-itto, Steven Heine and other Dogen scholars, Shinran scholarship on tariki and shinjin, Dzogchen and Mahamudra scholarship on direct introduction and stabilization, Huangbo and Chan studies on sudden enlightenment and anti-seeking rhetoric, non-instrumental ritual theory, MacIntyre on practices, Asad and Lindbeck on tradition and grammar, VCE and Cheetah House safety literature, and spiritual bypassing literature.

Risk of overread: Moderate to high. The model may simply rename established non-instrumental practice, practice-realization, no-attainment, sudden enlightenment, direct recognition, and Other Power. It also risks treating no-distance traditions as one family despite major differences between Dogen, Shinran, Dzogchen, Huangbo, Advaita, and Mahamudra. Human-condition fit is plausible but must stay narrow: stable self-directed seekers using no-distance language to justify withdrawal, passivity, meaning-loss, or status detachment. It is not for acute loneliness without support, severe depression, mania, psychosis, dissociation, trauma activation, addiction withdrawal, OCD or scrupulosity, unsafe authority, or cases requiring moral repair or clinical care.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Dogen, Shinran, Longchenpa, Huangbo, MacIntyre, VCE, spiritual-bypass literature, and prior Lumenary no-support and question-permission records, the separable claim is that a pre-registered seeker-sought distance field should improve prediction of held-out warning and repair language, especially for modern solo misuse of no-distance teachings.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Huangbo's search-refusal, Rinzai or huatou great doubt, direct-recognition traditions, and gradual paths that also use strong support.

Why it strains the model: Huangbo may reject the analyst's attempt to classify support as guard or bridge. Huatou uses a question or doubt as medicine, so distance-making can itself be the practice. Direct recognition may require very specific teacher-held authorization. Gradual paths may share the same warnings and supports, making the distance field redundant.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence. The model should be treated as a coding hypothesis, not a doctrine. It must include no-question, question-as-medicine, direct-recognition, practice-realization, vow-held, bridge, mixed-by-stage, and analyst-frame-refused values.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: No-distance texts use supports as guards of present realization, trust, conduct, or non-separation rather than bridges to later possession. Then: Held-out manuals and teacher interviews from Dogen, Shinran, Dzogchen, Mahamudra, and Chan will warn against private certainty, passivity, calculation, or isolation more than against insufficient effort toward a distant goal. Weakens if: Held-out warning and repair language is indistinguishable from ordinary instrumental path warnings once tradition, teacher quality, practice intensity, and social support are controlled. Test surface: Split-source blind coding of primary texts, manuals, ethical rules, and practitioner interviews.
- If: Modern unguided users of no-distance teachings have distinctive failure clusters. Then: Compared with guided practitioners, solo users more often report passivity disguised as freedom, withdrawal disguised as completion, contempt for practice, or private certainty without conduct repair. Weakens if: Guided and unguided users show the same failure clusters, or ordinary social support and mental-health risk predict outcomes better than no-distance exposure. Test surface: Screened practitioner survey, adverse-event interviews, app-user study, and teacher-reported case coding.
- If: Adding a distance field improves support-ecology prediction. Then: Coders using a distance field predict held-out warnings, repair instructions, and teaching-permission limits better than coders using support-holder fields alone. Weakens if: The distance field has no independent predictive value after adding tradition label, teacher style, institution, social support, and clinical risk. Test surface: Inter-rater coding experiment with pre-registered comparison to support-holder-only and VCE-factor baselines.

## Practitioner Test

Relevant practitioners:
- Soto Zen teachers and Dogen scholars
- Rinzai and Chan teachers
- Dzogchen and Mahamudra teachers
- Jodo Shinshu ministers and scholars
- Advaita teachers familiar with direct recognition and self-inquiry
- contemplative adverse-effects researchers
- spiritual directors and chaplains
- clinicians working with spiritual bypassing and meditation-related distress

Questions:
- In your tradition, are supports better described as bridges to a later result, guards of present realization, expressions of trust, ordinary conduct, or something else?
- Can you give a concrete case where a student used no-distance language to abandon necessary support, and how was it repaired?
- Does the distance field predict warnings and repairs that your existing lineage categories do not already predict?
- Where does this frame distort your tradition, especially in direct recognition, vow-held practice, huatou, or practice-realization?

Answers that reduce originality: Originality falls if practitioners say the bridge versus guard distinction is standard no-attainment, practice-realization, Other Power, or direct-recognition teaching, or if warnings are predicted better by lineage, teacher quality, institution, practice intensity, or clinical risk.

Answers that support contribution: Contribution rises if practitioners can blind-code distance status and if that field predicts concrete repair instructions, warnings, and solo-user risks better than support-holder coding alone.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Education, therapy, and organizational change

Structural translation: Before prescribing supports, determine whether the change model treats the desired state as a later achievement or as a currently enacted relation that must be protected.

Prediction: In ACT, behavioral activation, coaching, and training, models that frame the goal as already enacted through valued action will need guards against passivity and self-certainty, while models that frame the goal as distant skill acquisition will need bridges, scaffolds, and milestones.

Would count against it: If bridge-style supports and guard-style supports do not differ in warnings, failure modes, or repair instructions across nonreligious change domains, the distance field is too thin.
