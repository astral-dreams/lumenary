## Exact Claim

Low translation strain between traditions should not be treated as evidence of independent convergence when historical contact is plausible. In entangled cases, low strain may be a trace of borrowing, shared translators, shared polemic, shared training, or homogenized modern vocabulary, so independence must be established by external provenance before strain is allowed to count.

## Claim Units

- Translation strain measures mapping ease, not independent recurrence.
- The same variables that lower strain, shared lineage, translators, vocabulary, teachers, and polemical contact, can be traces of historical contact.
- In contacted traditions, low strain should lower confidence in independent convergence until provenance is established.
- Gaudapada and Madhyamaka show low strain on non-origination language while retaining high strain around self, Atman, Brahman, and remainder.
- External historical evidence, not strain magnitude, must establish whether a convergence is independent.
- The human-condition teaching warns spiritually unmoored seekers not to use abstract sameness as a substitute for real belonging.

Structural observation: The Gaudapada passages use non-origination and the firebrand image to say that apparent perceiver and perceived arise as appearances in motionless consciousness, with Shankara explicitly reading the unborn mind as Brahman. The Nagarjuna passages reject arising from self, other, both, or no cause, then identify dependent arising with emptiness and reject non-empty entities. The shared anti-origination grammar is real, but the texts authorize different remainders: Gaudapada can support unborn consciousness, while Madhyamaka works against intrinsic nature. The candidate is right that low strain on dialectic can coexist with high strain on remainder, but the claim needs stronger dating, authorship, and transmission work before borrowing is used as the controlling explanation.

Human-condition fit is real but narrow: it can help digitally unmoored seekers and people using perennialist sameness to soothe loneliness, but it may worsen cynicism, isolation, or analysis paralysis.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Smith, Sandmel, Freiberger, historical linguistics, source criticism, and prior Lumenary provenance gates, this candidate gives one sharper operational rule: in traditions with plausible contact, low translation strain should count against independent-convergence confidence until external provenance evidence clears the contact confound.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: A low-strain claim unit appearing in two traditions with strong evidence of no plausible contact, no shared translator, no common imperial or colonial vocabulary, and no later perennialist mediation.

Why it strains the model: If such cases replicate, low strain is not only a borrowing fingerprint. It may also mark human cognitive constraints, convergent practice effects, shared phenomenology, or genuinely independent recurrence. The model would need to become partial confound control, not sign inversion.

Confidence effect: Would lower confidence in the strong inversion from 0.76 to about 0.55 and shift status from extended protocol to a bounded caution. It would not revive naive convergence scoring because provenance would still be required.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Low strain is enriched by contact rather than by independent recurrence. Then: A coded corpus of cross-tradition convergence claims will show that low-strain pairs have higher rates of shared lineage, common translators, known polemic, teacher contact, colonial discourse, or modern perennialist vocabulary than high-strain pairs. Weakens if: Low-strain and high-strain pairs show no difference in contact enrichment after controlling for topic, genre, date, and language family. Test surface: Corpus study of comparative religion claims with blind strain coding and separate provenance coding.
- If: The Gaudapada case is a contact-confounded comparison. Then: A close coding of Mandukya Karika chapter 4 against MMK and Yogacara sources will find low-strain units concentrated in Buddhist technical or argumentative forms, while high-strain units cluster around Vedantic remainder, Atman, Brahman, Turiya, or consciousness. Weakens if: Low and high strain units do not track the proposed contact and remainder pattern, or the same non-origination structure is better explained by earlier Upanishadic sources alone. Test surface: Primary-text codebook with Sanskrit term mapping, dating notes, and independent specialist review.
- If: Provenance must be coded before strain has evidential weight. Then: When coders apply provenance-first coding before strain scoring, some apparent convergence claims will be downgraded or relabeled as shared source, borrowing, translator artifact, or shared milieu. Weakens if: Provenance-first coding almost never changes convergence scores compared with strain-only scoring. Test surface: A/B scoring test using the same claim units, one group strain-only, one group provenance-first.
- If: Low-strain sameness comforts a specific modern wound but can mislead belonging. Then: In a small pilot with stable spiritually eclectic readers, the Borrowed-Likeness Check reduces all-paths-same certainty and increases one concrete community or tradition-contact action without increasing loneliness or cynicism. Weakens if: The practice increases distrust, isolation, contempt, rumination, or reduces real belonging compared with a simple source-humility prompt. Test surface: Two-week randomized pilot measuring false-harmony endorsement, cynicism, loneliness, rumination, and real contact actions.
- If: Borrowed agreements and independent agreements behave differently under translation. Then: Borrowed or translator-mediated parallels will preserve unusually similar vocabulary, sequence, or metaphors across languages, while independent parallels will show more functional similarity with lower lexical and rhetorical alignment. Weakens if: Borrowed and independent cases show the same lexical, rhetorical, and role-similarity profiles. Test surface: Comparative text analysis with known-borrowing controls, no-contact controls, and modern translation controls.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Historical linguistics and cultural phylogenetics

Structural translation: Translation strain corresponds to surface resemblance. Provenance corresponds to cognacy, borrowing, areal contact, shared source, or chance. The candidate predicts that apparent closeness cannot be used as independent-origin evidence until contact pathways are modeled.

Prediction: In datasets of cultural traits, myths, or doctrinal phrases, high surface similarity among geographically or institutionally connected communities will more often trace to borrowing, shared source, or areal convergence than to independent recurrence. Independent recurrence will require systematic correspondence across held-out features, not only easy mapping of the focal claim.

Would count against it: If surface similarity reliably predicts independent recurrence after known contact routes are controlled, or if contact modeling adds no predictive value beyond structural similarity, the cross-domain analogy weakens.
