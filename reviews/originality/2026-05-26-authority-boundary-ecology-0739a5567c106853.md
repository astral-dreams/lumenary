## Exact Claim

At the edge of contemplative practice, comparative analysis should first establish each tradition's native authority-boundary: who recognizes maturity, what evidence counts, who may teach, what must be released, and how realization returns to ordinary conduct. Only after that should reflexive operations such as confirmation, cancellation, dissolution, and identity be coded and tested for predictive value against authorization, transmission, teaching strategy, and ethical re-entry.

## Claim Units

- The decisive unit near contemplative completion is a native authority-boundary, not doctrine, method type, or final experience alone.
- Authority-boundaries include maturity recognition, evidential rules, teaching permission, release requirements, and ordinary-conduct re-entry.
- Reflexive operations such as confirmation, cancellation, dissolution, and identity must be coded only after native boundaries are established.
- If reflexive operations predict authorization, transmission, teaching strategy, and ethical re-entry better than tradition label or metaphysical content, they become a comparative variable.
- If they do not predict those outcomes, they remain descriptive vocabulary inside a broader authority ecology.

Structural observation: The primary texts support boundary-first reading, but they also reduce novelty. Dogen strains a completion-boundary model because practice and realization are not cleanly sequential, realization is expressed through ongoing practice and verification by all things. Teresa repeatedly subordinates extraordinary experience to discernment, obedience, humility, Christological memory, and conduct, so authority is distributed through confessor, church, fruits, and suspicion of private certainty. The Tannisho relocates decisive efficacy away from self-owned practice and teacherly possession into Other Power, while still policing divergence through lineage memory and warnings against self-calculation. MN 10 gives a practice grammar of ardency, mindfulness, clear comprehension, hindrance work, arising and passing, and relinquishment, but it does not by itself establish later institutional teaching authorization. The close read supports the ecology claim, yet the need to separate realization, recognition, authorization, and conduct is already visible in the sources.

Secondary literature gap: The audit found strong near-neighbors in Asad's discursive tradition, Weber's sociology of religious authority, Lindbeck's doctrine-as-grammar, Sharf's critique of experience as an authorization category, Katz's contextualism, Catherine Bell's ritualization and power theory, Foucault's technologies of the self, Hadot's spiritual exercises, Lave and Wenger's communities of practice, Hori's Rinzai koan curriculum, and VCE-style contemplative science factor coding. It did not fully read all monographs, original Japanese, Spanish, Pali, Arabic, or Tibetan terms, and it did not interview teachers or institutional historians. This limits confidence.

Risk of overread: High. A boundary can be retrospectively constructed wherever a tradition has teachers, texts, warnings, or institutions. The model risks becoming unfalsifiable unless the boundary is coded from a held-out source before predicting transmission, warning, and repair patterns. Some traditions reject the analyst's boundary frame, especially Dogen-style practice-realization, radical Other Power, inherited religion, charismatic antinomian movements, lay devotional continuity, and traditions where realization is private but teaching authorization is institutional or political.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Asad, Lindbeck, Weber, Sharf, Katz, Bell, Foucault, Hadot, Hori, VCE, and prior Lumenary ledgers, the surviving distinct claim is a split-source predictive procedure: first code native authority-boundaries from one corpus, then code reflexive operations, then test whether those operations predict transmission, teaching permission, warnings, ethical re-entry, and repair patterns better than tradition identity, metaphysical content, social ecology, institution type, and teacher style. The broader claim that maturity is governed by authority, grammar, practice, institution, and conduct is not original.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen's practice-realization identity, inherited religious continuity, and radical Other Power cases.

Why it strains the model: If practice and realization are not two moments, or if authorization is continuous rather than a boundary event, the phrase 'at the edge of contemplative practice' misleads. Dogen can make the whole practice field the realization, not a completion threshold. Inherited religion and lay devotion can mature through continuity, community, and repetition without a distinct arrival point. Tannisho-style Other Power can reject the idea that the practitioner or teacher owns the decisive boundary.

Confidence effect: Reduces confidence in a boundary-centered version. The model should add no-boundary, continuous authorization, practice-realization, inherited continuity, radical Other Power, no-authorization-frame, and analyst-frame-refused values before treating boundary ecology as universal.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Native authority-boundary variables are more than descriptive. Then: Coders who only see entry, completion, and authorization passages should predict held-out warnings, teaching permissions, and ethical re-entry requirements above chance and above tradition-label baselines. Weakens if: Held-out warnings and permissions are predicted equally or better by broad tradition identity, institution type, teacher style, or metaphysical doctrine alone. Test surface: Split-source blind coding across Zen, Advaita, Dzogchen, Theravada, Teresa-style Christian contemplation, Sufism, Pure Land, and modern secular mindfulness.
- If: Reflexive operations add value after native boundaries are coded. Then: Texts coded as method-identity should show different authorization and repair patterns from texts coded as method-cancellation or method-dissolution, even within the same broad tradition family. Weakens if: Reflexive-operation labels fail inter-rater reliability or add no predictive value beyond authority-boundary variables. Test surface: Compare Soto practice-realization, Rinzai koan testing, Madhyamaka emptiness pedagogy, Advaita self-knowledge, and Teresa's discernment rules.
- If: Authority ecology governs ethical re-entry. Then: Traditions where maturity evidence is conduct-centered should show stronger ordinary-life verification rules and stronger warnings against private experience claims than traditions where maturity evidence is teacher-recognition-centered. Weakens if: Conduct warnings and private-experience suspicion occur independently of coded authority ecology, or mostly track social control intensity. Test surface: Manual sections on visions, powers, teaching permission, vows, precepts, humility, compassion, service, and teacher recognition.
- If: Boundary-first comparison reduces false convergence. Then: Pairs that look similar phenomenologically, such as nondual awareness reports, will diverge in authorization, who may teach, required safeguards, and what must be released. Weakens if: Phenomenological similarity alone predicts authorization and re-entry patterns as well as boundary coding. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological reports paired with manuals, lineage protocols, and teacher interviews.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Professional clinical training and psychotherapy supervision.

Structural translation: A therapeutic school has native authority-boundaries: who may treat, what counts as competence, what evidence licenses independence, what techniques must be released or retained, and how insight returns to ethical client care.

Prediction: If the structure is real, supervision manuals and credentialing rules should predict therapy-school-specific repair patterns and boundary violations better than method labels alone. Psychoanalytic, CBT, somatic, and family-systems traditions should differ not only by technique but by who may authorize competence, what evidence counts, and how practitioners are corrected after overreach.

Would count against it: If supervision failures and repairs are predicted just as well by generic legal risk, client population, clinician experience, or institution policy, with no added value from native authority-boundary coding, the comparative structure loses cross-domain force.
