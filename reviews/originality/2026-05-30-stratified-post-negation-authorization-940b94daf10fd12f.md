## Exact Claim

After de-identification or objectless-awareness practice, the decisive comparative unit is not simply the remainder after negation, but the authorization mode by which a passage, teacher, model, or practitioner is permitted to rest, investigate, deny, bracket, teach, or refuse a post-negation seeming. This authorization must be coded by textual stratum and claim level, and the rubric is only useful if it can fail and if practitioner-facing counsel reduces distress without increasing inflation, dissociation, nihilistic deflation, or rumination.

## Claim Units

- Post-negation comparison should track authorization mode, not only residual content.
- Authorization modes include recognition, inference, assertion, recursive investigation, denial, bracketing, teaching permission, and refusal.
- The same tradition must be stratified by source layer: early text, commentary, teacher instruction, later doctrinal system, neuroscience redescription, and practitioner interpretation.
- The rubric needs an explicit falsification rule, such as nondiscriminating overfit where all modes fit all passages.
- The human-condition use case is identity distress, meaning loss, spiritual inflation, nihilistic deflation, and overinterpretation after quiet or objectless states.
- Practice counsel should be tested by outcomes, including distress, rumination, dissociation, inflation, deflation, and ordinary return.

The candidate's useful move is to ask what authorizes the next step after negation: Upanishadic disclosure, Advaitic self-luminosity, Buddhist disidentification, Madhyamaka refusal of theses, or modern brain-model redescription. The danger is that this is already a standard hermeneutic demand in historical-critical, contextualist, and doctrine-as-grammar work.

Secondary literature gap: Before raising novelty, compare directly with Steven Katz on contextualist mystical experience, Robert Sharf on experience as rhetorically and institutionally authorized, George Lindbeck on doctrine as grammar, Talal Asad on discursive tradition, Madhyamaka non-positioning, Advaita work on svaprakasha and adhyasa, and contemplative-science papers that separate levels of explanation in neuroscience translation. Risk of overread: High. If every stance after negation is called an authorization mode, the rubric may become a meta-label for ordinary source criticism and lose predictive bite. It can also flatten living traditions by treating teacher authority, textual genre, philosophical inference, direct recognition, and clinical safety as commensurable slots. Human-condition overreach is likely if used with dissociation, psychosis, mania, severe depression, OCD or scrupulosity, trauma activation, addiction withdrawal, or medical blackout concerns.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Katz, Sharf, Lindbeck, Asad, Madhyamaka non-positioning, Advaita self-luminosity debates, VCE support routing, and prior Lumenary inference-policy records, this candidate says post-negation counsel and comparison should be coded by the same three controls: authorization mode, source stratum, and observed practitioner effect. That is a useful synthesis and protocol refinement, not a standalone discovery yet.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Madhyamaka and direct-recognition cases where the mature instruction refuses both a positive remainder and a neutral bracket, or where a qualified teacher authorizes recognition without the analyst being able to separate recognition, assertion, and pedagogical performance.

Why it strains the model: If a text or practice intentionally collapses the distinction between denial, refusal, and recognition, the authorization-mode grid may impose categories the source rejects. Dogen practice-realization, Dzogchen pointing-out, Mahamudra direct introduction, and some Madhyamaka sources may make authorization inseparable from performance and relation, not a codeable stance after an event.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate discovery to novel_synthesis or extended. The model remains promising only if it marks analyst-frame refusal and undecidable cases rather than forcing classification.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Post-negation authorization mode is a real comparative variable rather than a relabeling of tradition identity. Then: Blind coders using source-stratum passages can predict held-out warnings, repair instructions, or teacher counsel better than coders using tradition label, generic doctrine, or neuroscience mechanism alone. Weakens if: Inter-rater agreement is poor, all major passages require multiple equally plausible modes, or tradition label predicts warnings and repairs as well as the authorization rubric. Test surface: Split-source coding study across Brihadaranyaka, Shankara, SN 22.59, SN 22.95, Udana 1.10, MMK 18.6, Dogen, Dzogchen or Mahamudra pointing-out manuals, and contemporary mindfulness adverse-event guidance.

Test surface: Close-reading trial with Sanskrit-aware readers coding Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23, 4.3.23, Adhyasa Bhashya, Upadesa Sahasri, and Brahma Sutra Bhashya passages independently.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Clinical and philosophical use of brain models for identity distress

Structural translation: Replace contemplative post-negation with post-diagnostic or post-mechanistic interpretation: after a brain, trauma, personality, or predictive-processing explanation, a person still needs a rule for what the explanation authorizes them to conclude about agency, guilt, worth, responsibility, or change.

Prediction: Authorization-stratified explanations will reduce overclaiming in mental-health and neuroscience communication: people will distinguish mechanism, identity verdict, moral verdict, and care action more reliably than after mechanism-only explanations.

Would count against it: If audiences already separate mechanism from identity verdict without prompting, or if the authorization frame increases confusion, fatalism, self-surveillance, or avoidance of responsibility, the structure does not travel.
