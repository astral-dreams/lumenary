## Exact Claim

A borrowed spiritual sentence needs two different tests: public doctrine needs provenance, authority, correction, and source-regeneration, while a lonely or unmoored seeker should first test whether the sentence produces honest conduct, real contact, and willingness to be corrected.

## Claim Units

- Borrowed spiritual language has a doctrine gate and a seeker-care gate.
- Public claims require source provenance, practice context, authority, verification, and correction.
- For lonely or unmoored seekers, source criticism should not be the first test if it becomes isolation or checking.
- Present fruit means conduct, real contact, and willingness to receive correction.
- Personal use and public teaching are different acts with different safeguards.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Freiberger, Smith, Panikkar, lived religion, Pargament, spiritual-bypassing work, appropriation studies, reassurance-checking research, and prior Lumenary routing records, this idea's narrow contribution is a two-gate rule: provenance first for public doctrine, contact and correction first for stable lonely seekers using a borrowed sentence privately.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: An authorized hybrid community, such as a disciplined Christian-Buddhist, Hindu-Buddhist, recovery, or interspiritual lineage, may regenerate borrowed language through shared practice, correction, and authority without clean single-source provenance.

Why it strains the model: It creates a third carrier case. The sentence is neither merely source-pure doctrine nor merely private seeker comfort; the shared life of the hybrid community may be the source that now carries and corrects it.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the binary two-gate model. The model should add a hybrid-carrier category before public use.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: For public doctrine, provenance-first coding should improve accuracy over contact-first coding. Then: Expert coders using provenance, authority, practice aim, verification, and correction fields should catch more false convergences and inappropriate borrowings than coders who only assess present usefulness. Weakens if: If provenance fields do not improve expert agreement or error detection, the doctrine gate is weaker than claimed. Test surface: Blind coding of borrowed religious sentences by comparative religion scholars, source-community practitioners, and comparative theologians.
- If: For the target cohort, contact-first practice should help more than source-first checking. Then: Stable spiritually eclectic participants assigned to one real contact before posting should show fewer identity-display posts, less rumination, and more real conversations than those assigned to full provenance review first. Weakens if: If provenance-first review reduces loneliness and rumination as well or better, or contact-first increases harm, the routing rule fails. Test surface: Four-arm pilot: contact-first, provenance-first, ordinary journaling, and wait-before-posting.
- If: Source checking becomes risky when it functions like reassurance seeking. Then: Participants with high reassurance-seeking, OCD, or scrupulosity indicators should show more checking loops and less confidence after repeated source reviews. Weakens if: If repeated source review does not interact with reassurance-seeking risk, the clinical-adjacent caution is overdrawn. Test surface: Screened non-clinical pilot with exclusion and referral rules, plus clinician review.
- If: Hybrid communities need a third carrier category. Then: Communities with explicit correction holders, safety rules, and stable practice forms should handle borrowed language better than both solo eclectic users and purely provenance-only reviewers. Weakens if: If hybrid communities cannot protect against misuse or appropriation better than simple provenance review, the third category is unnecessary. Test surface: Case comparison of authorized hybrid lineages and mixed communities, using concrete misuse and repair cases.
