## Exact Claim

A spiritual path requires not only more or less practitioner capacity, but directionally specific capacities. Receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration should be coded as vector roles with magnitude, direction, locus, stage, safeguards, and failure mode. A path can lower or relocate a load only through a directionally compatible capacity or an explicit reorientation practice. The deeper generator of direction may be the path's account of self, liberation, and what remains after transformation.

## Claim Units

- Capacity is vector-valued, not scalar.
- The recurring roles are receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration.
- Each role must be coded for magnitude, direction, locus, stage, safeguards, and failure mode.
- Load can move from practitioner to teacher, vow, community, text, practice, or divine source only if a compatible capacity or reorientation is present.
- The vector directions may be generated by whole-path soteriology rather than by local practice technique alone.
- The model should predict cross-training resistance, student failure modes, and bridging practices better than scalar prerequisite models or broad agency-locus typologies.

- Tannisho, sections VII to IX, translated by Taitetsu Unno, https://www.themathesontrust.org/papers/fareasternreligions/Tannisho-Unno.pdf

Structural observation: SN 45.8 reads like a high-agency ledger: right view knows suffering and cessation, right resolve is directed toward renunciation and harmlessness, right effort generates desire and persistence, right mindfulness maintains ardent attention, and right concentration stabilizes the path. Tannisho VIII reverses the ownership grammar: nembutsu is not the practitioner's practice or good act because it is Other Power and free of self-power. Tannisho IX treats lack of joy not as simple incapacity but as blind passion already anticipated by the vow. The pair supports the vector insight because similar path functions are assigned different direction and locus. It also threatens the model because calling entrusting a capacity may already distort Shinran's grammar.

Secondary literature gap: The audit searched Pargament, Engler, Hadot, Ferrer, Katz, Sharf, Dahl, Lutz, Davidson, Lindahl, Britton, upaya literature, Tannisho, Bendowa, and SN 45.8. That gap blocks any candidate-discovery claim.

Risk of overread: High. The ledger may turn tradition-specific soteriology into analyst-owned cells. Pure Land entrusting, Dogen practice-realization, and Dzogchen recognition may not be practitioner capacities at all, even when they have observable preconditions.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Pargament, Engler, Dahl, Hadot, Ferrer, Katz, Sharf, upaya theory, and Shinran, this idea claims that path requirements can be coded as a reusable vector ledger where each functional role has magnitude, direction, locus, stage, safeguards, and failure mode, and that whole-path soteriology may generate the direction of each role. Its possible contribution is predictive rubric design, not the discovery that traditions differ in agency, capacity, or context.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen's Bendowa practice-realization, especially the claim that training and enlightenment are not two, https://www.abuddhistlibrary.com/Buddhism/C%20-%20Zen/Ancestors/Dogen/Bendowa/Bendowa.htm

Why it strains the model: If practice and realization are one, then load-transfer language can create the separation Dogen rejects. Zazen is not simply practitioner agency carrying a burden, nor divine or vow relocation. It is enactment of realization. The ledger can handle this only by adding values such as no-transfer, no-boundary, and analyst-frame-refused. Those values may save accuracy but weaken explanatory compression.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the model as a universal explanatory structure. It remains useful as a comparative heuristic, but the anomaly blocks promotion above novel_synthesis until Dogen, Pure Land, Dzogchen, and Christian grace cases are coded without distortion.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Capacity direction is generated by soteriology rather than only by practice technique. Then: Independent coders should infer later manual warnings and safeguards from early claims about self, liberation, and final transformation at above-chance rates. For example, Other Power texts should warn against calculation, practice-realization texts should warn against treating practice as a means to a later goal, and deconstructive paths should warn against reification and dissociation. Weakens if: Scalar maturity, broad tradition labels, or Pargament-style agency locus predict the same warnings as well as the vector ledger, or coders cannot agree on direction and locus. Test surface: Blind coding trial across SN 45.8, Tannisho, Bendowa, one Dzogchen or Mahamudra manual, one Christian grace manual, and one Sufi fana or baqa source.
- If: Reorientation cost is real and not just lack of general maturity. Then: Practitioners shifting from effort-heavy practice to surrender, vow, grace, or recognition practice should report specific misfires such as calculation, over-efforting, ownership of insight, or refusal of support. Teachers should prescribe bridging practices that change direction, not merely more practice time. Weakens if: Experienced practitioners switch smoothly when motivation and discipline are high, with no direction-specific friction, or teachers describe failures only as intensity, trauma, poor ethics, or generic immaturity. Test surface: Structured interviews with dual-trained practitioners in Theravada and Pure Land, Advaita and bhakti, Zen and Dzogchen or Mahamudra, plus teacher reports of student switching failures.
- If: Vector mismatch predicts adverse practice patterns better than broad practice category alone. Then: Adverse-practice reports should cluster by mismatch type: agency-heavy striving in surrender paths, deconstructive inquiry without integration capacity, devotional dependence without ethical agency, or nondual recognition without memory and stabilization supports. Weakens if: Practice intensity, psychiatric history, retreat setting, sleep, teacher quality, or social context explain the reports without residual value from vector direction and support locus. Test surface: Recode VCE-style meditation challenge reports and practitioner case reports with vector fields, then compare predictive value against existing practice, practitioner, relationship, and context factors.
- If: Gift and recognition traditions still have observable conditions, but those conditions will not be best described as practitioner-owned capacities. Then: Jodo Shinshu, Dzogchen, Mahamudra, and Christian grace teachers should accept some functional preconditions while objecting to capacity ownership language. Their corrections should cluster around vow-held, teacher-held, grace-held, recognition-event, and analyst-frame-refused values. Weakens if: Teachers either accept ordinary capacity language without reservation, or reject all functional precondition coding while still showing no predictable teaching safeguards. Test surface: Practitioner-scholar interviews and manual coding of Shinran, pointing-out instructions, and grace texts.

Questions:
- Are receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration separable teaching loads in your tradition, or does that list impose an outside psychology?
- Does directionality predict actual sequencing, warnings, and repair instructions for students, or is it obvious under older terms such as upaya, grace, self-power, Other Power, or ego development?
- When practitioners move between effort, surrender, vow, recognition, and practice-realization paths, do they need a reorientation practice, or only more maturity and guidance?
- Does calling trust, recognition, grace, or practice-realization a capacity distort the tradition's own grammar?
- Can the rubric predict a concrete student failure case before you tell us the lineage's usual repair?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say this is standard upaya, religious coping, contemplative-development theory, spiritual bypassing caution, cultural-linguistic theology, or ordinary lineage pedagogy. They also say the ledger does not change teaching choices or predict failures better than existing teacher judgment.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the ledger to predict failure modes, identify hidden reorientation costs, name support locus more accurately, and avoid common cross-tradition mistranslations that older scalar or agency-locus models miss.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and education

Structural translation: Replace spiritual capacities with learning or therapeutic capacities: attention, agency, trust, memory, reflexivity, and integration. Code each by magnitude, direction, support locus, stage, safeguard, and failure mode. A change-oriented protocol, acceptance-oriented protocol, exposure protocol, relational repair protocol, and skills curriculum should fail differently when the learner has the wrong direction of capacity rather than merely too little capacity.

Prediction: Students or clients with equal baseline ability should show different dropout, alliance rupture, transfer, and relapse patterns depending on whether the method requires self-direction, surrender to process, external scaffolding, memory of rules, reflexive monitoring, or post-session integration. Bridging exercises that reorient the capacity should improve transfer more than simply increasing intensity.

Would count against it: General executive function, motivation, instructor quality, trauma history, or dosage predicts outcomes as well as or better than vector direction, and direction coding has low inter-rater reliability.
