## Exact Claim

After quiet, blankness, or self-loosening, isolated practitioners should not make final private claims about self, no-self, attainment, failure, or worth until the report has met a reliable other, an ordinary duty, and time. The claimed advance is a relational burden rule: when the wound is solitary self-verdict, even a safety audit can become the same wound unless some correction is held by a trustworthy second voice.

## Claim Units

- Quiet or objectless states can become harmful when a lonely person must judge them alone.
- The first question should be who can prevent the state from becoming a worth, attainment, or disappearance verdict.
- Advaita protects an unobjectifiable knower, while early Buddhism refuses consciousness as owner.
- Read as training records, both sources imply private report handling before doctrine.
- The proposed protocol is reliable other, ordinary duty, and time before final interpretation.
- The target wound is loneliness and achievement-contingent self-worth in stable solo practitioners, not all contemplative silence.

Structural observation: Brihadaranyaka protects the unseen seer and the deep-sleep continuity of seeing, while SN 22.59 and SN 22.95 deny ownership of consciousness and train repeated examination of aggregates. Neither primary pair alone proves the need for a second voice. SN 22.95 even strains the model by telling the monk to make himself his own refuge. The second-voice structure is much closer to Ignatian discernment, where interior movements are delayed, examined, and often disclosed to a director or competent spiritual person, https://spiritualexercises.neocities.org/.

Secondary literature gap: The exact safety claim needs comparison with Ignatian spiritual direction, VCE meditation-challenge support routing, Cheetah House adverse-effect resources, OCD and scrupulosity reassurance-accommodation literature, spiritual-abuse research, chaplaincy and pastoral-care ethics, and peer support models. The unresolved empirical question is whether one trusted voice reduces verdict urgency beyond ordinary rest, journaling, conversation, or bounded clinical routing.

Risk of overread: Very high if universalized. Direct-recognition lineages, trained solitary practitioners, and SN 22.95-style self-reliance can make a mandatory second voice look like overcorrection. In OCD, scrupulosity, dependency, unsafe authority, high-control groups, or spiritual-abuse contexts, the second voice can become reassurance, coercion, shame, or control. For isolated people with no safe helper, the protocol may fail exactly where it claims relevance.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Ignatian discernment, VCE support routing, ERP anti-reassurance guidance, Cheetah House safety resources, Buddhist anti-ownership practice, and prior Lumenary gate-holder records, this idea says the first post-silence burden for lonely overinterpreters should be carried through one trustworthy voice, one ordinary duty, and time. That is a clear consolidation, not a standalone discovery.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Active OCD or scrupulosity, unsafe authority, and mature direct-recognition or solitary practice.

Why it strains the model: In OCD and scrupulosity, a trusted person can become a reassurance object. In abusive communities, a second voice can become control. In direct-recognition lineages or stable solitary practice, a second voice may be unnecessary or distorting. SN 22.95's own-refuge language is also a textual anomaly against a blanket relational rule.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and practice confidence. The protocol should be treated as a bounded safety-routing option for stable lonely or achievement-contingent overinterpreters with a safe helper, not as doctrine about silence.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: For stable lonely or achievement-contingent overinterpreters, a safe second voice reduces private verdict loops better than solo checking. Then: Compared with solo journaling, participants using One Trusted Voice report lower identity-verdict urgency, less shame, less inflation or nihilistic deflation, more ordinary action, and no rise in dependency. Weakens if: Rest, casual conversation, or ordinary journaling performs as well, or the trusted-voice arm increases shame, rumination, approval hunger, or dependency. Test surface: Four-arm pilot after quiet-state reports: solo journaling, no-verdict plus ordinary care, ordinary rest or conversation, and bounded trusted-voice routing.
- If: The helper must be bounded and anti-reassurance. Then: A helper protocol that validates distress, refuses repeated certainty-seeking, and returns the person to ordinary care outperforms unstructured reassurance. Weakens if: Repeated reassurance performs as well, or bounded support increases anxiety, shame, or relational control. Test surface: Pilot stratified by reassurance seeking, intolerance of uncertainty, loneliness, and contingencies of self-worth.
- If: Relational correction is a pattern in mature handling of powerful states, even when the metaphysics differs. Then: Held-out manuals and teacher interviews regularly place private reports inside teacher, text, community, conduct, time, or clinical correction channels. Weakens if: Mature sources reliably authorize private silence as sufficient warrant with stable ethical fruit, or support-holder coding cannot be distinguished from general institution or teacher style. Test surface: Split-source coding of Advaita, Theravada, Dzogchen, Mahamudra, Ignatian, and secular mindfulness materials.
- If: Human-condition benefit is cohort-specific. Then: Benefits are strongest for stable isolated, perfectionistic, or achievement-contingent practitioners and absent or harmful for active OCD, scrupulosity, dissociation, mania, severe depression, trauma activation, addiction withdrawal, unsafe authority, or medical blackout concerns. Weakens if: Effects are not moderated by loneliness, self-worth contingency, or checking risk, or adverse effects cluster in the intended cohort. Test surface: Screened two-week pilot with loneliness, rumination, self-worth contingency, reassurance seeking, adverse meditation-effect, and ordinary-action measures.
