## Exact Claim

Clocks count coordinated transitions but do not contain the lived holding of memory, attention, and expectation. The healthy present is a disciplined width that carries past and future without collapsing into rumination, thin-now mindfulness, or future dread.

## Claim Units

- Clock time is operational counting, not the full meaning of time.
- A clock reading becomes legible only within a held structure of remembered past, attended present, and intended future.
- Physics can coordinate events and relations but cannot replace lived temporal participation.
- The felt present is neither mere illusion nor the only real thing, it is where memory, care, and intention become actionable.
- The modern use case is anxiety, rumination, future dread, burnout, and achievement-contingent self-worth.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Augustine, Bergson, Husserl, Heidegger, balanced time perspective, and relational time physics, this idea's possible distinct contribution is a narrow practice hypothesis: explicitly train a present that carries memory and intention, then test whether it reduces both rumination and future dread better than present-only mindfulness, balanced time perspective coaching, or ordinary planning.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Observer-independent clocks and nonhuman timing systems, such as cesium clocks, pulsars, GPS satellites, automated trading systems, and biological clocks.

Why it strains the model: They count transitions, coordinate records, and guide action without a conscious holder of past, present, and future. This does not refute the phenomenology of legibility for persons, but it does refute any strong claim that physical clocks require human temporal holding in order to measure.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence sharply for the physics claim and keeps the idea as a renamed or applied extension, not a candidate discovery.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: A held-width practice adds value beyond present-only mindfulness and ordinary time-perspective psychoeducation. Then: In a two-week pilot with stable adults under deadline pressure, the practice reduces both rumination and future anxiety without increasing planning compulsion, dissociation, or self-surveillance. Weakens if: No advantage over balanced time perspective coaching, ACT-style values planning, or present-only mindfulness, or any increase in anxiety, dissociation, scrupulosity, or avoidance. Test surface: Randomized pilot with rumination, future anxiety, distress, missed-obligation, and adverse-effect measures.
- If: The clock-phenomenology distinction has pedagogical value. Then: Students taught Einstein-style clock coordination plus Bergson-Augustine duration will be less likely to conclude that relativity proves the felt present is simply unreal, while retaining correct physics answers. Weakens if: Students make the same illusion claim at the same rate, or physics accuracy drops because phenomenology confuses the operational account. Test surface: Pre-post physics teaching experiment with misconception coding and accuracy checks.
- If: Temporal-width practices form a real family across traditions and not just analyst vocabulary. Then: Examen, recollection, narrative confession, and review practices will show different warnings and repairs under stress than pure-present or no-thought practices, even after controlling for teacher style and clinical risk. Weakens if: Warnings and repairs are better predicted by institution, teacher style, intensity, and participant mental health than by temporal-width coding. Test surface: Split-source manual coding plus practitioner interviews across Christian, Buddhist, Jewish, Sufi, and secular practices.
