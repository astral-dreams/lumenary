## Exact Claim

After self-inquiry or contemplative practice, the urgent question is not neutral evidence. It is part of the practice situation. A question should be answered, set down, held by another, or turned into ordinary conduct according to the path's authorized question grammar and the practitioner's actual wound.

## Claim Units

- Post-practice questions are actions inside a practice ecology, not neutral windows into truth.
- Some paths authorize disciplined investigation; some refuse seeking; some use impossible questions as medicine; some route interpretation through trust, vow, teacher, or ordinary conduct.
- The wrong question can create the very distance it then tries to cross, especially for achievement-driven solo practitioners.
- Before coding residue, continuity, or authority after self-negation, Lumenary should code whether the question should be answered at all.
- The practice fit is narrow: stable self-directed practitioners with anxious self-monitoring, not clinical crisis, avoidance, or situations needing direct repair.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 2, upaya, Collingwood, Gadamer, Huangbo, Dogen, VCE, spiritual bypassing research, and prior Lumenary question-fit records, this idea claims that post-practice self-questions must be routed before they are answered because the question itself can function as authorized inquiry, medicine, avoidance, self-grading, or ordinary-conduct return depending on path grammar and wound.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Rinzai koan and Chan huatou practice, plus ethical repair and clinical treatment contexts.

Why it strains the model: Koan and huatou sources show that an impossible, urgent, identity-touching question can be the medicine rather than the problem. Ethical repair and therapy show the opposite risk: refusing a difficult question can become avoidance or harm concealment. A simple 'do not answer' rule fails both ways unless the routing categories are specific and teacher or clinician held when needed.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality confidence and publishability. The anomaly does not reject the model, but forces it into a narrow routing protocol with explicit question-as-medicine, accountability, and clinical-routing categories.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: psychotherapy, coaching, and education

Structural translation: The first intervention is not always answering the client's or student's question. The question may be data, avoidance, a task-framing error, a useful exposure cue, or a request that belongs with another helper.

Prediction: Question-routing before answering will reduce rumination and misdirected work among perfectionistic students, therapy clients, and founders compared with direct-answer coaching, especially when the question is about global identity or worth rather than the next concrete action.

Would count against it: If direct answering, ordinary Socratic questioning, standard CBT case formulation, or simple action planning works as well or better, or if routing mainly increases paralysis and reassurance seeking, the structure does not travel.
