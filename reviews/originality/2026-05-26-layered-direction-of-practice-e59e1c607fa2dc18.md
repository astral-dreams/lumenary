## Exact Claim

Direction in contemplative training is distributed across layers rather than located only in capacity grain or custody handoff. The model should code lexical definition, bodily routine, affective posture, attentional operation, teacher authorization, community interpretation, and post-insight integration before explaining transfer, mismatch, or harm.

## Claim Units

- Direction in contemplative training has no single fixed location.
- Grain and custody are not rival master explanations, they are layers in a wider map.
- Direction can enter through lexical definition, bodily routine, affective posture, attentional operation, teacher authorization, community interpretation, and post-insight integration.
- Cross-trained practitioners may experience confusion, flatness, reification, dissociation, or misplaced effort when methods from different traditions are combined without tracking where direction enters.
- A pre-registered layer rubric plus micro-phenomenological interviews could test whether prior practice direction predicts felt resistance, warnings, repairs, and outcomes.

Structural observation: The primary texts support distribution of direction, but they also reduce novelty. MN 10 does not present bare neutral attention, it gives frames, ardency, hindrance work, aggregate analysis, arising, and disappearance. Vivekachudamani directs the seeker from the start through discrimination, dispassion, teacher relation, and self-knowledge. Tannisho relocates decisive efficacy away from self-owned practice into entrusting and Other Power, even calling nembutsu non-practice and non-good from the practicer's side. Longchenpa's Trekcho instruction moves through preliminary deconstruction, teacher introduction, recognition of rigpa, and sustaining without effort or clinging. This close read supports the model's layer claim, but the structures are already visible in the sources themselves.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Dunne, Lutz, Lindbeck, Foucault, Hadot, Bourdieu, Pargament, VCE, Sharf, Katz, and prior Lumenary ledgers, the surviving distinct claim is a split-source predictive rule: code where practice direction enters before reading outcomes, then test whether layer mismatch predicts warnings, repairs, transfer resistance, and adverse effects better than technique, tradition label, social ecology, teacher style, and clinical risk. The general idea that practice has layered direction is not original.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Bahiya's immediate release, Tannisho's non-practice Other Power, and Dogen's practice-realization collapse the model's tendency to locate direction in analyzable layers.

Why it strains the model: Bahiya receives a brief instruction and is said to be released immediately, so textual coding cannot show whether direction came from prior training, readiness, teacher authority, or the instruction itself. Tannisho denies self-owned practice calculation and assigns decisive efficacy to Other Power, so calling nembutsu a directed practice can distort the source. Dogen-style practice-realization refuses a clean sequence of practice first and result later, so layer location may be an analyst imposition.

Confidence effect: This lowers confidence from candidate discovery to extended. The model remains useful as a coding heuristic only if it includes analyst-frame-refused, no-threshold, practice-realization, radical Other Power, and undecidable values.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: If direction layers have real explanatory force, split-source blind coders should infer warning and repair profiles from entry or instruction texts before reading manuals or interviews. Then: Layer scores should predict held-out warnings and repairs above baselines using tradition name, technique family, teacher style, social ecology, Pargament agency style, Lutz matrix dimensions, and VCE factors. Weakens if: Low inter-rater reliability, no predictive lift, or equally strong predictions from simpler baselines would weaken the model. Test surface: Six-case corpus: MN 10, Vivekachudamani, Tannisho, Bendowa or Genjokoan, a Dzogchen or Mahamudra pointing-out manual, and The Cloud of Unknowing.
- If: If cross-training difficulty comes partly from layer mismatch, micro-phenomenological interviews should reveal prior training signatures before participants give doctrinal explanations. Then: Blind raters should identify prior practice families above chance from descriptions of felt resistance, effort, object-orientation, surrender, teacher relation, and integration difficulty during a new practice. Weakens if: If raters cannot infer prior training, or if failures track trauma history, intensity, social belonging, or teacher relationship better than direction layers, the model weakens. Test surface: Petitmengin-style interviews with serious dual-trained practitioners during practice transitions.
- If: If framing and authorization are independent layers, the same attentional operation should produce different outcomes when embedded in different lexical, devotional, teacher, or community frames. Then: Matched instructions with identical attentional operations but different stated aim, authority, and integration rule should yield different reports of agency, effort, trust, self-reification, and repair needs. Weakens if: No measurable difference between differently framed versions, after controlling for expectancy and teacher effects, would weaken the layer claim. Test surface: Ethically low-risk short-practice experiments with experienced practitioners, not vulnerable beginners.
- If: If layer mismatch matters clinically, adverse-effect reports should cluster by mismatch type and repair type. Then: Attentional-operation mismatch should predict flatness or over-efforting repairs; custody or support-locus mismatch should predict guilt, claimant confusion, or devotional repair; community or authorization mismatch should predict trust and belonging repairs. Weakens if: If VCE factors alone explain adverse reports, or if repair instructions do not differ by layer, layered direction adds little. Test surface: Re-code VCE-style adverse reports and teacher case notes with a pre-registered rubric.
