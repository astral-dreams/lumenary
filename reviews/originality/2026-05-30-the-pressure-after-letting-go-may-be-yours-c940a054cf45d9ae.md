## Exact Claim

The demand to find what remains after letting go may be produced less by a tradition or path than by the practitioner's pre-practice control habit, especially achievement-contingent self-worth, so Lumenary's remainder-pressure rubrics may be coding a population artifact as if it were a stable contemplative structure.

## Claim Units

- Remainder pressure may be person-produced rather than path-produced.
- The primary texts most often cited do not clearly depict remainder pressure as a practice stage.
- Modern solo, achievement-oriented, control-seeking practitioners may over-report the pressure.
- Comparative rubrics that sort traditions by authorized remainder may be mis-specified if pressure occurrence tracks practitioner traits.
- The practice implication is to study the urgency before answering the question of what remains.

The candidate is strongest where it converts this textual absence into an empirical trait-versus-tradition test. It is weakest where MN 2 and self-inquiry already show that a question or method can generate the very pressure being discussed.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 2, spiritual materialism, spiritual bypassing, VCE, perfectionism research, and prior Lumenary question-fit records, this idea's separable claim is a dependent-variable test: post-negation remainder pressure should be predicted more by pre-practice achievement-contingency, control-seeking, intolerance of uncertainty, and need for closure than by tradition label once method, teacher, and context are controlled.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Rinzai koan and huatou great doubt, plus Advaita self-inquiry and Dzogchen or Mahamudra direct-recognition settings.

Why it strains the model: If pressure is intentionally generated by huatou or great-doubt practice across temperaments, or if direct-recognition lineages show distinct post-recognition pressures independent of achievement traits, the person-relative model is too narrow. Advaita self-inquiry also strains the model because the method itself asks attention to turn toward the I-source, which can create or disclose pressure by design.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and generality. The idea remains useful as a trait-control hypothesis, not as a replacement for tradition-linked or method-linked rubrics.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Remainder pressure is mainly a person-level artifact in the target cohort. Then: Achievement-contingent self-worth, perfectionism, intolerance of uncertainty, need for closure, and cross-domain control-seeking predict pressure intensity better than tradition label after controlling for practice type, teacher contact, retreat intensity, and clinical risk. Weakens if: Tradition or method explains most variance while person-level traits add little or no predictive value. Test surface: Preregistered survey and interview study across Advaita self-inquiry, Theravada not-self analysis, Dzogchen or Mahamudra direct recognition, Zen koan practice, and secular mindfulness users.
- If: The same practitioner carries a stable pressure style across methods. Then: In within-subject method-switch sessions, people high in achievement-contingency report urgent post-session naming pressure across multiple practices, with only modest method variation. Weakens if: The same person shows sharply different pressure profiles by method, such as strong pressure after self-inquiry, little after aggregate analysis, and different pressure after koan practice. Test surface: Within-subject crossover with blind coding of post-practice reports.
- If: Remainder pressure is more prominent in modern reporting ecologies than in the primary texts used to justify the frontier. Then: Primary manuals and early texts more often describe release, recognition, wrong question, or trained doubt than an ordinary stage of anxious remainder-hunting, while modern solo reports show the anxious remainder pattern. Weakens if: Primary manuals across multiple traditions explicitly describe urgent remainder-hunting as a common stage and give tradition-specific repairs. Test surface: Split-source corpus coding of primary texts, manuals, teacher instructions, and modern practitioner reports.
- If: The proposed practice helps only the narrow achievement-contingent cohort. Then: Weigh the Urge lowers immediate result-pressure and increases ordinary action in screened stable over-achievers more than ordinary journaling, but not in low-control practitioners or clinical-risk groups. Weakens if: It performs no better than rest, conversation, or journaling, or increases rumination, shame, self-surveillance, or derealization. Test surface: Randomized micro-pilot with safety screening and 24-hour follow-up.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Work completion, therapy termination, and unresolved decision-making.

Structural translation: The same person who demands a spiritual remainder after letting go should demand a final verdict after a project handoff, a therapy session, a difficult conversation, or an ambiguous decision.

Prediction: Post-practice remainder pressure will correlate with cross-domain closure pressure, such as compulsive rechecking, inability to end a task, or a need to extract a final lesson from every event.

Would count against it: If remainder pressure appears only after contemplative practice and does not correlate with non-spiritual closure pressure, the person-structure account weakens and method or tradition accounts gain weight.
