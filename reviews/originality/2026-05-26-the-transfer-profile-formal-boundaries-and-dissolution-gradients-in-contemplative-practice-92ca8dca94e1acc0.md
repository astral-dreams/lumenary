## Exact Claim

Contemplative effort and gift should be coded as a two-track transfer profile: formal agency markers and phenomenological dissolution gradients. The highest risk comes after partial relinquishment, when remaining self-claiming reappropriates egolessness, surrender, recognition, or attainment.

## Claim Units

- Formal transfer markers identify instructions, permissions, and prohibitions that change agency grammar at a stage or threshold.
- Phenomenological dissolution is uneven across view, affective conceit, habit, narrative identity, and social credential.
- Sharp formal boundaries can coexist with long lived afterwork, as in shinjin or recognition of rigpa.
- Gradual paths can still focus safeguards around agency grammar changes.
- A usable comparative rubric should code function, marker, stage interval, claimant layer, support holder, credited source, forbidden claimant, gradient width, stalling point, safeguard, and repair.
- The most dangerous interval is not initial relinquishment but the aftermath of partial dissolution, where the practitioner may own the surrender itself.
- Traditions should show specific safeguard clusters around these vulnerable intervals rather than only generic humility warnings.

Tannisho gives the opposite formal marker: entrusting, non-calculation, and Amida's working, while warning against claiming nembutsu as one's own practice. Cross-reading these texts supports the candidate's two-track architecture, but also shows that much of the architecture is already available in primary sources.

Secondary literature gap: The audit found strong near-neighbors but did not fully close-read Steve Bein, Taitetsu Unno, Pargament, Lindbeck, Trungpa, VCE coding materials, Shinran's Kyogyoshinsho, Rennyo letters, or Tibetan manuals beyond the cited Longchen Rabjam text. Risk of overread: High. The claimant-layer vocabulary is strongly shaped by Theravada categories and may distort Shinran, Dzogchen, Soto Zen, and traditions that reject agency accounting. The warning-cluster prediction also risks circular coding unless safeguards are coded from a separate corpus.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Khemaka, Shinran, Dzogchen instructions, Dogen, Pargament, Bein, Trungpa, VCE, and prior Lumenary ledgers, this idea says the comparative unit is a two-track transfer profile and predicts that safeguards cluster where formal agency grammar changes while partial dissolution leaves layer-specific reappropriation risks.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen-style practice-realization and inherited no-threshold practice cases.

Why it strains the model: If practice and realization are inseparable, with no boundary in realization and no beginning in practice, then formal transfer marker may be an analyst imposition. In inherited religious practice, there may also be no initial relinquishment event for the model to locate.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in universality. The model needs no-boundary, no-threshold, practice-realization, inherited-practice, and analyst-frame-refused values before public use.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Formal agency grammar changes predict where manuals place specific warnings and repairs. Then: Blind coders can identify agency markers from entry or stage passages, then predict warning clusters in separate manuals better than chance and better than generic humility themes. Weakens if: Warnings distribute evenly, track only intensity or teacher authority, or cannot be predicted from formal markers. Test surface: Split-corpus manual coding across Theravada, Jodo Shinshu, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Soto Zen, Christian grace, and Sufi fana or baqa sources.
- If: Partial dissolution produces layer-specific reappropriation patterns. Then: Practitioner interviews distinguish view-release, affective conceit, habit, narrative identity, and social credential layers, and each layer predicts different forms of owning the insight. Weakens if: Reports do not separate layers, or reappropriation is fully explained by ordinary pride, teacher feedback, status incentives, trauma, or community role. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with practitioners who report partial awakening, shinjin, direct recognition, surrender, or nondual insight.
- If: The rubric can be coded without importing Theravada categories into every tradition. Then: Independent coders using each tradition's own vocabulary reach acceptable reliability on formal markers, forbidden claimant, support holder, and repair categories. Weakens if: Reliability fails, or coders converge only after replacing indigenous terms with Buddhist fetter or conceit language. Test surface: Inter-rater trial with primary-text excerpts and practitioner review panels.
- If: Repair instructions that explicitly address post-boundary reappropriation outperform generic anti-pride cautions. Then: Students given stage-specific repair language show fewer ownership distortions than students given only general humility, safety, or effort instructions. Weakens if: Stage-specific repair adds no value after teacher relationship, screening, practice dose, group norms, and mental-health risk are controlled. Test surface: Prospective training comparison in low-risk contemplative education settings.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, addiction recovery, and leadership transition.

Structural translation: A formal identity boundary, such as sobriety date, diagnosis acceptance, role transfer, or apology, can occur before psychological self-claiming releases across habit, affect, narrative, and social credential layers.

Prediction: High-risk reappropriation will cluster after the formal change, when the person starts owning being recovered, humble, transformed, delegated, or healed. Programs with explicit post-boundary repair should outperform programs that treat the formal change as the main event.

Would count against it: If relapse, status inflation, or identity rigidity is fully explained by habit strength, incentives, social support, clinical severity, and time since change, with no predictive gain from formal marker plus dissolution-gradient mismatch, the structure weakens.
