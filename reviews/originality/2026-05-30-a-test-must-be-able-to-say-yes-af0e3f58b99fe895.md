## Exact Claim

A convergence test is not a real test if it can only downgrade apparent likeness. Translation-strain analysis should pre-register what would count as a surviving shared role, then permit three verdicts: confirmed partial convergence, false match, or undecidable. A procedure that never raises confidence is measuring its own skepticism rather than convergence.

## Claim Units

- A comparison method must allow more than one verdict.
- The current translation-strain frontier is biased toward downgrade-only gates.
- Before decomposition, analysts should state what a surviving shared role would look like and what would confirm it.
- After decomposition, the method should allow confirmed, false, or undecidable verdicts.
- A method that never upgrades confidence should be renamed as a skepticism protocol, not a convergence test.
- The human-condition target is reflective seekers whose comparison habit produces cynicism, meaning loss, digital comparison, and isolation.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Popper, Bayesian confirmation theory, Mayo, Lakatos, Freiberger, Smith, comparative theology, Millian comparison, and prior Lumenary translation-strain gates, this candidate's specific contribution is a local scoring repair: before decomposing a religious likeness, write the positive shared-role condition and upgrade bar, then require the method to permit confirmed, false, and undecidable verdicts, with provenance coded separately.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Atman and anatta share a de-identification role, but the shared role may be too thin to matter and too historically entangled to count as independent convergence.

Why it strains the model: If the positive verdict confirms only a generic role such as destabilizing ordinary identity, it may inflate trivial similarities. If a stronger role is required, metaphysical incompatibility and historical contact may keep the verdict undecidable. The same issue appears in Gaudapada and Nagarjuna: low strain may reflect borrowing or polemic, not independent support.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the upgrade bar until the method separates thin functional similarity, doctrinal sameness, and provenance. It supports a three-verdict protocol but blocks any claim of strong convergence.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: A pre-registered upgrade condition should produce some confirmed partial convergences in a diverse blind sample, not only downgrades. Then: Across cases such as Atman and anatta, apophatic theology and Buddhist emptiness, compassion practices, ritual confession, and ethical restraint, coders produce a non-zero rate of confirmed partial shared-role verdicts with acceptable agreement. Weakens if: If trained coders still produce almost no confirmations, or confirmations are too thin to predict anything, the method remains a skepticism engine or the upgrade bar is cosmetic. Test surface: Blind inter-rater trial comparing downgrade-scale strain analysis with confirm, expose, undecidable scoring.
- If: The three-verdict scheme should improve decision quality over a downgrade-magnitude scale. Then: Coders using confirmed, false, and undecidable reach higher agreement and make better held-out predictions about translation strain, warning profiles, practitioner transfer limits, and likely misuse. Weakens if: No better agreement or predictive power than ordinary strain scores, or worse false-positive convergence. Test surface: Split-source comparison task with held-out practitioner and manual evidence.
- If: Confirmed partial convergence should predict limited practice intelligibility without implying doctrinal sameness. Then: Practitioners trained in one tradition can recognize a bounded role in another tradition, such as de-identification or repentance, while still naming non-transferable metaphysical and authority differences. Weakens if: If practitioners either reject all shared-role claims as misleading or turn them into broad sameness, the protocol cannot hold its middle verdict. Test surface: Practitioner interviews and blinded scenario classification with Buddhist, Advaita, Christian, Sufi, and comparative theology respondents.
- If: Provenance coding should be orthogonal to positive shared-role scoring. Then: Some confirmed shared roles will be historically dependent, some independent, and some uncertain. Coders can distinguish role survival from borrowing status. Weakens if: If coders cannot separate role similarity from historical influence, every low-strain case becomes ambiguous and the upgrade verdict loses meaning. Test surface: Gaudapada and Nagarjuna, Christian apophasis and Buddhist emptiness, Pure Land and grace, plus no-contact control cases.
