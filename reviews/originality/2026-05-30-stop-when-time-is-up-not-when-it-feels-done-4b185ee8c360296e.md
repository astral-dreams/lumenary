## Exact Claim

Some reflective work has no natural internal end, especially for people prone to compulsive self-examination, perfectionism, or achievement-contingent self-worth. For those cases, completion should be defined before starting by an external limit, such as a fixed time or one bounded action, and the question should close for the day even if it does not feel resolved. A method has surrendered its hold when the practitioner can stop on schedule, not when reflection finally feels complete.

## Claim Units

- Some reflective practices lack a reliable natural terminus.
- For compulsive over-reflectors, felt resolution can be an unreliable and self-perpetuating completion criterion.
- A precommitted external limit, time or one concrete act, should close reflection for the day.
- Continuing past the chosen limit is often avoidance, reassurance seeking, or perfectionism disguised as diligence.
- The rule is contraindicated for under-reflection, acute grief, trauma processing, moral repair, clinical obsessive doubt without support, and non-instrumental practice-realization traditions.

Structural observation: Tractatus 6.54 and MN 22 both picture a method that should not be retained as an object after it has served its function. Neither source gives a modern behavioral stopping rule for a person whose compulsion is to keep checking whether they have really let go. The Heart Sutra complicates discard language because it negates attainment while preserving reliance. Dogen complicates it more sharply because practice may not be an instrument whose completion is externally marked. The candidate's distinct structural claim is not self-canceling method, which is old, but the external terminus for people whose inner completion signal is unreliable.

Secondary literature gap: The strongest near-neighbors are not only spiritual texts but clinical and cognitive stop-rule literatures. The audit needs full reading in mood-as-input studies, ERP manuals, intolerance-of-uncertainty protocols, rumination-focused CBT, metacognitive therapy, implementation intentions, and timeboxing research. It also needs practitioner checks from Zen and other non-instrumental practice contexts.

Risk of overread: High in clinical, grief, trauma, and moral-repair contexts. A timer can become reassurance, ritual, avoidance, or self-punishment. The model can mistake legitimate repeated practice, mourning, confession, discernment, and non-instrumental sitting for compulsive reopening. It should be framed as a triage rule for a specific failure mode, not as a general spiritual law.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike mood-as-input stop-rule research, obsessive-compulsive stopping-criteria studies, ERP, rumination therapies, timeboxing, implementation intentions, WIP limits, and prior Lumenary stopping-rule records, this idea says that self-canceling spiritual instructions fail for over-reflectors unless completion is moved outside felt resolution into a precommitted clock or bounded act. That combination is usable, but the mechanism is mostly already known.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: A reflective person in grief, moral repair, trauma integration, or non-instrumental practice who is harmed by stopping on schedule because the timer becomes avoidance, emotional bypassing, or a new compulsive ritual.

Why it strains the model: The model depends on the problem being over-reflection in search of certainty. In grief, confession, repair, trauma integration, or practice-realization, the needed process may be spacious, relational, recurring, or embodied rather than closed for the day by a timer. A timer may also become the reassurance ritual that ERP would target.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in broad teaching status but preserves the model as a narrow practice-fit rule for screened over-reflectors.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: For compulsive over-reflectors, external limits outperform felt-resolution stopping. Then: A fixed-time or one-act close reduces same-day reopening, rumination, self-worth distress, and redundant journaling compared with reflect-until-resolved instructions. Weakens if: The external limit performs no better, increases anxiety, becomes a ritual, or reduces responsible action. Test surface: Randomized two-week pilot with screened over-reflectors, daily logs, rumination scales, distress ratings, and action-completion measures.
- If: The benefit is moderated by failure mode. Then: High-rumination and high-perfectionism participants benefit more than under-reflectors, grievers, and avoidant participants. Weakens if: The timer helps equally across groups, or mainly helps by ordinary scheduling rather than by reducing compulsive resolution seeking. Test surface: Moderator analysis comparing over-reflection, avoidance, grief, and perfectionism profiles.
- If: The same structure travels to non-introspective over-refinement. Then: People who endlessly edit adequate documents, plans, or designs complete more work with bounded stopping rules, without lower blind quality ratings. Weakens if: Quality drops sharply, avoidance rises, or ordinary deadlines explain all benefits. Test surface: Writing, planning, or design experiment with blind quality ratings and revision-count metrics.
- If: Practice-realization and other non-instrumental contexts are true scope exceptions. Then: Teachers in non-instrumental traditions report that a timed close may be useful for session discipline but does not mark existential completion or method surrender. Weakens if: Such practitioners endorse the timer as a valid completion criterion for the practice's core aim. Test surface: Practitioner interviews with Soto Zen, Dzogchen, liturgical, and devotional teachers.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Software engineering and design review

Structural translation: A review or refactor can lack a natural internal feeling of done. For perfectionistic teams or individuals, completion should be defined by a precommitted timebox, acceptance criterion, or one bounded action, not by the feeling that the design is finally clean.

Prediction: On low-risk tasks, timeboxed review with a written stop rule will reduce churn, duplicate comments, and reopened decisions without reducing defect detection compared with review-until-satisfied.

Would count against it: Defects increase, useful design concerns are suppressed, or ordinary issue scoping and Definition of Done explain all observed benefits.
