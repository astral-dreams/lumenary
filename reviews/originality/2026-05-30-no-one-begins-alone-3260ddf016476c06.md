## Exact Claim

Self-release is risky when a person's steadiness has been held almost entirely by performance and control, unless some ordinary form of care already carries part of life. This support-placement claim should apply only where a path stages a real crossing; no-gap paths such as Dogen-style practice-realization and Huangbo-style no-seeking should be anomaly tests rather than extra boxes in the same table.

## Claim Units

- A person should not be asked to loosen the self that carries them unless some other care already carries part of life.
- Crossing paths require continuity to be held by attention, teacher, vow, community, ritual, body, duty, grace, or another support.
- No-gap paths may make the question of what remains an analyst error.
- People whose steadiness lives mainly in performance and control should be at higher risk during self-release, burnout, grief, or failure.
- The frontier should stop generating support rubrics until one person-level prediction and one no-gap anomaly test are run.

Shinran relocates efficacy to the Vow and forbids calculation by the practicer. The support contrast is real, but the candidate's no-gap boundary may be more doctrinal than practical.

Risk of overread: Very high. The broad claim that support, relationship, body, routine, faith, community, or duty carries life is already covered by extended cognition, secure-base theory, social baseline theory, religious coping, recovery literature, communal practice, and prior Lumenary continuity ecology. Human-condition fit is direct but narrow: stable high performers, caregivers, founders, students, app meditators, and burned-out self-improvers whose worth depends on performance and control. It is not for denied-agency wounds, coercion, spiritual abuse, unsafe authority, severe depression, mania, psychosis, dissociation, addiction withdrawal, acute crisis, lack of real support, or cases needing direct professional care.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike extended mind, religious coping, secure-base theory, AA, VCE, and prior Lumenary continuity records, this idea's only separable contribution is a halt-rule synthesis: test whether pre-release support placement predicts collapse severity in performance-held people, and do not apply carry-across-threshold models to no-gap paths unless those paths themselves predict support-loss failures.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: No-gap traditions may still show the same support-loss failures as staged crossing paths, because Dogen, Huangbo, Dzogchen, and other no-seeking settings still rely on teachers, lineage, vows, rhythms, texts, community, and correction.

Why it strains the model: If no-gap cases fail when teacher, ritual, community, or interpretive support fails, then the no-gap boundary is only doctrinal. The continuity question still applies practically, and the candidate's main distinction collapses back into generic support ecology.

Confidence effect: Lowers status from extended to renamed unless split-source coding shows that no-gap warnings and repairs differ reliably from crossing-path warnings and repairs.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Pre-release support placement is a real person-level vulnerability variable. Then: Among stable high performers, caregivers, founders, or self-directed practitioners, those whose steadiness was mostly performance-held should show more total destabilization during burnout, grief, failure, or intensive self-release than matched people whose steadiness was distributed across body, relationship, routine, duty, faith, teacher, or community. Weakens if: Sleep loss, trauma history, workload, event magnitude, financial precarity, diagnosis, social support quantity, and support quality explain collapse completely, with no added value for support placement. Test surface: Retrospective and prospective interviews with burnout clinicians, chaplains, recovery sponsors, caregivers, founders, and high-achieving professionals.
- If: The no-gap boundary is real rather than analyst convenience. Then: Dogen, Huangbo, and direct-recognition sources should warn mainly against seeking, grasping, staged attainment, and conceptualization, not against losing a carried remainder across a before-after threshold. Weakens if: Held-out manuals and practitioner interviews in no-gap traditions show warning and repair clusters centered on lost teacher, vow, community, ritual, or continuity support, just like staged effort paths. Test surface: Split-source coding of Bendowa, Genjokoan or Uji, Huangbo texts, one Dzogchen or Mahamudra direct-introduction manual, and teacher interviews.
- If: Name What Holds You addresses achievement-contingent over-control better than generic reflection. Then: After four uses, screened participants report lower self-produced steadiness pressure, at least one concrete leaning action, and no rise in helplessness, guilt, passivity, or dependency compared with rest-and-plan, social-support prompt, and ordinary journaling controls. Weakens if: The practice increases guilt, helplessness, self-rating, avoidance, dependency, or performs no better than ordinary rest and asking for help. Test surface: Two-week randomized pilot with stable adults and measures for burnout, perfectionism, shame, help-seeking, ordinary action, loneliness, and adverse effects.
- If: This candidate is distinguishable from prior Lumenary support-holder records. Then: Blind readers can state the added variables: crossing gate, no-gap exclusion, pre-release support placement, and frontier halt rule. Weakens if: Blind readers cannot distinguish this record from What Holds You That You Did Not Build, Continuity Ecology Under Negation, Some Help Should Not Be Managed, or Even No Path Needs Care. Test surface: Blind distinct-content coding with titles removed.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Organizational resilience and software operations

Structural translation: A system held by one heroic performer or one undocumented service should fail harder when that performer or service is unavailable than a system whose continuity is distributed across runbooks, backups, peer knowledge, escalation paths, and health checks.

Prediction: Teams with distributed continuity before disruption will recover faster, show less identity threat, and need fewer emergency heroics than teams held by a single high-control person, after controlling for incident severity and resources.

Would count against it: Incident severity, staffing, funding, manager quality, workload, and documentation explain recovery completely, and pre-disruption continuity distribution adds no measurable prediction.
