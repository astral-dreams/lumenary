## Exact Claim

The bridge between Upanishadic witness language, early Buddhist not-self analysis, and modern consciousness research is remainder pressure: the felt or conceptual demand to posit a final subject after object-like contents have been negated.

## Claim Units

- Self-negation can generate pressure to posit a final subject.
- Traditions manage this pressure differently: ontologize, recursively analyze, bracket phenomenologically, or model it cognitively.
- The pressure is separable from the inference policy used to interpret it.
- Modern self-modeling and meta-awareness research can test the pressure without settling metaphysics.

Structural observation: The texts show divergent management of remainder, but they do not clearly show a felt pressure. Brihadaranyaka supplies a seer-knower through a cosmological and theological sequence. SN 22.59 applies a disciplined not-self template to aggregates. The pressure variable is therefore not directly textual. It is an interpretive bridge from these texts to practice reports and cognitive science.

Secondary literature gap: The search found close components in minimal phenomenal experience, self-model theory, attention and agency factors, pure consciousness debates, and nirodha research. No exact prior phrase remainder pressure appeared, but the mechanisms are not new enough to call a discovery without empirical coding.

Risk of overread: High. The model may psychologize metaphysical claims and may invent a universal pressure that not all practitioners experience.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MPE, pure consciousness, or self-model literature, this idea names the pressure to promote a residual subject as a variable distinct from the tradition's final interpretation of that pressure.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Nirodha samapatti and cessation reports, where practitioners report a discontinuity or absence of consciousness rather than a pressure to posit an enduring witness.

Why it strains the model: If advanced practice can end reported consciousness without producing a subject-remainder pressure, the pressure is not an invariant. It may be tradition-specific, stage-specific, or retrospectively constructed.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate-discovery to novel-synthesis.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Remainder pressure should be detectable in reports before doctrinal interpretation is applied. Then: Practitioners should describe a pull toward phrases such as something remains, knowing remains, no one owns it, or cannot locate the knower before choosing metaphysical language. Weakens if: Reports move directly into doctrine or simple absence without any intermediate pressure category. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews after self-inquiry, vipassana, open monitoring, and cessation-adjacent retreats.
- If: Training style should shape how the pressure is resolved, not whether the pressure can arise at all. Then: Similar reports of content reduction should be resolved as Self, no-self, bracketed awareness, or self-model artifact depending on training context. Weakens if: Training context predicts the entire phenomenology so strongly that no shared pressure stage can be identified. Test surface: Longitudinal study of practitioners cross-trained in Advaita and Buddhist methods.
