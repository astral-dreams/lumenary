## Exact Claim

The effort-gift boundary is not how much the practitioner does, but who is allowed to claim the doing. Each path requires functions such as receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration, while assigning their support to a practitioner, teacher, vow, name, text, community, present act, awareness itself, or later integration, and explicitly forbidding some claimant from owning the function.

## Claim Units

- A path can require real functions while forbidding ordinary self-ownership of those functions.
- The right audit unit is a two-part code: support locus plus forbidden claimant.
- Right effort, Other Power, direct introduction, and practice-realization differ by which functions are required and which owner is disallowed.
- Gift paths should not be translated into hidden self-power, but every path must carry the work somewhere.
- Wrong-claimant failures should predict distinctive practice errors and adverse patterns.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- SN 45.8 Magga-vibhanga Sutta, right effort, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN45_8.html
- Shinran, Letter to Senshin, Other Power, https://shinranworks.com/letters/a-collection-of-letters-zensho-text/what-we-call-other-power/

Structural observation: SN 45.8 makes the practitioner the grammatical agent of desire, persistence, intent, prevention, abandonment, cultivation, and maintenance. Shinran's Letter to Senshin makes settled shinjin incompatible with calculation on the practicer's side, and says no working is true working. This comparison supports the function-claimant split: practice can retain necessary operations while changing who may claim them. But the split is already explicit in the primary materials, especially in Shinran, so the rubric mostly generalizes an old tension rather than discovering it.

Secondary literature gap: The closest structural gap is not self-power versus other-power, which is well known, but the per-function grid of support locus plus prohibited claimant. Still, Pargament's religious coping styles, religious locus of control, Christian monergism and synergism, Sri Vaishnava markata and marjara, Buddhist upaya, Engler's ego-strength thesis, and prior Lumenary capacity-ledger work already occupy much of the architecture.

Risk of overread: High. Claimant language may impose an ownership grammar on traditions that speak in terms of karma, grace, vow, emptiness, devotion, non-contrivance, or conventional truth. The model also risks flattening stage changes: the same action may be self-power before awakening, natural expression after awakening, and conventional compassion in public teaching.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Pargament, religious locus-of-control work, self-power and other-power debates, monergism and synergism, and the local capacity ledger, this idea says that each required practice function should be coded twice: where the function is operationally supported and which claimant the tradition forbids from owning it.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Chinul's sudden awakening and gradual cultivation, plus radical Jodo Shinshu Other Power.

Why it strains the model: Chinul makes the same cultivation necessary after awakening but empty of production, so the claimant status changes by stage. Shinran makes even entrusting wholly Other Power, so calling vow, hearing, or trust a support locus may be an analyst's domestication of grace. These cases strain any clean grid that treats support and claimant as stable cells.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate-discovery range to extended. The rubric remains useful, but only if it becomes stage-sensitive and allows traditions to reject the analyst's ownership vocabulary.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Practice manuals should warn not only about missing capacity, but about a necessary function being claimed by the wrong owner. Then: Pure Land texts warn against self-power calculation, Zen texts warn against gaining idea or contrivance, Advaita texts warn against egoic doership or premature realization, and effort-centered Buddhist texts warn against claiming aggregates as self while still authorizing cultivation. Weakens if: Manual warnings cluster around generic ethics, obedience, humility, or community order, with no recurring relation to wrong-claimant patterns. Test surface: Blind coding of manuals from Jodo Shinshu, Theravada, Soto Zen, Dzogchen, Advaita, and Sri Vaishnava prapatti.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report shifts in permitted first-person grammar when changing methods. Then: Practitioners distinguish felt modes such as I cultivate, I entrust, I recognize, I just sit, or this is not mine, and they can name which phrasing would feel doctrinally or practically wrong in each method. Weakens if: Practitioners report only generic differences in calm, concentration, devotion, or teacher style, with no felt shift in agency or claimant grammar. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with dual-trained practitioners, coded before doctrinal explanation is invited.
- If: Adverse practice events should sometimes cluster around wrong-claimant failures. Then: Gift practice shows self-power anxiety or false surrender, direct-introduction practice shows reification of recognition, practice-realization shows achievement seeking or empty formalism, and effort practice shows pride or mechanical striving. Weakens if: Adverse reports are fully explained by trauma history, retreat intensity, sleep loss, psychiatric vulnerability, poor supervision, or social isolation, with no added value from claimant coding. Test surface: Re-coding VCE-style interviews and teacher case consultations for prior training, current method, claimant language, and failure type.

- Would this rubric change how you sequence practice, diagnose student errors, or warn cross-trained practitioners?

Answers that reduce originality: Teachers say the rubric merely renames standard warnings, or psychologists say it is Pargament and locus-of-control language applied to contemplative texts. Practitioners find claimant language alien or misleading.

Answers that support contribution: Teachers can use the grid to predict previously missed failure modes, especially in cross-training, and can identify function-specific wrong-claimant warnings in primary manuals before seeing the model.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Identity-transforming therapy and recovery programs

Structural translation: Support may be held by client agency, therapist, group, protocol, medicine, higher power, present-session enactment, or integration practice, while some claimant is warned not to take sole credit.

Prediction: Programs with similar behavioral tasks but different claimant rules should produce different failure modes: self-help over-ownership, passive surrender, therapist dependency, medicine-as-savior attribution, or empty compliance with protocol.

Would count against it: Ordinary skills training shows the same claimant safeguards and failure modes, or therapy outcomes are predicted by alliance, dosage, diagnosis, and social support without any added value from claimant coding.
