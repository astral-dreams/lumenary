## Exact Claim

Awakening paths should be compared by where they place five functional loads: receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration. Effort, gift, direct introduction, and practice-realization do not remove capacity requirements, they redistribute them to the practitioner, teacher, vow, community, text, present act, or post-insight discipline.

## Claim Units

- The needed practitioner support is not one generic minimum self, but a five-column ledger: receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration.
- A path can reduce one capacity load only by raising another or relocating the load to a teacher, vow, community, text, or post-insight discipline.
- Effortful Buddhist practice places high load on agency, memory, and reflexivity before insight.
- Other-power practice lowers self-powered agency but raises or relocates receiving, hearing, entrusting, and freedom from calculation.
- Direct-introduction practice lowers production at the threshold but raises recognition, teacher relation, and stabilization.
- The model predicts distinct failure modes when a load is absent, inflated, or placed in the wrong locus.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- SN 45.8 Vibhangasutta, SuttaCentral, https://suttacentral.net/sn45.8/en/bodhi
- Shinran, Letter to Senshin, Collected Works of Shinran, https://shinranworks.com/letters/a-collection-of-letters-zensho-text/what-we-call-other-power/

Structural observation: SN 45.8 strongly supports the agency side of the ledger: right effort requires desire, striving, energy, applied mind, and maintenance across wholesome and unwholesome states. Shinran's Letter to Senshin sharply resists that grammar by denying calculative working on the practicer's side and placing settlement in Other Power. The ledger is useful because it notices the contrast, but the phrase receiving capacity is not simply textual in Shinran. The text more nearly says that the decisive settling is not possessed by the practitioner. Longchen Rabjam's Trekcho instruction, https://www.lotsawahouse.org/tibetan-masters/longchen-rabjam/lama-yangtik-trekcho-instruction, supports the relocation pattern by rejecting contrived effort while still requiring recognition of what has been introduced, undistracted abiding, vows, devotion, renunciation, and post-meditation conduct.

Secondary literature gap: Search found very close prior art in Engler's ego-strength-before-no-self thesis, Buddhist personhood debates, Pudgalavada arguments about karma and liberation, Buddhist functional-self and self-pattern theory, Upaya as capacity-sensitive pedagogy, spiritual bypassing literature, and Lumenary's prior audited idea Every Path Needs What Its Teaching Dissolves. I did not find an exact five-column gift-effort ledger with relocation rules across Pure Land, Dzogchen, right effort, and practice-realization, but the main architecture was already specified in the prior Lumenary audit's next-loop instruction.

Risk of overread: High. The model may smuggle self-power back into Shinran by treating entrusting as a practitioner capacity. It may also psychologize Dzogchen recognition as a personal skill rather than primordial awareness recognizing itself. The safer formulation is that paths require functional support loci, not necessarily capacities owned by the practitioner.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Engler, Upaya, Pudgalavada personhood debates, or the prior Lumenary minimum-self claim, this idea says the comparative unit is a five-field load distribution, and that effort, gift, direct introduction, and practice-realization can be tested by where they relocate receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration. Because this follows directly from an earlier Lumenary audit instruction, it is best treated as an extension.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Radical Jodo Shinshu other-power: true shinjin is not a human capacity at all, but Amida's working, and self-assessment as capacity may itself be self-power calculation.

Why it strains the model: If entrusting is not merely low agency but a denial that the practitioner owns the decisive receiving condition, then the ledger misnames a theological claim as a psychological capacity. A similar Dzogchen objection says recognition is not a practitioner's skill but primordial awareness recognized through introduction.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and confidence. The model survives only if capacity is redefined as functional support locus rather than practitioner-owned competence.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Practice manuals should show patterned safeguards matching their load distribution. Then: Right-effort and gradual-path texts should emphasize intention, repetition, memory, correction, and ethical maintenance. Other-power and direct-introduction texts should not show zero safeguards, but should show safeguards around doubt, false trust, teacher qualification, distraction, antinomian misuse, recognition, and stabilization. Weakens if: Manuals show no relation between path type and safeguard profile, or safeguards track only institution, genre, or teacher personality. Test surface: Coded comparison of SN 45.8, Shinran letters and Tannisho, Longchen Rabjam Trekcho materials, Dogen Bendowa, and contemporary lineage manuals.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report different permitted self-capacities when switching methods. Then: The same practitioner reports that vipassana authorizes effortful observation and memory of instructions, Pure Land authorizes relinquishing calculative self-power, Dzogchen authorizes recognition and non-distraction, and Soto practice-realization authorizes full enactment without future-gaining. Weakens if: Reports are explained better by temperament, teacher style, or retreat setting, with no stable method-specific capacity shift. Test surface: Structured interviews with practitioners trained in at least two of Theravada, Jodo Shinshu, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Soto Zen, Advaita, and Sufism.
- If: Failures should follow the misplaced load, not merely practice intensity. Then: Gift practice distorted into agency produces anxious self-checking or pride. Direct introduction without stabilization produces reification, distraction, or antinomian confidence. Effort practice without memory or reflexivity produces mechanical repetition or bypassing. Weakens if: Failure types do not correlate with load placement, or all traditions show the same generic adverse patterns after controlling for trauma, isolation, sleep, and teacher support. Test surface: Re-coding adverse meditation reports, teacher case notes, and practitioner interviews with ledger fields.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and transformative education

Structural translation: Any method that transforms identity must place functional load somewhere: client agency, trust in therapist or method, memory of practice, reflexive self-observation, group support, or integration after the decisive session.

Prediction: Identity-transforming modalities such as trauma therapy, psychedelic therapy, exposure therapy, Internal Family Systems, and critical pedagogy should differ from ordinary skills training by having stronger trust-building, screening, memory supports, facilitator authority, and integration practices. Low-agency modalities should compensate with higher relational containment and post-session integration.

Would count against it: Identity-transforming methods require no more scaffolding than ordinary skill acquisition, or failures do not track where agency, trust, memory, reflexivity, and integration are placed.
