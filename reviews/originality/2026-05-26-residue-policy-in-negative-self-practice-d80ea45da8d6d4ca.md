## Exact Claim

The precise Advaita-Buddhist comparison should ask what each path permits to remain after negation: Brihadaranyaka preserves an unobjectifiable seer-knower, while SN 22.59 withholds authorization from any aggregate including consciousness.

## Claim Units

- Both traditions unsettle identification with ordinary experience.
- The key comparison unit is residue policy after negation.
- Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 permits an unobjectifiable seer-knower as remainder.
- SN 22.59 applies not-self analysis to consciousness and does not authorize an aggregate remainder.
- The strain is not just self versus no-self, but whether de-objectification licenses residue.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 3.7.23, https://sacred-texts.com/hin/sbe15/sbe15070.htm
- SN 22.59 Anattalakkhana Sutta, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN22_59.html

Structural observation: The contrast is textually real but needs narrowing. Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 is not merely a meditation report after negation. It is part of the antaryamin sequence, where the inner ruler is within cosmic elements, beings, organs, mind, and knowledge. SN 22.59 is not a general ontology of every possible remainder; it is an analysis of the aggregates as unfit for appropriation. The proposed residue policy works if it is limited to the comparison between authorized Self-language and non-appropriation of aggregate consciousness.

Secondary literature gap: Close prior art includes Advaita-Buddhism comparison, Shankara on witnessing consciousness, Buddhist not-self strategy debates, and scholarship on consciousness without content. The specific phrase residue policy was not found, but the structural contrast is well known.

Risk of overread: Moderate to high. The idea risks importing later Advaita sakshin doctrine into an older Upanishadic passage and treating one early Buddhist discourse as all Buddhist self-analysis.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike standard self versus no-self comparisons, this idea proposes a smaller operational field: after ordinary identifications are negated, record whether the tradition authorizes, tests, refuses, or leaves undecided any remainder.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dhammatalks' note to SN 22.59 points to texts about a kind of consciousness outside the aggregate range of all, and Thanissaro argues not-self is strategic rather than a denial of all remainder.

Why it strains the model: If early Buddhist materials allow an unestablished or unobjectified dimension beyond aggregate consciousness, then SN 22.59 cannot be used as simple no-remainder policy. It only blocks aggregate appropriation.

Confidence effect: Reduces confidence and requires the residue field to distinguish aggregate remainder from non-aggregate liberation language.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Practice manuals should diverge at the point where attention tries to identify awareness itself. Then: Advaita instructions should more often say rest as, recognize, or abide as awareness, while vipassana instructions should more often say observe knowing, consciousness, or awareness as arising, passing, empty, or not-self. Weakens if: Manuals across both traditions use the same instruction at that point, or both authorize and de-authorize awareness in similar ways. Test surface: Coding Ramana, Shankara-lineage, Mahasi, Thai Forest, and early sutta practice instructions.
- If: Practitioners trained in different residue policies should differ in post-negation interpretation even when phenomenological reports are similar. Then: After reporting contentless or objectless awareness, Advaita-trained practitioners should more often use Self or awareness-as-ground language, while Buddhist-trained practitioners should resist ontological upgrade. Weakens if: Training tradition does not predict interpretation, or reports do not pass through any recognizable residue question. Test surface: Structured interviews after self-inquiry, vipassana, and open-awareness retreats.
