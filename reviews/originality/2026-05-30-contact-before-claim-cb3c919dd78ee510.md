## Exact Claim

Borrowed spiritual language should be routed by use and wound. If a borrowed sentence is used as doctrine, public authority, evidence, or cross-tradition convergence, it needs provenance, carrier conditions, correction, and regeneration checks. If a stable lonely seeker uses it privately for hope or orientation, the first test should be contact and conduct: bring it to a real person, duty, community, repair, or safe help-seeking before making it an identity claim. Borrowing becomes dangerous when it replaces action, contact, correction, grief work, or help-seeking, not simply because it is borrowed.

## Claim Units

- Doctrine, public authority, evidence, and convergence claims require provenance-first scrutiny.
- Private orientation for a stable lonely seeker may require contact-first testing before full source audit.
- Borrowed spiritual language is not intrinsically harmful; substitution for action, contact, correction, grief work, or help-seeking is the danger signal.
- Contact-first means enacted relation: say it to a real person, live it in a duty, bring it into community, repair harm, or seek help.
- The contact-first branch is bounded by safety exclusions for unsafe relationships, abusive communities, clinical distress, compulsive checking, and public identity use.

Structural observation: James already separates origin from value and gives a fruits-over-roots test for religious life. Ignatius operationalizes a practitioner version: movements are discerned by where they lead, by timing, by desolation safeguards, and by disclosure to a competent spiritual person. Dogen adds a different enacted-test structure: realization is not certified before practice, it occurs in doing and returning to the place of practice. These sources strongly anticipate the contact-and-conduct side. The candidate's distinct move is not conduct-testing itself, but routing: source-first for public claim, contact-first for stable private orientation, then delayed provenance before teaching or identity authority.

Secondary literature gap: Read pastoral discernment and spiritual-direction literature directly, especially Ignatian discernment manuals, contemporary spiritual direction ethics, Buddhist teacher-student guidance, interfaith pastoral counseling, and religious appropriation ethics. Also read Wuthnow on seeking and practice-oriented spirituality, Roof and Bender on spiritual bricolage and lived spirituality, Pargament on religious coping, Turkle on digital conversation, Salkovskis and Rachman on checking and reassurance, social prescribing evidence, behavioral activation for social connectedness, and prior Lumenary records on translation strain, correction holders, and anti-overchecking.

Risk of overread: High if published as a discovery. Conduct-testing, fruits-testing, counsel, disclosure, practice-realization, social connection, and anti-checking are all established. The idea can overread loneliness as a source-confusion problem, or underread appropriation, power, and unsafe community risk. Human-condition fit is clearer than many recent records but narrow: stable lonely or digitally unmoored seekers, people facing meaning loss, feeling out of place, digital comparison, and achievement-contingent spiritual identity. It is not for unsafe relationships, abusive groups, acute grief crisis, OCD or scrupulosity, dissociation, mania, psychosis, severe depression, addiction withdrawal, medical concern, coercive workload, or cases where a teacher, clinician, or safeguarding pathway should hold the next step.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Panikkar, Freiberger, Smith, Sandmel, Lindbeck, Asad, James, Ignatius, Dogen, Wuthnow, Turkle, social prescribing, anti-checking psychology, and recent Lumenary translation-strain records, this candidate's specific contribution is a two-track routing rule: borrowed language used as public doctrine or evidence must pass provenance and regeneration first, while borrowed language used privately by a stable lonely seeker should first be carried into safe contact and conduct so it does not become a substitute for belonging, repair, grief work, or help-seeking.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: A lonely seeker borrows a sentence from an abusive teacher, extremist online group, coercive community, distorted translation, or marginalized tradition, then follows contact-first by contacting the unsafe source or acting from the sentence before provenance and authority checks.

Why it strains the model: The model assumes contact and conduct are often safer than source-checking for the lonely private cohort. Unsafe contact reverses that assumption. In some cases, provenance, safeguarding, appropriation analysis, clinician routing, or teacher-held correction must precede contact. The public-private boundary is also unstable online: a private comfort phrase can become identity branding or advice within minutes.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence and publishability unless the first step is a safety and authority screen, not contact itself. The model should be treated as a bounded routing protocol, not a general teaching.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Stable lonely seekers using borrowed spiritual sentences privately will benefit more from contact-first carry than from provenance-first auditing. Then: Over two and six weeks, a contact-first group shows lower loneliness and rumination, fewer identity-display posts, more real conversations, more help-seeking or repair actions, and no increase in shame compared with provenance-first and ordinary journaling arms. Weakens if: No improvement over ordinary journaling or social-support prompts, increased shame, increased rumination, or increased unsafe contact. Test surface: Four-arm randomized pilot: provenance-first gate, solo carry-first, contact-first carry-first, ordinary journaling plus social-support prompt.
- If: Practitioners will distinguish first gates by use type. Then: Scholars and teachers recommend provenance-first scrutiny for teaching, publishing, doctrine, and convergence claims, but recommend counsel, conduct, relational fruit, or safe community contact first for stable private orientation cases. Weakens if: Practitioners recommend the same gate in both cases, or say the split is just ordinary discernment with no added decision value. Test surface: Blind vignette study with comparative religion scholars, spiritual directors, Buddhist teachers, Advaita teachers, interfaith ministers, and clinicians.
- If: Harmful borrowing is predicted more by substitution behavior than by borrowing itself. Then: Diary and social-media coding shows worse outcomes when borrowed sentences replace action, contact, correction, grief work, or help-seeking, even after controlling for whether the sentence is borrowed. Weakens if: Borrowing itself predicts harm independent of avoidance, identity display, public authority claims, source safety, and mental-health variables. Test surface: Longitudinal diary study with provenance coding, behavior coding, loneliness, rumination, help-seeking, correction, and real-contact measures.
- If: Contact-first carry should preserve later correction rather than harden identity. Then: Participants who first enact the sentence in safe contact are more willing to revise, contextualize, or retire it after a delayed provenance review than participants who first turn it into a profile identity or public teaching. Weakens if: Contact-first increases attachment, appropriation, defensiveness, refusal of correction, or public misuse. Test surface: Two-stage pilot: initial contact-first exercise, delayed provenance and carrier-condition audit, blind coding of revision willingness.
