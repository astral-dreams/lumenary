## Exact Claim

Cross-tradition convergence may come from recurring human interface invariants such as attention, identity, salience, agency, and boundary-making, rather than from discovery of the same hidden metaphysical substance.

## Claim Units

- Independent traditions can converge without sharing a metaphysical object.
- The shared structure is the human interface of attention, identity, salience, agency, and boundaries.
- Witness-consciousness, emptiness, nondual awareness, wu wei, and surrender may report alterations of that interface.
- The model preserves comparative value while blocking naive perennialism.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 3.7.23, Max Muller translation, https://sacred-texts.com/hin/sbe15/sbe15070.htm
- SN 22.59 Anattalakkhana Sutta, Thanissaro Bhikkhu translation, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN22_59.html

Structural observation: The pair supports only part of the model. Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 repeatedly places an inner ruler within earth, beings, organs, mind, and knowledge, then identifies this as Self and immortal. SN 22.59 examines form, feeling, perception, fabrications, and consciousness, then rejects each as mine, self, or what I am because each is inconstant and not masterable. Both disturb ordinary identity and boundary-making, but their final authority structure diverges sharply: one licenses an unobjectifiable Self, the other de-authorizes ownership of all aggregates including consciousness.

Secondary literature gap: Search found strong near-neighbors in constructivist mysticism, Stace and Forman on pure consciousness, enactivism, Metzinger's minimal phenomenal experience work, self-model theory, and neurophenomenology. No source found the exact five-part interface list as a comparative-spirituality rubric, but the structural move is heavily precedented.

Risk of overread: High. The model can silently translate ontological, devotional, ritual, and soteriological claims into cognitive-interface language. It may explain reports, but it does not by itself interpret traditions on their own terms.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Stace, Forman, Katz, Varela, Thompson, Rosch, or Metzinger, this idea proposes a Lumenary comparison unit in which apparent spiritual convergence is scored by recurring changes to attention, identity, salience, agency, and boundary-making, without inferring one shared metaphysical object.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Theistic surrender and grace traditions, for example Christian apophatic prayer or Islamic tawakkul, may treat relation to God as irreducible, not as de-emphasis of agency or boundary-making.

Why it strains the model: If the practitioner says the core invariant is not an altered interface but a real Other who initiates and judges the transformation, the model becomes a reduction rather than an interpretation.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate-discovery range to novel-synthesis range.

Then: Blind coders should reliably tag these interface variables in practitioner reports better than chance, while also preserving doctrinal differences. Weakens if: Reports cluster mainly by tradition-specific doctrine, devotional object, or teacher vocabulary, with weak cross-tradition interface structure. Test surface: Qualitative coding of meditation, prayer, and contemplative-inquiry interviews across Advaita, Theravada, Daoist, Sufi, and Christian sources.
