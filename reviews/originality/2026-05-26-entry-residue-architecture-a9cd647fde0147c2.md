## Exact Claim

Transformative traditions organize the beginning of practice through an interaction between what remains authorized after critique and how entry into transformation is narrated, enacted, or discovered. Residue authorization constrains coherent entry grammars, entry events can disclose what the tradition later names as usable remainder, and downstream safeguards, warnings, effort theories, and verification forms should be predicted by four linked variables: residue authorization, entry grammar, circularity severity, and source layer.

## Claim Units

- Traditions authorize or refuse some remainder after critique, such as self, no-self continuity, grace, vow, teacher recognition, practice-realization, ritual, community, or silence.
- Traditions narrate or enact entry through grammars such as crisis, vow, grace, teacher encounter, inherited participation, gradual socialization, no-threshold practice, or self-disclosure.
- Residue authorization constrains which entry grammars are coherent.
- Entry events can disclose what the tradition later names as usable remainder.
- Circularity severity is separable: some paths require the transformed capacity in order to begin transformation.
- Source layer matters, so canonical doctrine, entry ritual, practitioner report, pedagogy, and later systematization should not be collapsed.
- Downstream safeguards, warnings, effort theories, and verification forms should be predicted by the interaction of the four variables, not by first-break type or residue policy alone.

Structural observation: The primary texts support an interaction field but also reduce originality. Tannisho makes entry depend on entrusting to Amida's vow, relocates decisive efficacy to Other Power, and warns against self-power, teacher ownership, and self-will. SN 22.59 negates ownership of the five aggregates and moves through disenchantment, release, and knowledge of release, without authorizing a hidden self as remainder. Dogen strains the sequence because practice and realization are one, so beginning and fruit cannot be cleanly separated. The Gita complicates the model by mixing action, multiple causes, divine inner rulership, and final surrender, so one text may contain several entry-residue logics by chapter and voice.

Secondary literature gap: This audit checked accessible primary translations, local prior Lumenary audits, and open sources on conversion, rites of passage, doctrine-as-grammar, religious coping, situated learning, Pure Land Other Power, Christian grace, and practice-realization. It did not fully review Rambo's full monograph, Lindbeck's full book, Japanese, Pali, Sanskrit, Tibetan, Latin, or Chinese originals, Shinran scholarship, Dogen scholarship, Sufi bayah and fana sources, Dzogchen pointing-out manuals, or practitioner interviews.

Risk of overread: High. The model can become self-confirming if entry grammar, residue authorization, warning, and verification are coded from the same passage. It can also distort traditions that reject agency accounting by forcing them into an analyst's support and remainder vocabulary.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Rambo, Van Gennep, Turner, Lindbeck, Pargament, Lave and Wenger, VCE, Shinran, Dogen, Orange, and prior Lumenary ledgers, this idea says the key test is additive prediction: independently coded residue authorization and entry grammar should interact to predict held-out safeguards, warnings, effort theories, verification rules, and repairs better than entry-only, residue-only, agency-style, social-ecology, tradition-identity, or source-layer baselines.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen practice-realization and inherited religious participation.

Why it strains the model: Dogen-style practice-realization collapses the ordinary sequence of beginning, method, and fruit. Inherited participation may have no salient first-break event. Both cases strain a model that assumes transformation begins through a narratable threshold and later authorizes a remainder.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence unless the rubric includes no-threshold, no-break, inherited-practice, practice-realization, no-custody-frame, analyst-frame-refused, and undecidable values, and still makes held-out predictions.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: If entry-residue interaction is real, split-source coding of entry texts and residue doctrine should predict later warning clusters better than entry grammar or residue policy alone. Then: Vow-held or grace-held entries should warn against self-credit and effort appropriation. No-self entries should warn against nihilism, dissociation, or reification. Self-inquiry entries should warn against objectifying the witness. Practice-realization entries should warn against instrumentalizing practice. Weakens if: Warnings are predicted equally well by generic tradition identity, teacher style, Pargament agency style, Rambo conversion stage, VCE factors, or social ecology, with no incremental gain from the interaction term. Test surface: Blind-coded corpus with entry passages, separate residue passages, and held-out manuals or practitioner interviews.
- If: Traditions with similar entry forms but different residue policies should develop different verification and repair rules. Then: Teacher-encounter traditions that authorize hidden Self, no-self release, direct recognition, or grace should verify and repair different failures even when the visible entry event looks similar. Weakens if: Similar teacher-encounter forms produce the same warning and repair profile regardless of residue authorization. Test surface: Paired comparison of Advaita self-inquiry, Dzogchen or Mahamudra pointing-out, Zen encounter, and Christian spiritual direction.
- If: Traditions with similar residue authorization but different entry grammar should produce different beginner safeguards. Then: Two traditions that both authorize non-acquired reality should differ if one begins through vow or grace, another through direct recognition, and another through continuous practice-realization. Weakens if: Safeguards track only residue authorization or only social institution, not entry grammar. Test surface: Within-family and cross-family comparisons: Pure Land, Christian grace, Soto Zen, Dzogchen, and Advaita.
- If: Circularity severity should predict auxiliary support structures. Then: High-circularity paths should rely more on vow, grace, teacher authorization, ritual threshold, paradox pedagogy, community holding, or no-threshold practice-realization. Weakens if: Circularity severity does not correlate with auxiliary supports once social ecology and institutional inheritance are controlled. Test surface: Pre-registered coding across at least eight traditions, with inter-rater reliability.
- If: Source layer should change the coding outcome. Then: Later systematization should smooth circularity and harmonize residue tensions more than ritual instructions, practitioner reports, or early canonical texts. Weakens if: Canonical, ritual, practitioner, pedagogical, and later doctrinal layers produce the same codes and the same prediction accuracy. Test surface: Same-tradition corpora split by canonical text, initiation or entry text, manual, practitioner report, and later commentary.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Addiction recovery, therapy, education, and burnout recovery.

Structural translation: Entry grammar becomes the way a person is allowed to begin change before they feel changed. Residue authorization becomes what capacity or support remains trustworthy after self-critique. Circularity severity becomes how much the cure presupposes the cured capacity. Source layer becomes the difference between program doctrine, facilitator practice, participant report, and later theory.

Prediction: Programs for high-shame, addicted, or burned-out cohorts should fail more often when they demand self-owned agency after agency has been discredited, and should do better when initial support is located in sponsor, group, ritual, environment, medication, scaffold, or grace-like trust while later verification gradually returns appropriate responsibility.

Would count against it: Outcomes and repair patterns are explained by alliance quality, socioeconomic support, diagnosis, program intensity, medication, facilitator skill, or peer support, with no incremental predictive value from entry-residue coding.
