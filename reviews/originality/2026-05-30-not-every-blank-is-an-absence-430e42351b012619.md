## Exact Claim

Report-grammar analysis applies only after a prior routing question: does the source treat the gap as opaque and knowable from its edges, or as claimed-transparent with awareness said to span the gap? Conflating these classes creates false convergence.

## Claim Units

- Post-gap report grammar assumes the gap is opaque and evidence appears at the edges.
- Advaita, Yoga, Tibetan sleep-yoga, and some objectless-sleep reports claim awareness within the apparent gap.
- Early Buddhist cessation and not-self sources resist turning gap reports into a witness claim.
- Opacity versus claimed transparency is upstream of clause-level report grammar.
- The teaching fit is discontinuity anxiety, but it must not turn uncertainty into metaphysical reassurance or rumination.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Thompson, Windt, Alcaraz-Sanchez, classical Advaita and Buddhist sources, and prior Lumenary gap-report work, this idea says the first audit question is whether a gap is being treated as opaque or claimed-transparent before report grammar is coded. That is a useful extension, not a discovery.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Objectless-sleep interviews that initially look transparent but, under micro-phenomenological probing, show minimal self, indeterminate location, agency, emotion, or modality-like content.

Why it strains the model: If transparency reports are edge reconstruction or subtly contentful states, the opacity versus transparency split may track doctrinal framing and interview style rather than a real phenomenological class.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and confidence. The model remains useful as a claim-routing rule, but not as evidence that transparent gaps exist.
