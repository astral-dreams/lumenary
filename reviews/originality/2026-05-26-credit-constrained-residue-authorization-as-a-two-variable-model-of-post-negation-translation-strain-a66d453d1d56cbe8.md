## Exact Claim

Translation strain after self-negation is best coded as an interaction between credit distribution, which assigns the power behind transformation, and residue authorization, which says what may be inhabited, refused, left unclaimed, self-resolved, or held in principled silence after ordinary self-identification is unsettled. Credit distribution constrains but does not determine residue authorization, so both variables should be coded separately and tested for incremental predictive value.

## Claim Units

- Credit distribution is an upstream constraint on post-negation residue policy.
- Residue authorization is not fully derivative of credit distribution.
- The residue axis has at least five values: positive authorization, explicit refusal, non-positioning, self-resolving remainder, and undecidable silence.
- Advaita and early Buddhism differ not only by self versus no-self, but by the interaction between credit grammar and post-negation instruction.
- The model should predict practice consequences, warnings, cross-training strain, and translation strain better than either variable alone.

Structural observation: The primary texts support the need for more than a self versus no-self contrast, but they also reduce novelty. Brihadaranyaka 3.7 positively identifies an inner ruler and knower through a cosmological sequence, while SN 22.59 denies fitness for ownership across all aggregates including consciousness. The Gita 3.27 weakens egoic doership by attributing action to nature's gunas, but it does not remove all practical agency from the path. The Tannisho is the hardest case for simple credit coding: it relocates efficacy to Amida, refuses teacher ownership, and calls nembutsu non-practice and non-good for the practitioner. That supports credit distribution as real, but it also shows that source-credit traditions can refuse the very analyst category of practice credit.

Secondary literature gap: The audit used accessible primary translations, local prior Lumenary audits and dialogues, open academic records, and practitioner-facing sources. It did not fully read Pargament's original 1988 article, Sells's full monograph, Katz's original essay, Longchenpa in Tibetan, Shankara in Sanskrit, or practitioner interviews. This prevents any uniqueness claim.

Risk of overread: High. The model can make credit distribution look upstream by definition. Apophatic silence may arise from a theory of predication rather than appropriation risk. Dzogchen and Zen may block the search sequence before residue appears. Pure Land may reject the whole self-owned capacity frame. If the same passages are used to code credit and residue, the model becomes circular.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Apophatic predication refusal, Zen or Huangbo search-refusal, Dogen practice-realization, and radical Other Power in Shinran.

Why it strains the model: These cases may not follow the sequence credit distribution constrains residue authorization. Apophatic silence can be driven by claims about language and divine transcendence. Huangbo-style search refusal blocks the operation that would generate residue. Dogen collapses practice and realization. Shinran relocates efficacy so thoroughly that nembutsu is framed as non-practice for the practitioner. If these are central cases, the model needs at least a third variable: search grammar, predication theory, or practice-realization structure.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and model confidence. The model remains useful as a coding heuristic, but not as a discovery unless it handles these anomaly classes without catch-all bins.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Credit distribution constrains but does not determine residue authorization. Then: Within one credit type, independently coded texts should show more than one residue policy. For example, ground-centered sources should separate Advaita positive authorization, Dzogchen self-resolving remainder, and apophatic undecidable silence. Weakens if: If residue categories collapse into credit categories, or if raters cannot distinguish the residue values without already knowing tradition labels, the second axis is redundant. Test surface: Split-source blind coding of at least six traditions, with inter-rater reliability before any public claim.
- If: The interaction of credit and residue should predict practice consequences better than either variable alone. Then: Held-out manuals and teacher interviews should show distinct warning and repair profiles, such as pride around practitioner-owned positive remainder, passivity around source-credit, reification around ground-credit, and anti-calculation warnings around Other Power. Weakens if: Warnings are better predicted by institution, teacher style, lineage politics, trauma risk, retreat intensity, or generic spiritual bypassing than by the credit-residue interaction. Test surface: Compare interaction model against Pargament agency styles, VCE factors, Lutz matrix variables, and prior Lumenary ledgers.
- If: Residue authorization is separable from doctrine vocabulary. Then: Dual-trained practitioners should report different operational instructions at the same apparent nondual or post-negation threshold, such as inhabit witness, do not claim consciousness, rest without fabrication, entrust, or refuse the question. Weakens if: Practitioners say the differences are only teacher vocabulary, temperament, or institutional identity, and do not change actual sequencing, warnings, or repair. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with Advaita, Theravada, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Zen, Pure Land, and Christian apophatic practitioners.
- If: The model should help identify translation strain before doctrinal conflict becomes explicit. Then: When translating between traditions, pairs with similar credit but divergent residue policy should produce more misunderstanding than pairs with divergent metaphysics but similar operational residue rules. Weakens if: Translation strain tracks explicit metaphysics, historical polemic, language family, teacher authority, or borrowing history better than the proposed variables. Test surface: Blind prediction on translation commentaries and practitioner cross-training cases, including Advaita and Dzogchen, Zen and Shin, and apophatic Christianity and Madhyamaka.
- If: The current residue taxonomy should be exhaustive enough for initial coding. Then: Most primary passages can be coded as positive authorization, explicit refusal, non-positioning, self-resolving remainder, undecidable silence, or analyst-frame refusal. Weakens if: Large numbers of passages require new categories such as relational remainder, liturgical remainder, ethical remainder, institution-held remainder, no-search, or no-remainder report. Test surface: Pilot coding on Brihadaranyaka 3.7, SN 22.59, Ud 1.10, Tannisho, Dogen, Pseudo-Dionysius, one Dzogchen manual, and one Sufi fana or baqa source.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, addiction recovery, and behavior-change programs

Structural translation: Credit distribution becomes the assignment of change agency to client effort, therapist, group, medication, protocol, higher power, acceptance process, or already-present health. Residue authorization becomes what remains legitimate after the old identity story loosens: responsible agent, no-owner process, surrendered self, witness, relational self, or silence about self.

Prediction: Programs with similar techniques but different agency grammar should produce different failure and residue language. Strong self-credit programs should show pride and shame residue. Higher-power programs should show surrender versus passivity tension. Protocol-centered programs should show formalism. Acceptance or innate-health models should show premature completion claims. The interaction should add predictive value beyond alliance, dosage, diagnosis, resources, and social support.

Would count against it: Outcome and failure language are fully explained by alliance, severity, dosage, socioeconomic pressure, clinician competence, group support, and prior trauma, with no independent effect from agency grammar or post-identity residue.
