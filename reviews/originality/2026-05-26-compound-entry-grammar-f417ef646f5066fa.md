## Exact Claim

A tradition's account of spiritual entry is best modeled as a compound grammar with four fields: what incapacity blocks ordinary entry, what minimum participation counts as real entry, what break mechanism is required if self-generated threshold entry is denied, and what residue policy governs claims after ordinary agency is negated. First-break type is not universal. It becomes an irreducible variable only in high-severity traditions that explicitly deny that any naturally self-generated threshold act can found the path.

## Claim Units

- Spiritual entry should be modeled as a compound grammar rather than a single conversion, initiation, or practice-beginning event.
- The grammar has four fields: incapacity diagnosis, minimum participation threshold, break mechanism, and post-negation residue policy.
- First-break type is conditional, not universal.
- First-break becomes irreducible only when a tradition denies that ordinary self-generated threshold acts can found the path.
- The model should predict warnings, verification rules, and repair instructions through split-source coding.

Structural observation: Orange is a near-exact high-severity case: prayer, will, desire, assent, and the beginning of faith are attributed to grace, not unaided nature. Tannisho supplies a vow-held analogue, where entrusting to Amida's Primal Vow displaces self-power and reshapes warning language. SN 22.59 does not fit the same entry grammar, because it negates ownership of aggregates and moves through disenchantment and release without clearly requiring an external break mechanism. Dogen is the strongest anomaly: practice and realization are treated as one, and beginner practice is already implicated in realization, so entry and fruition cannot be cleanly sequenced as threshold and later attainment.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Rambo, Lofland-Stark, Van Gennep, Turner, Lindbeck, Pargament, Lave and Wenger, Orange, Shinran, Dogen, VCE, Meno-style inquiry paradoxes, and prior Lumenary entry-residue records, this idea says entry should be coded through four interacting fields, incapacity, minimum participation, break mechanism, and residue policy, and that first-break type becomes irreducible only where a tradition denies that ordinary self-generated threshold entry can found the path.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Inherited religious formation and Dogen-style practice-realization.

Why it strains the model: In inherited formation, there may be no discrete threshold act, no single first break, and no separate residue policy available to the beginner. In Dogen-style practice-realization, beginning and realization are not cleanly separated, so the entry grammar may be the same as the fruition grammar. These cases force the model to include no-threshold, no-break, continuous-authorization, practice-realization, and analyst-frame-refused values.

Confidence effect: This lowers confidence from candidate-discovery range to extended range. The model remains useful as a coding heuristic, but anomaly handling must be proven through blind coding before it can carry public claim weight.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: High-severity traditions that deny self-generated threshold entry should show distinctive warnings against self-credit or self-powered attainment. Then: Entry texts coded as grace-held, vow-held, or teacher-recognition entry should predict held-out warning texts targeting pride, self-effort absolutism, unauthorized claim-making, despair, passivity, or premature certainty. Weakens if: If held-out manuals from these traditions warn no more against self-credit than matched traditions with ordinary participation thresholds, the first-break variable loses predictive force. Test surface: Split-source coding across Orange and Augustinian grace texts, Tannisho and Shinran letters, Dzogchen or Mahamudra pointing-out manuals, and matched Theravada or Confucian cultivation texts.
- If: Minimum participation threshold should predict beginner repair instructions independently of broad tradition label. Then: Legitimate-peripheral or inherited-participation traditions should repair failure by restoring role, community, ritual, repetition, apprenticeship, or teacher relationship, while high-severity break traditions should repair failure by reorienting trust, grace, vow, recognition, or authorization. Weakens if: If repairs cluster mainly by institution, teacher style, social class, clinical risk, or generic conversion stage rather than coded threshold grammar, the model is mostly redundant with social-ecological theories. Test surface: Blind coding of entry sources, followed by held-out practitioner interviews about failed beginnings and repair instructions.
- If: Residue policy should interact with entry grammar rather than merely duplicate it. Then: Two traditions with similar entry thresholds but different residue policies should show different verification rules and different forbidden claimant warnings. Weakens if: If residue policy adds no predictive gain beyond entry mechanism, support locus, social ecology, and tradition identity, the compound model should collapse into simpler entry or agency models. Test surface: Compare Theravada no-self analysis, Advaita self-inquiry, Pure Land Other Power, Soto practice-realization, and Dzogchen recognition using inter-rater coding and held-out warning manuals.
- If: First-break should be non-universal. Then: Gradual cultivation, inherited practice, legitimate peripheral participation, and practice-realization cases should be codeable without a first-break field, or should receive a no-break or analyst-frame-refused value. Weakens if: If all cases require first-break accounting, the conditional claim is false. If none do after controls, the first-break field is unnecessary. Test surface: Initial six-case trial: Theravada sangha entry, Confucian cultivation, Jodo Shinshu, Augustinian grace, Zen great doubt, and Dzogchen pointing-out.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, recovery, and education entry into identity-changing practice

Structural translation: Replace spiritual incapacity with a learner's blocked agency model, minimum participation with legitimate first participation, break mechanism with external authorization or scaffold, and residue policy with what forms of self-trust remain valid after the old identity is challenged.

Prediction: Methods that deny the learner's old self-model as competent should need stronger scaffolded authorization and clearer residue rules than methods that allow peripheral participation from the start. Exposure therapy, addiction recovery, and apprenticeship learning should differ predictably in what counts as real entry and what kind of self-claim is treated as relapse, resistance, dependency, or premature mastery.

Would count against it: If entry failures and repairs in these domains are predicted equally well by motivation, alliance, skill difficulty, access, social support, and risk factors, with no added value from threshold plus residue coding, the structure does not travel.
