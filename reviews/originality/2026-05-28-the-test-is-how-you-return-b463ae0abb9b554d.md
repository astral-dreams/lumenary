## Exact Claim

A practice is not complete when it gives a person an experience. It is complete only when it teaches the person how to return to ordinary life. The return test asks whether the method lets the result stand or undermines the claim to have a result, and whether the method should be kept, set down, or carried as conduct. The practical danger is turning method or insight into proof of worth, or abandoning the method before it has trained ordinary conduct.

## Claim Units

- Practice completion should be judged by return to ordinary conduct, not by peak experience alone.
- Completion has two pressures: whether the result stands or is undermined, and whether the method is retained, released, or embodied.
- Different primary texts model different endings: MN 22 releases the raft after right use, Heart Sutra negates attainment while retaining reliance, Mandukya allows a known Self, Rinzai koan work exhausts ordinary thinking while remaining curriculum, and Dogen collapses before and after through practice-realization.
- The target modern problem is achievement-contingent self-worth, burnout, and compulsive conversion of every result into identity.
- The target cohort is stable adults who already use practice, study, work, prayer, or reflection and tend to turn outcomes into pride, shame, comparison, or status.
- Non-fit cases include acute crisis, severe depression, addiction withdrawal, dissociation, trauma activation, scrupulosity, destabilizing meditation experiences, and people under explicit teacher, vow, or clinical instructions.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike the raft simile, apophatic unsaying, therapeutic readings of Nagarjuna and Wittgenstein, Dogen practice-realization, VCE aftercare, Asad's discursive tradition, and Lindbeck's doctrine grammar, this idea's distinct claim is a practical comparative test: code whether a method validates or undermines the result, then code whether the method is retained, released, or embodied, and see whether that interaction predicts warnings, re-entry duties, identity risks, and repairs better than doctrine, institution, teacher style, or clinical-risk variables alone.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen practice-realization, radical Other Power in Tannisho, inherited communal religion, and successful ordinary ethical practice without a marked peak experience.

Why it strains the model: These cases resist the sequence of method, result, and return. Dogen makes practice and realization inseparable. Tannisho relocates efficacy to Amida's Vow and can make nembutsu non-practice from the practitioner's side. Inherited religion may not have a discrete completion event. Ordinary ethical formation may return without peak experience at all.

Confidence effect: Prevents candidate-discovery status. The model is useful as a heuristic only if it can code no-threshold, no-peak, Other Power, inherited practice, and practice-realization without forcing them into a modern after-experience frame.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The two-pressure return grid has predictive value beyond broad tradition labels. Then: Manuals or teachers whose methods are coded as retained after insight will prescribe different warnings and return duties than those coded as released or embodied, even within the same broad tradition. Weakens if: Warnings and return duties are better predicted by institution type, teacher style, intensity, clinical risk, or ordinary moral teaching than by the two-pressure grid. Test surface: Split-source blind coding of primary instruction passages, held-out warnings, teacher interviews, and inter-rater reliability.
- If: The Return Check helps achievement-contingent practitioners avoid turning practice into self-proof. Then: After two weeks, stable adults using the check report lower pride, shame, comparison, and post-result rumination, with equal or improved ordinary responsibility. Weakens if: The check increases rumination, self-surveillance, spiritual pride, avoidance of work, or contempt for ordinary achievement, or performs no better than a simple rest-and-plan prompt. Test surface: Small randomized pilot comparing Return Check, generic journaling, and rest-and-plan controls.
- If: Cross-training will expose different return grammars. Then: Practitioners trained in Advaita inquiry, Prajnaparamita practice, raft-style early Buddhist training, and Soto practice-realization will describe different appropriate relations to insight: stable recognition, resisted attainment, released tool, or ongoing practice-conduct identity. Weakens if: Practitioners converge on the same return rule after controlling for teacher and personality, or differences reduce to vocabulary without behavioral, warning, or repair consequences. Test surface: Dual-trained practitioner interviews and behavior-coded follow-up after comparable retreat experiences.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Education and professional training

Structural translation: A learning method is not complete when it produces a peak performance or test score. It is complete when the learner knows whether to keep the scaffold, remove it, or embody it as fluent conduct.

Prediction: In music, surgery, athletics, or software apprenticeship, scaffold-retain, scaffold-release, and scaffold-embody patterns will predict different coaching warnings and transition failures better than skill level alone.

Would count against it: Transition failures are fully explained by task difficulty, talent, hours practiced, and supervisor quality, with no added value from scaffold-retain, release, or embody coding.
