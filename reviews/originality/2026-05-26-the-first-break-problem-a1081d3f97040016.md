## Exact Claim

Spiritual paths face an upstream circularity: the very person needing transformation lacks the wisdom, freedom, faith, or recognition needed to begin. Traditions solve this first-break problem through mechanisms such as social encounter, cosmic gift, prevenient grace, or self-disclosure, and the chosen solution predicts downstream practice architecture.

## Claim Units

- There is a structural circularity at the beginning of transformative spiritual practice.
- Traditions can be compared by their first-break mechanism: social encounter, cosmic gift, prevenient grace, self-disclosure, and possibly no-break or gradual-break cases.
- The severity of the circularity tracks anthropology, especially whether human nature is seen as capable, impaired, bound, sinful, or already endowed.
- The entry solution predicts effort theory, verification, characteristic failure, and safeguards later in the path.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- SN 55.5, To Sariputta, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN55_5.html
- Second Council of Orange, canons 3-7, https://www.crivoice.org/creedorange.html

Structural observation: SN 55.5 lists association with people of integrity first, followed by listening, appropriate attention, and practice in accordance with the Dhamma. This supports an external entry condition, but it does not explicitly say the practitioner is incapable of appropriate attention without grace. Orange is much stronger: even prayer, will, desire, striving, seeking, and the beginning of faith are gifts of grace. The comparison supports a spectrum of first-break severity, but weakens the claim that the same paradox is equally live in both texts. SN 55.5 may be a conditional path model, while Orange is an explicit anti-self-initiation doctrine.

Secondary literature gap: The strongest prior art includes Plato's Meno on inquiry into what one does not know, Augustine and Orange on initium fidei, William James on volitional and self-surrender conversion, Rambo's conversion stages, Rahner's supernatural existential, Simone Kotva on effort and grace, Yogacara asraya-paravrtti, Dzogchen pointing-out literature, and transformative learning theory. The audit did not find the exact four-value cross-tradition typology with downstream predictions, but most structural ingredients are already named in adjacent literatures.

Risk of overread: Moderate to high. The model may project a philosophical bootstrapping problem onto practitioners who begin through family, ritual, obedience, attraction, crisis, community, or habit without experiencing a paradox. It may also collapse distinct cases: Shinran's Other Power and Orange's prevenient grace are structurally close, while Dzogchen self-disclosure and Platonic recollection may be closer than the typology admits.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Meno, Augustine, Orange, James, Rambo, Rahner, Kotva, Yogacara, and Dzogchen sources, this idea claims that the first-break mechanism is a cross-tradition variable that predicts later effort theory, verification, safeguards, and failure modes. That comparative-predictive use is the main contribution, not the circularity itself.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Mencian Confucian cultivation and inherited ritual traditions, where the path starts from innate moral sprouts, family formation, ritual participation, or ordinary socialization rather than a dramatic break in incapacity.

Why it strains the model: If some traditions assume an already-open moral capacity, then first-break is not universal. Also, SN 55.5 strains the model because social encounter comes first, but appropriate attention and practice are immediately active. This leaves a residual question: what enables recognition of the person of integrity? The model either needs latent capacity, no-break, or gradual-break values, or it generates infinite regress.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the universality claim but strengthens the model if revised as a variable with none, mild, gradual, social, gift, grace, and self-disclosure values.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Traditions with severe first-break solutions should show entry-point safeguards that match the feared false beginning. Then: Jodo Shinshu warns against self-power calculation, Orange-Augustinian grace traditions warn against pride and presumption, Dzogchen warns against premature recognition and fabrication, and social-encounter models warn against isolation from good friends or teachers. Weakens if: Entry warnings are no stronger or more type-specific than in mild or no-break traditions, or they track institution and polemic better than first-break mechanism. Test surface: Stage-coded analysis of entry instructions, initiation manuals, sermons, and teacher warnings.
- If: Anthropology should predict first-break severity. Then: Traditions with deep incapacity, sin, mappo, avidya as pervasive delusion, or ordinary mind as unreliable should use more external, prior, or self-disclosing breaks than traditions with innate goodness or workable capacities. Weakens if: Optimistic anthropologies use severe external first-break mechanisms just as often, or pessimistic anthropologies use ordinary self-cultivation without special entry safeguards. Test surface: Comparative coding of primary texts and doctrinal manuals across Confucian, Theravada, Pure Land, Christian, Dzogchen, Yogacara, and Zen sources.
- If: Historical hybrids should be more stable when their first-break mechanisms are compatible. Then: Movements that combine social encounter with gradual cultivation, or self-disclosure with original-endowment views, should stabilize more readily than hybrids that combine radical other-power with strongly self-powered entry practice. Weakens if: Hybrid success is better predicted by patronage, institutions, language, politics, charisma, or ritual compatibility than by first-break type. Test surface: Historical comparison of Chan, Pure Land-Zen syntheses, Christian contemplative revivals, and modern nondual movements.
- If: Practitioners should recognize entry grammar differences even when they do not use philosophical circularity language. Then: They can describe whether the first move is meeting, hearing, being called, being enabled, surrendering, recognizing what was present, cultivating a seed, or simply continuing inherited practice. Weakens if: Practitioners across traditions describe entry in generic terms, and teacher guidance does not change according to first-break grammar. Test surface: Practitioner and teacher interviews, coded without revealing the typology.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, education, and recovery programs

Structural translation: A process that changes the very capacities needed to enter it must explain how participation begins: through trusted encounter, crisis, institutional scaffolding, latent capacity, surrender, medication, group containment, or gradual readiness building.

Prediction: Programs aimed at distorted thinking, addiction, trauma, or critical thinking should have identifiable first-break mechanisms and entry safeguards. For example, readiness work, alliance formation, motivational interviewing, peer sponsor encounter, or disorienting dilemma should predict downstream practice architecture.

Would count against it: Entry success and downstream structure are fully explained by alliance, severity, resources, coercion, incentives, dosage, and social support, with no added predictive power from first-break type.
