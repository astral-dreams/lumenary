## Exact Claim

The felt post-practice demand to settle whether there is a true self, no self, a lost self, or a witness is often produced by the self-inquiry technique just used. Therefore the urgency of the question is not by itself evidence about reality, especially for solo and app-based practitioners without teacher calibration.

## Claim Units

- Post-negation identity pressure is partly technique-induced, not merely discovered.
- Witness-style inquiry should increase owner-seeking or residual-subject pressure.
- Aggregate or not-self analysis should reduce owner-seeking pressure.
- Within one practitioner, pressure should shift when the technique shifts.
- The practical use is safety for stable solo practitioners who turn post-practice identity pressure into anxiety, meaning crisis, or achievement-contingent self-worth.

Structural observation: MN 2 is a very close primary near-neighbor: it treats first-person metaphysical questions about being, not-being, and self as inappropriate attention that generates self-view and no-self-view tangles. Ramana's self-enquiry deliberately turns attention toward the source of the I and says the Self remains when mind subsides. SN 22.59 and SN 22.95 apply inspection to all aggregates, including consciousness, while Udana 1.10 blocks the move from seeing to a located you. Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 protects an unseen seer and knower through a repeated inner-ruler grammar. The candidate is strongest where it turns this textual contrast into a within-subject method-switch prediction. It is weakest if it treats Buddhist and Advaita texts as simple machines for producing experience rather than as discipline-specific soteriological instructions.

Secondary literature gap: Before publication, read Steven Katz on contextualist mysticism, Robert Sharf on the rhetoric of meditative experience, Ann Taves on attributed experiences, VCE and Progress or Pathology papers by Lindahl and colleagues, self-focused attention and rumination literature, depersonalization and derealization clinical sources, Ramana and later Advaita practitioner manuals, Mahamudra or Dzogchen direct-recognition manuals, and micro-phenomenological interview methods.

Risk of overread: High. The claim can collapse MN 2's old warning about inappropriate self-questions, constructivist religious-experience theory, VCE practice-context findings, and prior Lumenary remainder-pressure work into a new slogan. Human-condition fit is real but narrow: anxiety, identity distress, meaning loss, and achievement-contingent self-worth after solo self-inquiry. It is not for persistent depersonalization, derealization, psychosis, mania, trauma activation, severe depression, panic disorder, addiction withdrawal, or people under competent teacher or clinical care.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike MN 2, Ramana self-enquiry, VCE, Katz, Sharf, and prior Lumenary remainder-pressure work, this idea specifically predicts that the same practitioner will show different post-negation identity pressure depending on the immediately preceding technique, so the pressure itself is an instrument artifact until it survives method-switch testing.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Stable direct-recognition reports in Advaita, Dzogchen, Mahamudra, or clear-light sleep, plus clinical depersonalization and derealization that persist independent of technique.

Why it strains the model: If trained practitioners report the same recognition, no-location, or absence across different methods and over time, the artifact claim weakens. If distressed practitioners show persistent unreality outside practice, the pressure is not just the tool's echo and requires clinical routing.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in any broad claim. Supports only a bounded hypothesis for stable solo practitioners after specific sessions, with direct-recognition and clinical cases treated as exclusions or anomaly classes.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Witness inquiry generates more residual-owner pressure than aggregate analysis in the same person. Then: In a randomized within-subject crossover, dual-trained practitioners report more urges to find the knower, source, witness, or true self after who-am-I or watcher inquiry, and less after five-aggregate not-self analysis. Weakens if: The felt pressure is stable across methods, or it tracks trait anxiety, sleep, intensity, teacher expectation, or doctrine better than the immediately preceding technique. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews and short post-session surveys after counterbalanced Advaita-style self-inquiry, SN 22.59-style aggregate analysis, and neutral breath practice.
- If: The question prompt itself contributes to the pressure. Then: After the same quiet-state induction, participants prompted with who is aware produce more owner-seeking language than participants prompted with is this mine or what changed in conduct. Weakens if: Prompt wording has no effect, or all groups converge on the same self, no-self, or no-location language after controlling for prior training. Test surface: Randomized prompt experiment with screened stable meditators, coded blind for owner terms, absence terms, no-location terms, urgency, and distress.
- If: Treating the pull as a technique echo should reduce urgent metaphysical self-verdicts for the target cohort. Then: The Name the Tool practice reduces identity-verdict urgency, rumination, and self-worth scoring more than ordinary journaling, without increasing avoidance or dissociation. Weakens if: Ordinary rest or social contact works as well, the prompt increases checking, or participants ignore clinical symptoms because the practice labels them technique artifacts. Test surface: Small screened pilot with solo app users and self-inquiry readers, excluding depersonalization or derealization disorder, OCD or scrupulosity, psychosis, mania, severe depression, trauma activation, and addiction withdrawal.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: software incident response and organizational learning

Structural translation: An inquiry tool can create the kind of agent it then appears to discover. Blame-oriented prompts produce owner-hunting, while systems prompts produce process-hunting.

Prediction: Teams asked who owns this failure immediately after an incident will generate more hidden-agent and personal-responsibility narratives than teams asked which process conditions made this likely, even with identical incident data.

Would count against it: If blame or system narratives are explained entirely by organizational culture, severity, or manager behavior and do not change with prompt structure, the tool-artifact structure does not travel.
