## Exact Claim

At completion, contemplative methods face a self-authority question: some confirm themselves, some cancel themselves, and some dissolve the practitioner beyond method-talk, with consequences for verification and transmission.

## Claim Units

- A method's relation to its own result may be a comparative variable upstream of doctrinal content.
- Mandukya is classified as self-confirming because analysis of waking, dreaming, and deep sleep culminates in turiya as Atman to be known.
- Nagarjuna, Heart Sutra, Zen koan, and Eckhart are classified as self-canceling because their tools undermine graspable thesis, wisdom, attainment, or desire.
- Zhuangzi, Dzogchen, and Sufi fana are classified as self-dissolving because the practitioner or frame of method becomes irrelevant.
- The type should predict verification architecture, teaching strategy, and ethical consequences.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Mandukya Upanishad verse 7, Sanskrit and translation, https://texts.wara.in/upanishads/mukhya/mandukya/ and https://gita.pub/upanishad/mandukya/verse/7/
- Heart Sutra, Sanskrit and English, https://sa.wikisource.org/wiki/प्रज्ञापारमिताहृदय and https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Translation:Shorter_Prajñāpāramitā_Hṛdaya_Sūtra

Structural observation: The Mandukya and Heart Sutra contrast is promising but not clean. Mandukya verse 7 negates ordinary consciousness categories, then ends by saying this is Atman and is to be known, which supports a confirming limit move. Yet Shankara's commentary also says affirmative and negative language fail, so Mandukya contains a self-canceling linguistic element. The Heart Sutra negates aggregates, sense bases, ignorance, the four truths, wisdom, and attainment, which supports the self-canceling reading. Yet it immediately says bodhisattvas rely on Prajnaparamita and gives a mantra, so the method is retained as reliance and recitation, not simply canceled. The close read supports a two-axis model better than a pure three-box typology.

Secondary literature gap: I did not find an exact three-type typology of contemplative methods as confirming, canceling, and dissolving at completion. But the self-canceling portion has very strong prior art in Nagarjuna, Wittgenstein ladder readings, Buddhist raft discussions, and Sells on apophatic unsaying. The dissolving portion is also standard in fana, zuowang, and Dzogchen descriptions. The new part is the comparative axis and its predicted consequences, not the individual patterns.

Risk of overread: High. The model imposes a modern reflexive category, method's relation to itself, on traditions that may speak instead of upaya, realization, grace, direct recognition, non-grasping, or annihilation of ego. It also risks treating stage-specific pedagogies as stable types.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Smith on Nagarjuna and Wittgenstein, Sells on apophatic unsaying, or the raft and ladder motifs, this idea proposes a three-way comparative axis and claims that the axis predicts verification and transmission better than doctrine alone.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Validated-then-abandoned hybrids, especially MN 22's raft, Heart Sutra reliance on Prajnaparamita after no-attainment, and Rinzai koan curriculum after kensho.

Why it strains the model: These cases mix confirmation, cancellation, and dissolution across stages. The raft must be grasped properly before being released. The Heart Sutra negates attainment but retains reliance and mantra. Koan practice may exhaust conceptual striving yet becomes a formal curriculum with tests. If hybrids are normal, the three types are not stable categories.

Confidence effect: Substantially lowers confidence in the three-box typology. The contribution survives better as a two-axis or staged model.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Method-type should predict verification architecture better than tradition membership. Then: Eckhart and Nagarjuna should share more indirect, anti-possessive verification patterns with each other than Eckhart shares with more kataphatic Christian mystics. Advaita witness practice should use more direct recognition language than Madhyamaka critique. Weakens if: Christian sources cluster together by tradition more strongly than by method-type, or all traditions use the same teacher, lineage, and conduct criteria regardless of method relation. Test surface: Coded comparison of practice manuals, teacher testing language, and post-realization instructions across at least six traditions.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report different relations to their own insight when switching method-types. Then: After Advaita inquiry, practitioners describe recognition as stable witness or Self. After Heart Sutra or Madhyamaka practice, they resist treating any insight as an attained object. After Dzogchen or fana language, they emphasize effortless presence or transformed embodiment over method success. Weakens if: Practitioners report no stable difference, or differences track teacher personality and institutional context more than method-type. Test surface: Structured interviews with practitioners trained in more than one contemplative lineage.
- If: Hybrid traditions should show explicit boundary management between method stages. Then: Texts should mark when to use, trust, undermine, release, or embody a method. Examples would include raft language, preliminary versus main practice, koan curriculum before and after kensho, or thorn-removing-thorn metaphors. Weakens if: No boundary markers appear, or methods freely confirm, cancel, and dissolve without any pedagogical distinction. Test surface: Historical and textual analysis of Zen, Dzogchen, Advaita, Madhyamaka, Sufi, and Christian apophatic training sequences.

## Practitioner Test

Relevant practitioners:
- Rinzai Zen teachers familiar with koan curriculum
- Madhyamaka scholars and monastic teachers
- Advaita Vedanta self-inquiry teachers
- Dzogchen or Mahamudra teachers
- Sufi teachers familiar with fana and baqa
- Christian apophatic spiritual directors

Questions:
- At completion, can the method still authorize its result, or must it be released, denied, or embodied?
- Is confirming, canceling, dissolving an obvious distinction from inside your training, or does it distort the practice?
- Do your verification methods change depending on whether the practice confirms, cancels, or dissolves itself?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say every serious path already uses all three stages, or identify the model as a familiar upaya, raft, ladder, no-thesis, fana, or Gelassenheit pattern with new labels.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners find that the type distinction clarifies why different lineages test realization, authorize teachers, and warn against grasping insight in different ways.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, education, and research methods

Structural translation: Some methods confirm themselves by producing stable results, some cancel themselves by dissolving the problem frame, and some dissolve into skilled embodiment where the practitioner no longer follows the method explicitly.

Prediction: Methods that cancel their own frame should rely more on supervision, case judgment, paradox, and indirect signs than on checklist fidelity. Methods that confirm themselves should tolerate manuals and outcome criteria better. Methods that dissolve into embodiment should prioritize apprenticeship and exemplars.

Would count against it: If confirmation, cancellation, and dissolution do not predict training, verification, or institutional form outside contemplative traditions, the proposed structure may be domain-specific rhetoric.
