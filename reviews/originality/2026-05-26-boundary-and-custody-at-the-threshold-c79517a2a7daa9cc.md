## Exact Claim

When contemplative practice weakens ordinary self-ownership of attention and authority, two candidate variables shape what follows: boundary operation and custody rule. Boundary operation asks whether the method validates itself, undermines itself, or becomes irrelevant as the practitioner matures beyond method-talk. Custody rule asks where freed attention and authority may belong afterward: no owner, hidden Self, God, teacher, lineage, vow, conduct, Prajnaparamita, or embodied responsiveness. Their interaction may predict verification architecture, teaching strategy, and reentry more precisely than doctrinal content, boundary operation alone, or custody alone.

## Claim Units

- Self-weakening contemplative practice creates a threshold where ordinary ownership of attention and authority is weakened.
- Boundary operation is a variable: the method may validate itself, undermine itself, become irrelevant, or need another value such as practice-realization identity.
- Custody rule is a variable: freed attention or authority may be assigned to no owner, Self, God, teacher, lineage, vow, conduct, Prajnaparamita, embodied responsiveness, or no custody frame.
- The interaction of boundary operation and custody rule may predict verification architecture, teaching strategy, and reentry better than doctrine, boundary alone, or custody alone.
- The claim must be tested by independent coding, not inferred from the same passages used to confirm predictions.

Structural observation: The primary texts partly support the model but mostly lower novelty. MN 22 already gives a boundary operation in which the Dhamma is valid for crossing and then not to be grasped, so method validation and method release are staged together. The Heart Sutra negates attainment but preserves reliance on Prajnaparamita, which is almost an exact prior structure for boundary cancellation plus retained custody. Tannisho relocates efficacy to Amida's Original Vow, denies self-powered merit, rejects teacher ownership, and restricts reading to those mature in faith, so custody, verification, and warning are already internally linked. Dogen strains the whole threshold premise: practice-enlightenment and verification by all things collapse the gap between method and completion, making boundary and custody look less like two variables and more like an analyst's grid imposed on a no-threshold practice field.

Secondary literature gap: The audit used accessible primary translations, local Lumenary audits and dialogues, open academic records, and practitioner-facing sources. It did not read Japanese, Sanskrit, Pali, Tibetan, Greek, Arabic, or Latin originals, and it did not interview teachers. Further work should read Asad, Lindbeck, Weber, Sharf, Hori, Sells, Pargament, VCE research, Dogen scholarship, Pure Land scholarship, and apophatic theology in greater depth before any stronger novelty claim.

Risk of overread: High. The model can become circular if boundary, custody, verification, and reentry are inferred from the same passage. It can also translate salvation, liberation, grace, lineage, conduct, and ritual into a modern governance vocabulary of attention and authority. Some traditions may refuse the threshold frame, especially Dogen practice-realization, inherited religion, radical Other Power, apophatic predication refusal, and traditions where authorization is political, communal, sacramental, or continuous rather than contemplative.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen practice-realization identity, radical Other Power in Tannisho, inherited communal religion, and apophatic refusal in Dionysius.

Why it strains the model: Dogen removes the gap between method and result, so there may be no threshold where boundary and custody can be assigned. Tannisho places decisive working in Amida's vow while warning against self-calculation, so calling entrusting a local custody handoff can reintroduce the self-power frame the text resists. Inherited religion may mature by continuity and ritual participation rather than by a discernible handoff. Dionysius places the divine Cause beyond affirmation and negation, which destabilizes God as a simple custody recipient.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate discovery to extended. The model needs mandatory no-threshold, practice-realization, radical Other Power, inherited continuity, apophatic refusal, no-custody-frame, continuous authorization, and analyst-frame-refused values.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Boundary operation and custody rule interact rather than merely adding two descriptive labels. Then: Split-source coders who code boundary operation from one corpus and custody rule from another should predict held-out verification signs, teaching permission, warning profiles, and reentry disciplines above tradition-label, doctrine-only, boundary-only, and custody-only baselines. Weakens if: The interaction term adds no predictive power, or the same predictions are explained by institution type, teacher style, stage sequence, social ecology, or clinical-risk variables. Test surface: Pre-registered coding of Advaita, Theravada, Rinzai, Soto, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Jodo Shinshu, Christian apophatic practice, and Sufi fana or baqa sources.
- If: Pairs sharing one axis but differing on the other should diverge predictably. Then: Cases with similar boundary cancellation but different custody rules, such as Heart Sutra reliance, Eckhartian Gelassenheit, and Madhyamaka non-positioning, should diverge in verification and reentry. Cases with similar vow or grace custody but different boundary operations should diverge in method retention and warning style. Weakens if: Pairs cluster mainly by broad tradition, institution, genre, teacher lineage, or language family rather than by boundary-custody pairing. Test surface: Matched primary-text pairs and held-out manuals, plus blind clustering against coded outcomes.
- If: The variables are observable to practitioners as actual repair distinctions, not just analyst vocabulary. Then: Teachers should report concrete cases where the boundary operation was understood but custody was misassigned, or custody was understood but the method-boundary was mishandled, and they should prescribe different repairs for each. Weakens if: Practitioners say the distinction is standard lineage discernment with new labels, or cannot name any repair that differs from ordinary pedagogy, upaya, grace, vow, or supervision. Test surface: Structured interviews with teachers and senior practitioners, asking for failed-student and cross-training cases rather than agreement with the theory.
- If: The model can be coded reliably before outcome inspection. Then: Independent coders should reach acceptable agreement on boundary operation, custody rule, stage, source layer, and analyst-frame refusal before seeing warnings, verification, and repair passages. ## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy, clinical supervision, and professional formation.

Structural translation: Boundary operation becomes how a method treats itself at maturity: retained protocol, self-undermining tool, embodied skill, or practice-identity. Custody rule becomes where authority and responsibility move after change: client agency, therapist, supervisor, group, protocol, medication, values, professional ethics, or embodied responsiveness.

Prediction: Training systems that alter identity or attention should show distinct termination, supervision, relapse, and ethical-reentry patterns based on the interaction between method-boundary and custody. For example, a protocol that is later internalized should differ from one that is explicitly released, and both should differ when responsibility is held by client values, supervisor authority, group accountability, or professional ethics.

Would count against it: If outcomes, supervision failures, termination protocols, and ethical repairs are predicted just as well by diagnosis, alliance, institution policy, practitioner experience, or generic risk management, with no added value from boundary-custody interaction.
