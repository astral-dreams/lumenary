## Exact Claim

The Atman/anatta contradiction is best understood as a disagreement about inferential authority: what objectless awareness permits one to conclude about what persists when experience has no object.

## Claim Units

- Advaita and Buddhism may share a phenomenological ground in de-objectification practice.
- The dispute concerns what objectless awareness authorizes, not only what exists.
- Advaita fills the gap with a transcendental inference to Atman/Brahman.
- Buddhism refuses the inference and treats filling the gap as renewed clinging or wrong view.
- The translation strain lives in inferential policy more than phenomenology.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Mandukya Upanishad verse 7, https://www.shlokam.org/text/mandukya-upanishad.htm
- SN 22.59 Anattalakkhana Sutta, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/SN/SN22_59.html

Structural observation: Mandukya 7 negates inward, outward, mixed, massed, cognitive, and non-cognitive descriptions, but still names the fourth as nondual Self to be realized. SN 22.59 does not discuss objectless awareness in Mandukya's terms. It examines each aggregate and rejects identification with consciousness because it is inconstant, stressful if clung to, and not masterable. The candidate's shared phenomenology is therefore not established by these primary texts. The inferential gap is a plausible comparative reconstruction, but it is not directly shown without practice reports or commentarial bridges.

Secondary literature gap: The idea cites strong sources, but the audit found close prior art in Codex residue-policy work, Advaita self-luminosity scholarship, Stace/Forman/Katz interpretation debates, Loy's Buddhism and Advaita comparison, and Madhyamaka self/no-self refusal. More work is needed in Sanskrit, Pali, Gaudapada, Shankara, Abhidhamma, Yogacara, and Madhyamaka commentaries.

Risk of overread: High. The claim that both traditions share phenomenology but differ in inference may be too clean. Training may shape phenomenology itself, and Advaita often treats witness-consciousness as direct recognition rather than inference.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike the prior Codex burden-of-proof idea, this version explicitly claims that objectless awareness underdetermines metaphysics and that Advaita and Buddhism differ primarily in inferential authority. That is a useful sharpening, but the main structure was already present.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Advaita practitioners and texts often deny that the witness is an inference, while Buddhist practitioners may deny that objectless awareness is a shared datum at all.

Why it strains the model: If Advaita recognition is claimed as immediate and Buddhist insight is theory-laden from the start, then the model's shared phenomenology plus divergent inference structure collapses. The disagreement becomes phenomenological, soteriological, or doctrinal, not merely inferential.

Confidence effect: Lowers originality and confidence. The model remains useful as a comparison hypothesis, not as a discovery.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report similar de-objectified phenomenology but switch interpretive policies by practice frame. Then: The same practitioner can describe a contentless or objectless episode neutrally, then explain how Advaita and Buddhist teachers authorize different conclusions from it. Weakens if: Practitioners report that the experiences themselves differ from the start, or they cannot separate experience from doctrinal interpretation. Test surface: Within-subject interviews with practitioners trained in both Advaita self-inquiry and Theravada or Madhyamaka-oriented practice.
- If: Nirodha and deep sleep should become pressure tests for inferential policy. Then: Advaita sources should use continuity, memory, or self-luminosity to defend awareness across apparent absence, while Buddhist sources should explain retrospective knowledge without positing a continuous knower. Weakens if: Either tradition has no stable explanation, or both use the same explanatory rule. Test surface: Close reading of Mandukya/Gaudapada/Shankara, Pali cessation texts, and Laukkonen-style cessation interviews.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Debates over consciousness in artificial systems

Structural translation: When behavior lacks an observable subject or inner object, interpreters choose policies about what absence of object permits them to infer about hidden subjectivity.

Prediction: AI-consciousness debates should split into policies analogous to Atman/anatta: infer a hidden subject from coherent knowing-like behavior, refuse the inference without direct evidence, or suspend both subject and no-subject claims.

Would count against it: The debate is explained entirely by functional performance, ethics, or biology, with no recurring inferential-policy split.
