## Exact Claim

A comparative practice rubric should first ask how a tradition relates to claim-making itself. Support locus and forbidden claimant are useful only after coding the tradition's claimability stance as regulatory, dissolutive, ontological, or mixed-by-stage. That prior stance should predict the tradition's characteristic safeguards and failure modes.

## Claim Units

- Support locus can be coded by function: receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, integration, teacher, vow, text, community, present act, awareness itself, or later integration.
- The forbidden-claimant column is valid only where a tradition uses a regulatory grammar of identifiable agents, permissions, and prohibitions.
- Dissolutive traditions do not merely prohibit appropriation, they unsettle the agent-action or subject-object frame that makes appropriation intelligible.
- Ontological traditions deny that the decisive reality is produced, acquired, or missing, so practice removes obstruction rather than creating the goal.
- Mixed-by-stage traditions shift claimability logic across phases, for example sudden awakening followed by gradual cultivation.
- Stance should predict distinct failure modes: wrong claimant, formalism, reification, premature realization, passivity, or stage-transition confusion.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Lindbeck's doctrine-as-grammar, Pargament's religious agency styles, Nagarjuna's agent-action critique, Dogen's practice-realization, Dzogchen primordial purity, Advaita praptasya prapti, Srivaishnava effort-grace debates, upaya pedagogy, and prior Lumenary credit-distribution records, this idea says the first coding question is whether the claiming framework itself is authorized, dissolved, ontologically denied, or stage-dependent. Its possible contribution is not the anti-appropriation insight, but the rule that support-locus and forbidden-claimant rubrics are valid only after that prior stance is coded.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Longchen Rabjam's Trekcho instruction combines primordial, spontaneously present awareness with repeated instructions to meditate, observe karmic law, keep vows, cultivate devotion, and apply practice until self-grasping fades.

Why it strains the model: This is not neatly regulatory, dissolutive, or ontological. It may be simultaneous rather than mixed-by-stage. If one primary text requires multiple stance codes at the same time, the model's categories may be heuristic tags rather than stable structural types.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence and blocks candidate-discovery status. The model remains useful as a coding heuristic, but the stance categories need multi-value coding, stage coding, and an analyst-frame-refused option.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Claimability stance predicts warning and repair patterns beyond credit distribution and support locus. Then: If coders classify a text as dissolutive from doctrinal passages alone, later manual warnings should target attainment-reification, subject-object fixation, or formalism more than wrong effort level or wrong credit assignment. Weakens if: Warnings are generic across traditions or are better predicted by teacher style, institution, intensity of practice, trauma risk, or ordinary upaya pedagogy. Test surface: Split-source blind coding of doctrinal passages, then separate warning sections and teacher interviews.
- If: Claimability stance and credit distribution are separable variables. Then: Srivaishnava sub-schools should share a broadly regulatory claimability stance while differing on how effort and grace are distributed. Weakens if: The stance changes whenever credit distribution changes, making the new variable redundant. Test surface: Manual coding of Vadakalai and Tenkalai prapatti sources, with coders blinded to the proposed model.
- If: Ontological denial produces a distinctive premature-realization failure mode. Then: Advaita and Dzogchen teachers should report recurring cases where students use already-present language to avoid preparation, conduct, integration, or verification. Weakens if: These cases are no more common than in regulatory traditions, or spiritual bypassing explains them without reference to ontological claimability. Test surface: Practitioner interviews, adverse-practice reports, and comparison with spiritual-bypassing literature.
- If: Mixed-by-stage transitions are high-vulnerability points. Then: In Chinul-like or Zen training contexts, confusion should cluster around the movement from recognition or sudden awakening into gradual cultivation and integration. Weakens if: Confusion clusters around retreat intensity, social isolation, teacher misconduct, or mental health factors rather than stance transitions. Test surface: Longitudinal practitioner reports and teacher case interviews coded against baseline risk factors.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and coaching models of client agency

Structural translation: Support locus becomes the location of change functions: client, therapist, method, group, medication, environment, insight, or later integration. Claimability stance becomes the model's prior relation to agency: strengthen agency, share agency, dissolve a self-story, treat health as already present but obstructed, or shift stance by stage.

Prediction: Therapies with similar techniques but different agency-frame stance should produce different failure modes. Agency-strengthening models should risk self-blame, surrender-based models should risk passivity, self-dissolving models should risk dissociation or avoidance, and already-whole models should risk bypassing concrete behavioral repair.

Would count against it: Failure patterns are explained by alliance, diagnosis, therapist skill, treatment dose, socioeconomic pressure, or trauma history with no additional predictive value from agency-frame stance.
