## Exact Claim

For achievement-driven people, recurring self-audits about practice are often unsafe. The diagnostic grid should usually remain with teachers, designers, clinicians, communities, or one-time setup. The user-facing form should be brief and behavioral: name the tool lightly, stop making it proof of worth, and perform one ordinary re-entry act. After-use reflection is appropriate only when the path is staged, the person is not caught in compulsive checking, and the check does not increase rumination, shame, avoidance, reassurance seeking, or tool-performance anxiety.

## Claim Units

- Recurring practice self-audit can harm achievement-driven people when it becomes proof of worth.
- A diagnostic grid can be useful backstage for a teacher, clinician, designer, or community while being harmful as a repeated frontstage worksheet.
- The safer user-facing form is a non-scoring transition into observable conduct.
- After-use reflection needs path, disposition, and effect gates.
- The human-condition target is narrow: achievement-contingent self-worth, burnout, perfectionism, rumination, and compulsive self-monitoring.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike CBT formulation, ACT, behavioral activation, ERP, mood-as-input stop rules, mood-monitoring safety research, Ignatian Examen, upaya, scaffolding, VCE, and prior Lumenary after-use records, this idea claims that diagnostic-grid location is itself a testable variable: backstage for care holders and designers, frontstage only as a brief non-scoring re-entry act for achievement-driven over-auditors.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Structured self-review can be valid and beneficial: Ignatian Examen, Stoic evening review, monastic confession preparation, mood tracking in measurement-based care, early meditation logs, and CBT homework can increase accountability, learning, and repair.

Why it strains the model: If repeated self-review helps high achievers when it is time-limited, compassionate, action-linked, and sometimes guided, then the backstage rule is too broad. The real variable may be not grid versus no grid, but scoring, frequency, clinical risk, guidance, interpretation, and whether the act becomes a compulsion. A second anomaly is avoidance: doing one ordinary act can become a way to dodge grief, confession, moral repair, relational accountability, or needed clinical care.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in any general no-audit teaching. Supports a narrower delivery-location protocol: withhold repeated self-scoring grids from over-auditors unless bounded, externally held, and empirically shown not to increase checking, shame, avoidance, or reassurance seeking.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Backstage diagnostic structure plus frontstage maxim-and-act is safer than repeated self-administered grids for over-auditors. Then: In screened achievement-driven participants, maxim-plus-act and behavior-only re-entry conditions reduce rumination, shame, tool-performance anxiety, and reassurance seeking more than a repeated After-Use Check, while producing equal or better ordinary action completion within 24 hours. Weakens if: The full self-audit performs as well or better without increased checking or shame, or effects are explained by baseline OCD risk, perfectionism, sleep, workload coercion, social support, therapist alliance, or teacher contact. Test surface: Four-arm pilot: repeated After-Use Check, behavior-only re-entry, maxim-plus-act without rating, and ordinary journaling or usual practice.
- If: Delivery location is separable from prior Lumenary custody, support-holder, and no-audit models. Then: Blind coders can identify delivery location, backstage holder, frontstage burden, scoring, frequency, and re-entry act from intervention descriptions, and these fields predict safety outcomes beyond path type, support holder, clinical risk, and tradition label. Weakens if: Coders cannot distinguish delivery location from support holder or custody, or the field adds no predictive power over prior codebooks. Test surface: Inter-rater codebook trial on Lumenary practices plus CBT formulation, ACT exercises, ERP anti-checking rules, Examen, Soto instruction, Pure Land guidance, and MBCT homework.
- If: The harm mechanism is worth-conversion, not reflection itself. Then: Participants high in contingent self-worth, socially prescribed perfectionism, and reassurance seeking show the largest adverse response to self-rating grids; participants low on these traits or under guided review show less harm or benefit. Weakens if: Adverse response does not interact with contingent self-worth, perfectionism, OCD or scrupulosity risk, guidance, scoring, or frequency. Test surface: Moderation analysis using validated measures of perfectionism, contingent self-worth, rumination, reassurance seeking, burnout, and action completion.
- If: Practitioners already distinguish private diagnostic formulation from public instruction in concrete cases. Then: Teachers, clinicians, and spiritual directors report holding complex distinctions privately while giving vulnerable students simpler conduct, rest, relational, or support instructions, and they can name cases where giving the full grid caused self-surveillance or shame. Weakens if: Practitioners routinely give full grids without harm, or decisions are fully explained by teacher style, tradition, clinical severity, or institutional norms. Test surface: Structured interviews asking for concrete failed-student cases and repairs, not agreement with the slogan.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: software incident response and workplace performance review

Structural translation: Complex diagnostic taxonomies may help incident leads, managers, coaches, or system designers, while repeated individual self-scoring can create defensive performance identity. Prediction: Teams that keep detailed incident diagnostics backstage with leads while giving contributors one concrete repair or learning action will show less defensive reporting, less burnout-linked self-blame, fewer duplicate meta-meetings, and equal or better repair completion than teams requiring repeated individual reflection grids after every incident or performance miss.

Would count against it: If outcomes are explained by manager trust, workload, severity, compensation incentives, team maturity, psychological safety, or process quality, and delivery location adds no measurable prediction.
