## Exact Claim

Spiritual prerequisites are not neutral capacities later applied to insight. Attention, discrimination, trust, longing, self-observation, and integration are trained with a directional grain toward the specific dissolution a tradition seeks, and mismatched grain should predict distinctive resistance and harm patterns.

## Claim Units

- Preparatory capacities are shaped by the dissolution they serve.
- The same named capacity, such as attention or self-observation, functions differently in Theravada, Advaita, Sufism, Pure Land, Dzogchen, and Soto Zen.
- Capacity and dissolution are mutually conditioned rather than independently first.
- The five ledger variables carry direction, not only level.
- When a practitioner trained in one tradition's grain attempts another tradition's dissolution, the old grain should resist or distort the new practice.
- Adverse contemplative effects should cluster by grain mismatch, not only by missing prerequisites or practice intensity.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- MN 10 Satipatthana Sutta and SN 22.59 Anattalakkhana Sutta, https://suttacentral.net/mn10/en/nyanamoli-thera and https://suttacentral.net/sn22.59/en/bodhi
- Vivekachudamani vv. 17-30, https://shlokam.org/text/vivekachudamani.htm

Structural observation: Vivekachudamani strongly supports the grain claim because its prerequisites are already defined by the Advaita target: discrimination is framed as conviction that Brahman is real and the world is unreal, and calmness is tied to resting the mind on that goal. MN 10 is less explicit if read alone: it instructs body, feeling, mind, and dhamma contemplation with mindfulness and clear comprehension, but the opening formula does not itself say every act is aimed at non-ownership. SN 22.59 supplies that direction by repeatedly using impermanence, suffering, and non-mastery to reject mine, I, and self. The close reading therefore supports a weaker claim: some traditions explicitly grain prerequisites toward their final insight, while in others the grain must be established by reading practice instructions together with broader doctrinal and soteriological frames.

Secondary literature gap: Search found strong near-neighbors in Katz's constructivism, Sharf on meditative experience and bare attention, Dunne on Buddhist styles of mindfulness, Ferrer's participatory theory, Hadot-style spiritual exercises, Engler's developmental prerequisite thesis, Lindahl and Britton's VCE work, and local Lumenary work on attentional instruments and verification architecture. I did not find the exact phrase grain of capacity or a specific claim that prerequisites, rather than experiences, are directionally carved toward the dissolution they enable. Still, most structural components are known.

Risk of overread: High. The model may infer final-directional grain from a comparative observer's map rather than from practitioner experience. It also risks using a Madhyamaka fire-and-fuel lens as if all traditions accept dependent co-arising between capacity and dissolution. In Advaita or Sufi frames, sequence, grace, or revelation may be more important than co-arising.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Katz, Sharf, Dunne, Ferrer, Engler, and the local attentional-instrument thesis, this idea says that prerequisites themselves carry a directional grain toward the transformation they enable, and that this grain should predict specific transfer resistance and adverse effects when a practitioner crosses traditions.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Successful hybrids and dual-trained practitioners who switch methods smoothly without reported directional resistance.

Why it strains the model: If a vipassana-trained practitioner can move into Advaita self-inquiry, devotional surrender, or Dzogchen recognition without attention persistently tracking impermanence, non-ownership, or analytic deconstruction, then grain may be a comparative description rather than a lived capacity structure. Chan, Zen, modern cross-training, and eclectic contemplative therapy are especially important anomaly classes.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate discovery to novel synthesis. The model is promising only if practitioner reports and manual coding show directional resistance beyond ordinary difficulty, teacher style, or conceptual disagreement.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Dual-trained practitioners should experience method-specific transfer resistance. Then: Vipassana-trained practitioners attempting Advaita self-inquiry should initially keep tracking change and non-ownership rather than resting in witness-recognition. Advaita-trained practitioners attempting vipassana should tend to seek a stable knower or background rather than evenly noting impermanence. Practitioners trained in non-attachment who enter devotional surrender should report affective restraint or suspicion of dependence. Weakens if: Practitioners report smooth switching with no directional pull, or reported resistance is fully explained by temperament, teacher instructions, language, or community identity. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with dual-trained practitioners, coded before doctrinal explanation is invited.
- If: Primary manuals should grain prerequisites toward their final dissolution. Then: Vivekachudamani-style qualifications should define readiness in terms of the real versus unreal and longing for liberation. Satipatthana and later insight manuals should define readiness through impermanence, suffering, non-ownership, and ethical abandonment. Sufi manuals should orient self-knowledge toward creaturely dependence before God. Dzogchen manuals should orient preparation toward devotion, introduction, recognition, and non-distraction. Weakens if: Prerequisites are generic across manuals, such as calm, attention, ethics, and perseverance, with little relation to the final insight language of each path. Test surface: Blind coding of Visuddhimagga chapter 1, Vivekachudamani vv. 17-30, Cloud of Unknowing chapters 3-6, al-Qushayri's Risala, Longchen Rabjam Trekcho, and Satipatthana-related materials.
- If: Adverse effects should cluster by grain mismatch, not only by intensity, trauma history, or lack of prerequisites. Then: Witness-trained practitioners pushed into rapid de-identification should show witness reification more than dissociation. Non-attachment-trained practitioners attempting devotional surrender should show emotional flattening more than spiritual inflation. Devotional practitioners entering analytic deconstruction should show relational rupture or fear of betrayal more than abstract nihilism. Weakens if: Adverse experiences cluster only by retreat intensity, sleep disruption, psychiatric history, social isolation, or teacher support, with no added explanatory value from prior practice grain. Test surface: Re-coding VCE-style interview data, retreat adverse-event reports, and teacher case consultations for prior training, current method, failure type, and interpretive frame.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy modalities

Structural translation: A therapeutic capacity such as attention to emotion is not neutral. CBT, psychoanalysis, Internal Family Systems, somatic therapy, and exposure therapy train attention toward different transformations, such as belief revision, transference interpretation, parts differentiation, nervous-system regulation, or fear extinction.

Prediction: Clients and therapists crossing modalities should show grain-specific resistance: CBT-trained clients may convert psychoanalytic free association into problem solving, somatic-therapy clients may treat cognitive restructuring as disembodiment, and IFS-trained clients may over-personify processes in exposure work. Successful integrative therapy should include explicit re-graining, not only adding techniques.

Would count against it: Therapeutic skills transfer without directional residue, or outcomes and failures are predicted only by alliance, diagnosis, and treatment dose with no benefit from prior-modality grain.
