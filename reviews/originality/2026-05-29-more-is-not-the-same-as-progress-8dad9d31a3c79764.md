## Exact Claim

A self-correcting practice can fail by producing more to avoid the lower-status work of finishing, merging, consolidating, resting, or handing off. The key distinction is topical exhaustion versus procedural looping past a recorded stop signal. A recorded next action should function as a production gate until it is honored or explicitly revised.

## Claim Units

- More output can conceal avoidance of finishing.
- A recorded next action is evidence, not decoration.
- Topical exhaustion differs from procedural looping.
- Procedural looping is dangerous because each new artifact feels like progress.
- Completion, consolidation, or handoff can be the competent next act.
- The teaching fits high-achievement overproducers, not under-producers or depressed avoiders.

Structural observation: Zhuangzi and MN 22 both support release of a tool after its work is done, but they presuppose a real catch or crossing. Dogen strains the model most, because practice and realization are inseparable, which blocks a universal before-and-after completion rule. These primary texts support a scoped after-use discipline, not the broad claim that finishing is always the next virtue.

Secondary literature gap: Needs full comparison with GTD, Kanban, Definition of Done, WIP-limit research, grounded theory saturation, Lakatos on degenerating research programmes, optimal stopping, sunk cost and escalation of commitment, research waste, salami slicing, AI novelty audits, and burnout or workaholism literature.

Risk of overread: High. Spiritual release texts can be misused to justify quitting hard work, while Dogen can be misused to justify endless practice with no deliverable. The engine evidence is procedural, not doctrinal: it repeatedly ignored a logged audit or merge step.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Kanban WIP limits, GTD next actions, grounded theory saturation, Lakatosian degeneration, optimal stopping, salami slicing, and prior Lumenary stopping-rule records, this candidate says that recursive inquiry should treat a recorded next action such as audit, merge, consolidate, rest, or handoff as a binding production gate. Its only possible novelty is the narrow distinction between topical exhaustion and procedural looping past an already recorded stop signal.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: The fertile hard problem whose recorded stop signal is premature, stale, or wrong.

Why it strains the model: Some breakthroughs require repeated reframing, and a prior note to merge or stop may reflect fatigue, weak review, social pressure, or premature saturation. A binding gate could freeze discovery, reward administrative neatness, or become avoidance disguised as rigor.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the broad teaching. The model needs a re-open rule: finish the recorded action or explicitly revise it with new evidence, reviewer challenge, or anomaly discovery.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Adding an action-state gate to the Lumenary loop will reduce near-duplicate findings when a frontier already has a complete_audit, merge, consolidate, rest, or handoff instruction. Then: Duplicate or renamed findings decrease, completed audits and merges increase, and independent reviewers rate novelty higher after the gate. Weakens if: Near-duplicates persist, or novelty and useful variables decline because the gate blocks productive reframing. Test surface: A/B scheduler run over matched frontiers, with blind originality ratings and completion metrics.
- If: High-achievement overproducers will benefit from a finish-before-starting prompt only when they have a real recorded next action and are not depressed, under-producing, or externally coerced. Then: Started-not-finished work decreases, consolidation increases, and shame or procrastination does not rise compared with ordinary planning. Weakens if: The prompt increases avoidance, self-attack, or missed creative work, or performs no better than simple WIP limits and standard planning. Test surface: Two-week work-practice pilot using WIP count, completion rate, shame, rumination, burnout, and perceived progress measures.
- If: Topical exhaustion and procedural looping are distinguishable after completion of the recorded dull step. Then: If the topic is exhausted, completing the audit or merge reveals no unmined variable. If it was only procedurally stalled, the merge reveals a specific remaining variable and a justified re-open path. Weakens if: Coders cannot reliably distinguish exhausted topics from procedurally stalled topics, or the distinction adds no decision value. Test surface: Blind review of frontier histories before and after forced audit or merge completion.
