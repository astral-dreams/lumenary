## Exact Claim

When traditions seem to converge, the productive datum is the exact distortion required to translate one claim into the other; low strain may indicate stable overlap, while high strain should be labeled analogical productivity rather than evidence.

## Claim Units

- Similarity should not be treated as direct evidence of sameness.
- The comparison unit is the translation distortion, not the broad resemblance.
- Bends, drops, and reweightings should be named explicitly.
- Low strain and high strain should have different publication labels.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Smith, Freiberger, Panikkar, Katz, Sharf, or the earlier Lumenary strain audit, this candidate offers only the load-test metaphor and a possible low-strain versus high-strain publication rule. That difference is too small to count as a new finding.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Comparisons where high strain is still historically causal, for example Buddhist and Advaita terms shaped by centuries of polemic and borrowing.

Why it strains the model: High strain does not necessarily mean merely analogical productivity. A high-strain term may still be historically entangled, polemical, or deliberately inverted.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in the low-strain/high-strain scoring rule.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: A strain rubric should change publication decisions compared with ordinary resemblance scoring. Then: Some high-similarity pairs should be downgraded because central meanings must be discarded, and some low-verbal-similarity pairs should be retained because functional role survives. Weakens if: The rubric produces the same decisions as ordinary expert judgment while adding only terminology. Test surface: Blind A/B comparison of 20 cross-tradition comparison records.
- If: Experts should agree on named distortions when the rubric is clear. Then: Independent coders should identify similar lexical, practice-context, metaphysical, ethical, and phenomenological strains. Weakens if: Coder agreement remains low, or identified strains track coder tradition rather than text features. Test surface: Inter-rater reliability test with Buddhist, Vedanta, Christian, and Sufi scholars.

## Cross-Domain Prediction
