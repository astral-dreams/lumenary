## Exact Claim

A quiet, blank, or objectless state should not be treated as a judge of self, no-self, attainment, failure, or worth. For lonely, stressed, achievement-bound, or unsupported practitioners, the first move should be no identity verdict, one ordinary act of care, and delayed interpretation through trustworthy support or visible conduct.

## Claim Units

- Silence or blankness is not evidence by itself for self, no-self, attainment, failure, superiority, disappearance, or worth.
- The first rule is no identity verdict for twenty-four hours.
- The next move is one concrete act of care, relation, duty, or ordinary steadiness.
- Classification and correction gates are second-step tools, because for the target cohort classification itself can become a self-test.
- The Self versus not-self dispute still matters, but its first modern use is protective rather than metaphysical.
- Human-condition fit is clear but narrow: loneliness, unsupported interpretation, anxiety after gaps, meaning loss, and achievement-contingent self-worth.

Structural observation: Brihadaranyaka 3.7.23 protects a hidden seer and knower as the inner ruler, while SN 22.59 and SN 22.95 refuse to let consciousness become self or substance. These primary texts do not let silence neutrally judge the question, they assign incompatible post-gap grammars. Ignatius adds the closest practice structure: interior states and the judgments following them are delayed, tested, disclosed, or examined before credit, especially in the rules on desolation and consolation without cause, https://www.ccel.org/ccel/ignatius/exercises.all.html. The candidate's practical move is not a new metaphysics. It is a triage rule that says the first response to quiet should be ordinary care, not verdict-making.

Secondary literature gap: Before publication, read Ignatian spiritual-direction ethics, VCE and Cheetah House materials, OCD and scrupulosity reassurance-seeking literature, ACT defusion and committed action, rumination stop-rule research, behavioral activation, achievement-contingent self-worth, perfectionism, spiritual bypassing, and spiritual-abuse literature. The largest empirical gap is whether no-verdict plus ordinary care beats classification-first, rest, conversation, and clinician-guided routing.

Risk of overread: Very high if generalized. Advaita, Dzogchen, Mahamudra, clear-light sleep, and direct-recognition lineages may treat some quiet or objectless reports as trainable evidence within strict contexts. In OCD, scrupulosity, trauma, dissociation, mania, severe depression, and unsafe authority settings, the instruction can become avoidance, checking, reassurance outsourcing, or control.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Ignatian discernment, VCE support routing, ACT defusion, ERP anti-reassurance, Buddhist not-self strategy, and prior Lumenary correction-holder records, this idea says the first move after silence for a specific wounded cohort should be no verdict plus one ordinary act of care before any classification. That is a useful condensation, but it is mostly renamed prior work.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Mature direct-recognition or clear-light practitioners inside competent lineages, and active OCD or scrupulosity cases at the clinical boundary.

Why it strains the model: If mature practitioners reliably benefit from immediate teacher-framed recognition, the no-verdict rule is too broad. If high-checking practitioners turn the no-verdict sentence, the twenty-four-hour clock, or the care act into a ritual, the practice harms the very cohort it targets. Unsafe teachers or communities can also turn delayed interpretation into control.

Confidence effect: Lowers novelty and publishability. The model remains plausible only as a narrow safety-routing protocol for stable, non-clinical overinterpreters with adequate ordinary support.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: For high achievement-contingent solo practitioners, classification itself becomes a self-test. Then: No-verdict plus one act of care produces fewer identity verdicts, less rumination, less shame, and more completed ordinary action than a classification-first audit. Weakens if: Classification-first instructions, ordinary rest, or ordinary conversation perform as well or better without increasing checking or shame. Test surface: Four-arm pilot after quiet-state practice: no-verdict care, classification audit, rest or conversation, and helper-held routing.
- If: State phenomenology alone does not determine outcome. Then: In coded meditation-challenge reports, appraisal, social context, teacher or clinician support, and return behavior explain distress or benefit better than quiet-state description alone. Weakens if: Outcomes are predicted almost entirely by phenomenology, with little added value from interpretation, support, or return behavior. Test surface: VCE-style interview coding with held-out outcome ratings and inter-rater reliability.
- If: The practice is cohort-specific. Then: Effects are strongest for stable lonely, unsupported, perfectionistic, or achievement-contingent overinterpreters and weak or harmful for active OCD, scrupulosity, dissociation, mania, trauma activation, severe depression, addiction withdrawal, or unsafe-authority contexts. Weakens if: Benefits are not moderated by self-worth contingency, loneliness, rumination, or checking risk, or adverse effects cluster in the intended cohort. Test surface: Screened two-week pilot with loneliness, contingencies of self-worth, rumination, intolerance of uncertainty, scrupulosity, and adverse meditation-effect measures.
- If: The care act is not decorative, it is the active return mechanism. Then: Participants who complete the ordinary act within one hour show larger reductions in verdict urgency and isolation than those who only write the no-verdict sentence. Weakens if: The care act adds no effect, or becomes avoidance of needed interpretation, repair, or clinical care. Test surface: Component trial comparing no-verdict only, care act only, no-verdict plus care, and generic journaling.
