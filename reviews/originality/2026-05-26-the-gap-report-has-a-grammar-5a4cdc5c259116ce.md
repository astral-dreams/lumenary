## Exact Claim

After an objectless or silent state, the first later report is not neutral memory. It is a trained admissibility grammar that assigns evidential weight to the subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, or relation.

## Claim Units

- Post-gap reports distribute authority instead of simply preserving memory.
- Advaita deep-sleep arguments weight the subject or witness in reports such as I slept and knew nothing.
- Early Buddhist cessation sources weight absence, transition, conditioned development, and post-emergence inclination rather than a surviving owner.
- Daoist sitting-in-forgetfulness weights transformed aftereffect rather than hidden witness continuity.
- Sufi dhikr or fana language may weight the Remembered or relation over the rememberer.
- The distribution of evidential weight can be used as a cross-tradition comparison variable.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 4.3.23 with Shankara commentary, https://vivekavani.com/bru4c3v23/
- MN 44 Culavedalla Sutta plus AN 9.34, https://www.dhammatalks.org/suttas/MN/MN44.html and https://suttas.github.io/an9.34

Structural observation: Brihadaranyaka 4.3.23 treats apparent non-seeing in deep sleep as compatible with uninterrupted witness vision, because the object side has fallen away. MN 44 treats cessation through prior development, ceasing and arising fabrications, and post-emergence contact with emptiness, the signless, and the undirected. AN 9.34 makes the absence of feeling part of the reason cessation can be called pleasant. The contrast supports the proposed grammar, subject continuity versus conditioned transition and absence, but also shows that the grammar is already explicit in primary sources.

Secondary literature gap: I did not find an exact source that classifies Advaita, Buddhist, Daoist, and Sufi post-gap reports by subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, and relation. But Evan Thompson's Open MIND essay gives a very close Advaita versus Nyaya analysis of the report I slept peacefully and I did not know anything, including memory, inference, minimal selfhood, and retrospective awareness. Alcaraz-Sanchez's micro-phenomenological study also directly studies reportability, pronoun use, and possible post-hoc self attribution in objectless sleep reports.

Risk of overread: High. The model may make grammar carry what traditions assign to realization, teacher verification, metaphysics, ritual, or ethical fruit. Translation also matters, since first-person grammar in English may not map cleanly onto Sanskrit, Pali, Chinese, Arabic, or Persian terms.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Thompson on Advaita and Nyaya, or micro-phenomenology on objectless sleep reports, this idea says the evidential clause selected after a gap is a cross-tradition admissibility rule that can be coded across subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, and relation.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Tibetan clear-light sleep and contemporary objectless-awareness sleep reports that claim awareness during the gap, not only after it.

Why it strains the model: If trained practitioners can give stable, carefully interviewed reports of awareness while the gap is unfolding, then the later report is not merely assigning evidence after an uninspectable silence. The model would need to distinguish in-gap phenomenology from post-gap evidential framing.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence from candidate discovery to extended. The anomaly does not reject the model, but it makes post-gap grammar secondary rather than foundational in some cases.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Traditions should show stable differences in which clause of a post-gap report they treat as admissible evidence. Then: Advaita materials should privilege witness or subject continuity; early Buddhist cessation materials should privilege cessation, transition, and fruit; Daoist zuowang should privilege aftereffect and forgetting; dhikr or fana materials should privilege relation or the Remembered. Weakens if: If evidence weights vary mainly by translator, genre, or individual teacher rather than tradition and method, the model loses predictive value. Test surface: Blind coding of primary texts, practice manuals, and practitioner interviews using subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, relation, teacher recognition, and behavioral fruit tags.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should shift evidential weighting when switching practice frames. Then: The same practitioner speaks differently about sleep, cessation, or objectless meditation under Advaita, Theravada, and Tibetan prompts. Weakens if: Practitioners preserve the same evidential interpretation across frames, or report that the distinction is imposed by the interviewer. Test surface: Structured interviews with dual-trained Advaita, Buddhist, Sufi, Daoist, and micro-phenomenology-trained practitioners.
- If: Original-language terms should support the proposed categories, not merely English pronoun artifacts. Then: Sanskrit, Pali, Chinese, Arabic, or Persian sources should mark subject, absence, transition, aftereffect, or relation through recurring lexical and grammatical patterns. Weakens if: The categories disappear or become unstable when checked against original terms and multiple translations. Test surface: Close read of Brihadaranyaka 4.3.23, Yoga Sutra 1.10, MN 44, AN 9.34, Zhuangzi zuowang passages, and primary dhikr or fana texts.

- Sufi teachers familiar with dhikr, fana, and baqa

Questions:
- When you hear a report after deep sleep, cessation, forgetting, or remembrance, which part of the report counts as evidence?
- Is this report-weighting obvious within your training, or does naming it change how the practice is understood?
- Would your tradition reject another tradition's report because it weights the wrong clause?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say the distinction is already standard as pramana, memory versus inference, fruition review, teacher verification, or adab of remembrance, and that the new labels do not change practice.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners can use the rubric to explain why cross-tradition disputes persist even when phenomenological reports sound similar.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Anesthesia, blackout, trauma, and psychedelic integration reports

Structural translation: After an interval that cannot be inspected while it occurs, communities decide whether to trust subject continuity, absence of recall, physiological transition, aftereffects, or relational testimony.

Prediction: Clinical and legal settings should show different admissibility grammars for blackout reports, anesthesia emergence, trauma memory, and psychedelic ego dissolution, and these grammars should predict what is treated as evidence of continuity, harm, or transformation.

Would count against it: If these domains use no stable admissibility patterns, or if all rely only on external measurement regardless of later report structure, the proposed structure may be limited to contemplative rhetoric.
