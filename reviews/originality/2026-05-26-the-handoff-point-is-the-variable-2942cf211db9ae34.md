## Exact Claim

The useful comparative variable is not effort versus gift on a fixed scale, but the stage at which a trained function becomes unclaimable by the practitioner, who or what then carries it, and which safeguards appear when that transfer fails.

## Claim Units

- Effort and gift should be modeled as timed transfer rather than fixed opposition.
- Traditions train functions such as receiving, agency, memory, reflexivity, and integration, then may forbid ordinary-self ownership of those same functions.
- Other-power, direct-introduction, effort-centered, and practice-realization paths differ by transfer point, support holder, credited source, forbidden claimant, safeguard, and repair.
- This can become a coding rubric for manuals and practitioner interviews.

## Primary Text Close Read

Sources:
- SN 45.8, Magga-vibhanga Sutta, https://www.accesstoinsight.org/tipitaka/sn/sn45/sn45.008.than.html
- Shinran, Tannisho, A Record in Lament of Divergences, https://shinranworks.com/related-works-by-other-authors/a-record-in-lament-of-divergences/

Structural observation: SN 45.8 makes right effort operational and practitioner-facing: desire, endeavor, persistence, intent, and maintenance of skillful qualities are all assigned to the monk's practice field. Tannisho reverses the claimant grammar: nembutsu is not a practice or good act when it arises from Other Power, and claims such as my disciples are rejected because the working belongs to Amida. The comparison supports a real difference in claimant policy, but Tannisho is not a late handoff after training. It often refuses self-claim from the entrance, so the model needs a no-transfer or analyst-frame-refused value.

Secondary literature gap: The search found close prior structures in religious coping styles, self-power and other-power, Dogen practice-realization, Dzogchen direct introduction, Engler on ego strength before no-self, the Buddhist raft and upaya pattern, and local Lumenary capacity-ledger and credit-distribution work. I did not find the exact field list as a cross-tradition coding sheet, but the core agency-allocation and means-end reversal are well established.

Risk of overread: High. The model risks imposing a staged transfer on traditions that deny transfer, collapse before and after, or shift grammar by genre and audience. Longchenpa's Trekcho gives recognition without contrived effort but also gives preliminaries, devotion, vows, conduct, and post-recognition practice, which means production is denied only at some moments, not globally.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Pargament, self-power and other-power debates, Dogen practice-realization, and local Lumenary credit models, this idea says the decisive unit is a timed transfer for each required function, with observable safeguards and repairs at that transfer point.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Radical Jodo Shinshu, especially Tannisho chapters 8 and 10, where nembutsu is not the practitioner's practice or good act and no working is true working.

Why it strains the model: If even hearing, saying, entrusting, and gratitude are Amida's working rather than trained human functions, then the analyst's transfer language may smuggle self-power into a tradition built to reject that framing. Dogen also strains the model because practice-realization collapses bridge and arrival rather than transferring ownership from one holder to another.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in universality and requires a refusal-category. The idea remains useful as a coding heuristic, but not as a claim that all traditions perform a handoff.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Manuals should mark transfer points with special safeguards rather than generic warnings. Then: Pure Land texts should warn against calculation, Dzogchen texts against contrivance and premature recognition, Soto texts against gaining idea, early Buddhist texts against appropriation of aggregates or attainment ownership, and Gita-style karma-yoga texts against fruit ownership. Weakens if: Warnings cluster by institution, teacher temperament, ethics, or historical polemic rather than by transfer point. Test surface: Blind stage-coded manual analysis across at least three texts per tradition.
- If: Dual-trained practitioners should report shifts in permitted first-person grammar when changing methods. Then: Interviewees can distinguish I cultivate, I entrust, recognition happens, and just sitting enacts, without being prompted with the rubric. Weakens if: Practitioners report no felt shift, or shifts are fully explained by vocabulary, teacher style, temperament, or institutional identity. Test surface: Micro-phenomenological interviews with dual-trained practitioners and teachers.
- If: The handoff rubric should add predictive value beyond Pargament-style responsibility allocation and the local capacity ledger. Then: Two practices with the same broad credit type but different transfer timing show different safeguards and repair instructions. Weakens if: Support locus and credit-distribution coding predict the same outcomes without needing handoff timing. Test surface: Inter-rater coding of matched pairs such as Soto and Dzogchen, Pure Land and Christian grace, Theravada and karma-yoga.
- If: Failed transfers should produce type-specific repair practices. Then: Self-power calculation is repaired by hearing or entrusting, contrivance by relaxing into introduced awareness, gaining idea by just-sitting correction, and attainment ownership by no-self or humility teachings. Weakens if: Repair practices do not map to transfer failures, or all traditions prescribe the same repairs for the same student problems. Test surface: Teacher interviews and case reports from retreat, pastoral, and sangha settings.

- Where in your training sequence does a student become especially likely to claim what the practice says cannot be claimed?
- Does naming support holder, credited source, forbidden claimant, safeguard, and repair change how you would guide a student?

Answers that reduce originality: Practitioners say the idea is obvious stage pedagogy, already covered by self-power and other-power, grace and works, upaya, no-gaining, or religious coping. Pure Land teachers say the transfer frame itself is doctrinally misleading.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners say the rubric predicts concrete failure points and repair instructions better than effort-gift language, especially in cross-training cases and direct-introduction settings.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Psychotherapy and transformative education

Structural translation: Identity-changing work trains capacities that later must be relocated from client ego, therapist, group, medication, protocol, insight, or ongoing practice.

Prediction: Methods that move agency from therapist or group to client, or from willpower to acceptance, should show special safeguards at transfer points, such as relapse planning, integration, alliance repair, and warnings against premature independence.

Would count against it: Ordinary skill acquisition shows the same warnings, or outcomes are fully explained by severity, alliance, dosage, social support, and resources without any transfer-specific signal.
