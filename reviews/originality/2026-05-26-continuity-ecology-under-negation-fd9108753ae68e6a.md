## Exact Claim

Self negating or self transcending paths preserve enough receptivity and reliability for transformation through a continuity ecology. This continuity may be internally managed, externally scaffolded, relationally entrusted, ritually stabilized, institutionally maintained, or theologically relocated. The path's dissolution target may pressure where continuity migrates, residue policy governs what remains authorized after negation, and practice ecology may either express doctrine or historically precede it.

## Claim Units

- Self negating paths require transformability, not necessarily a minimum self.
- Continuity can be distributed across body, habit, teacher, ritual, vow, community, grace, text, institution, and environment.
- Dissolution target and residue policy should predict where continuity is preserved or relocated.
- Practice ecology may cause doctrine as often as doctrine causes practice ecology.
- The model is testable by coding doctrine, supports, warnings, verification methods, and failure modes from separate source sets.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike extended mind religious cognition, Asad's discursive tradition, Lindbeck's doctrine-as-grammar, VCE, Pargament, Buddhist continuity-without-self literature, and prior Lumenary ledgers, this idea says that self-negating paths preserve transformability through a codable continuity ecology and predicts that dissolution target plus residue policy will partly forecast where continuity is stored after institutional, ritual, and historical controls.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Dogen's practice-realization and inherited-practice cases.

Why it strains the model: Dogen's Bendowa denies a clean boundary in realization and denies a beginning in practice. Inherited religious participation can also preserve continuity before any explicit self-negating doctrine is consciously adopted. In both cases, the model may impose a migration story where the tradition presents no transfer, no threshold, or no separate continuity problem.

Confidence effect: This lowers confidence in any universal causal claim. The model should remain an interpretive coding heuristic unless split-source tests show predictive gain over practice-realization, socialization, and institutional history.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: Dissolution target and residue policy exert independent pressure on continuity placement. Then: Coders who see only doctrinal negation passages can predict above baseline whether later manuals locate support in effort, attention, teacher, vow, grace, ritual, community, text, or no-boundary practice-realization. Weakens if: Prediction fails, or ritual form, institution type, teacher authority, socialization pattern, or historical borrowing predicts support placement better than dissolution target and residue policy. Test surface: Split-source blind coding across Theravada, Advaita, Soto Zen, Jodo Shinshu, Dzogchen or Mahamudra, Christian grace, Sufi fana and baqa, and secular mindfulness manuals.
- If: Continuity ecology is not a decorative description but a failure-mode predictor. Then: When the path's primary support holder is absent or confused, failures cluster by support type: effort paths show striving and fatigue, teacher-held paths show dependency or disorientation, vow or grace-held paths show calculation and doubt, no-boundary paths show laxity or antinomian misunderstanding. Weakens if: Failure modes are better explained by dose, trauma history, charisma, group norms, mental-health risk, socioeconomic stress, or ordinary teacher quality after controls. Test surface: Practitioner interviews, adverse-event reports, VCE-style coding, and training-program records.
- If: Practice ecology can precede doctrine but should leave historical traces when it does. Then: Where support structures predate doctrinal refinement, later doctrine explicitly explains, legitimates, or polices already existing teacher, ritual, vow, or institutional arrangements. Weakens if: Historical cases show no relation between prior ecology and later doctrine, or show doctrine changing without support-ecology change and without new warning or verification patterns. Test surface: Historical comparison of Shinran communities, Soto monastic practice, Theravada reform movements, modern mindfulness programs, and Christian grace controversies.
- If: Minimum receptivity has observable thresholds rather than being a trivial requirement. Then: Traditions screen, sequence, or repair students around capacities to be addressed, receive instruction or grace, register transformation, continue practice, and avoid obstructing the path's support holder. Weakens if: Practitioners cannot identify distinct thresholds beyond generic motivation, ethics, or mental stability, or thresholds do not vary with support ecology. Test surface: Teacher interviews and manual coding with separate fields for entry, warning, verification, and repair.

Answers that support contribution: Practitioners independently identify continuity holders and say the rubric helps predict concrete warnings, screening, sequencing, or repair instructions that existing lineage vocabulary did not make explicit.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Addiction recovery and psychotherapy for compulsive self-control collapse.

Structural translation: When an intervention negates autonomous control, it must relocate continuity into a sponsor, group, higher power, routine, script, therapist relation, values commitment, medication, or environment design.

Prediction: Programs that more strongly deny individual self-mastery should rely more visibly on external or relational continuity supports and should show predictable failures when participants convert surrender into self-ownership, isolate from the group, or lose routine anchors.

Would count against it: No relationship appears between autonomy-negating language and support relocation, or outcomes are fully predicted by generic social support, treatment intensity, diagnosis, medication, and socioeconomic stability.
