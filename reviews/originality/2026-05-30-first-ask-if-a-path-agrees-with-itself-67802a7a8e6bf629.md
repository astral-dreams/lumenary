## Exact Claim

A translation-strain score needs a denominator. Before claiming that two traditions converge or clash on a claim unit, Lumenary must measure weighted within-tradition variance for the same unit. If internal spread is as wide as the between-tradition gap, the comparison is selection-dependent and should receive no evidential weight.

## Claim Units

- Between-tradition strain is unreliable without within-tradition variance.
- Traditions vary across sub-schools, stages, genres, institutions, and polemical eras.
- If internal variance rivals the cross-tradition gap, the verdict depends on which internal variant was selected.
- Within-tradition variance must be weighted by operative practice centrality, not raw textual existence.
- A comparison can pass a provenance gate but fail a variance gate.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike ordinary internal-diversity cautions, Freiberger's selection framework, Smith's redescription model, and world-religions essentialism critiques, this candidate proposes a specific admissibility test: a cross-tradition strain score has evidential weight only when between-tradition strain exceeds weighted within-tradition variance for the same claim unit.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Stage-specific and operative-consensus cases, such as breath attention in early Buddhist practice, basic Vinaya rules, or a catechism-level Christian confession.

Why it strains the model: A tradition can have enormous textual and historical variance while still giving practitioners a narrow operative instruction at a defined stage. If the denominator counts every variant equally, it will falsely mark stable practice instructions as selection-dependent. If it weights only operative variants, the model becomes dependent on disputed judgments about authority, institution, stage, and living practice.

Confidence effect: Lowers confidence in any simple variance metric. Raises the need for operative weighting and inter-rater reliability before the gate can carry public evidential weight.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: High internal-variance units should produce unstable convergence verdicts. Then: Coders assigned Theravada anatta versus Pudgalavada person, and Shankara-style self-recognition versus scriptural-testimony Advaita, will return contradictory convergence or divergence verdicts for the Atman and anatta comparison. Weakens if: Convergence verdicts stay stable no matter which legitimate internal variant is selected. Test surface: Blind coder experiment with controlled claim units and assigned internal variants.
- If: Low internal-variance units should produce stable verdicts. Then: Comparisons on narrower practice instructions, such as attending to breath, ethical restraint, or confession of fault, will show stronger agreement among coders than metaphysical self comparisons. Weakens if: Verdict stability is not predicted by internal-variance scores, or low-variance units produce just as much disagreement. Test surface: Inter-rater comparison study across high-variance and low-variance claim units.
- If: Operative weighting matters more than raw textual spread. Then: Weighting variants by living practice centrality, stage, institution, and teacher use will change which comparisons fail the variance gate. Weakens if: Raw textual variance and operative weighted variance produce the same decisions, or experts cannot agree on weights above chance. Test surface: Expert and practitioner weighting exercise with inter-rater reliability analysis.
- If: The variance gate is orthogonal to the provenance gate. Then: Some comparisons will be historically independent but internally unstable, while others will be internally stable but historically entangled through borrowing, shared translators, or modern discourse. Weakens if: Provenance and internal variance always move together, making the new gate redundant. Test surface: Paired application of provenance-first coding and variance-gate coding to the same comparison set.
- If: The self-agreement check helps a specific modern cohort without becoming avoidance. Then: Stable reflective readers exposed to online comparative slogans show less all-is-one universalism and less cynical dismissal, while still choosing one concrete practice action within a week. Weakens if: The practice increases rumination, paralysis, distrust, or avoidance of commitment compared with a simple source-humility prompt. Test surface: Small randomized pilot with measures for rumination, action completion, cynicism, false-harmony endorsement, and practice commitment.
