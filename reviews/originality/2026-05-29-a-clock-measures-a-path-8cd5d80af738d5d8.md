## Exact Claim

A clock measures path-dependent physical change along one worldline, not a universal river of time. The felt now is local enough for conduct and responsibility, and modern suffering worsens when another person's clock becomes a verdict on one's own life rather than a tool for coordination.

## Claim Units

- A clock measures repeatable transitions along a worldline.
- There is no single cosmic now that ranks all lives as early, late, ahead, or behind.
- The felt now is where records have reached this body and action remains possible.
- Clocks should coordinate obligations without becoming metaphysical or social verdicts.
- The modern use case is time anxiety, digital comparison, burnout, and feeling late in life.

## Unlike Statement

Unlike Einstein, Minkowski, local becoming, Rovelli, Page-Wootters, Dogen, social clock theory, chrononormativity, and the prior Lumenary clock record, this idea's possible contribution is a testable practical distinction: separate owed coordination times from borrowed comparison times, then see whether that reduces behind-in-life distress without weakening punctuality or responsibility.

## Anomaly Probe

Candidate: Caregiving, medicine, teamwork, school, transport, and wage labor create shared deadlines that are not merely borrowed comparison clocks.

Why it strains the model: Some external clocks are obligations of justice, safety, and trust. If path language lets a person dismiss medication times, childcare pickup, workplace dependencies, or promises, the model becomes harmful and false at the practice level.

Confidence effect: Keeps the idea at extended. It can only survive by sharply separating coordination obligations from status comparison.

## Falsifiable Predictions

- If: The owed-coordination versus borrowed-comparison distinction has practical value. Then: People trained in the distinction report less behind-in-life and comparative shame language while keeping equal or better punctuality, promise-keeping, and planning behavior. Weakens if: Missed obligations increase, avoidance rises, or outcomes are no better than self-compassion, time management, or ordinary social-clock psychoeducation. Test surface: Randomized pilot with deadline-stressed students, caregivers, founders, and knowledge workers.
- If: Proper-time-first pedagogy changes metaphysical misconceptions. Then: Students introduced to worldlines and proper time before block-universe diagrams will make fewer claims that relativity proves the present is fake or that there is one hidden cosmic schedule. Weakens if: Misconception rates match standard lessons, or the path framing worsens physics accuracy. Test surface: Physics education experiment with pre-post misconception coding.
- If: Behind-in-life suffering is partly driven by borrowed social clocks rather than workload alone. Then: Digital comparison exposure and perceived social-clock deviation predict behind-in-life distress after controlling for actual deadlines, workload, income, age, and caregiving load. Weakens if: Distress is fully explained by material overload, insecurity, discrimination, sleep loss, or clinical anxiety, with no added effect from comparison clocks. Test surface: Survey and diary study using social comparison, social-clock deviation, workload, and distress measures.

## Cross-Domain Prediction

Domain: Distributed systems and team operations

Structural translation: A distributed system has local process histories and coordination protocols, not one privileged global now. The practical question is which timestamps mark real dependencies and which merely create misleading rank comparisons.

Prediction: Teams using dependency-aware local timelines, with explicit interface commitments, will show less false lateness blame and better bottleneck diagnosis than teams managed only by global comparison dashboards.

Would count against it: A single global deadline dashboard predicts delays, responsibility, morale, and coordination failures as well as or better than dependency-aware local timelines.
