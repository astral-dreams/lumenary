# Can investigators distinguish capacity-internal direction from doctrinal framing...

**The tension:** Geyi conflation. Wu is cosmogonically generative; sunyata is deconstructive. Different functions despite surface resemblance.

## Steelman And Challenge

**Steelman:** The Grain of Capacity is strongest when read as a disciplined anti-neutrality thesis. It does not merely say that traditions interpret the same experience differently. It says that the abilities a path asks a practitioner to cultivate, attention, discrimination, self-observation, trust, longing, and integration, are already shaped toward the kind of release or recognition the path will later authorize. This makes the model useful because it yields predictions: cross-training should leave directional residue, and adverse effects should differ by mismatch type, not only by intensity, trauma history, or missing preparation.

The Grain of Capacity should survive, but in narrower form. Its strongest version is not that every prerequisite is inherently carved toward a final dissolution. That overreads some texts and risks making the comparative analyst's map look like the practitioner's lived training. The stronger claim is conditional: where a tradition explicitly trains a capacity through final-aim language, the capacity likely carries grain; where it trains a more general loosening, restraint, or attentional stability, the decisive difference may be the custody rule applied after ordinary ownership is weakened. This distinction would also protect the model from the geyi problem. It would not force wu, sunyata, apophasis, witness, and surrender into one generic family of dissolutions. Instead, it would ask at each step: is the practice shaping the capacity, authorizing the handoff, or interpreting the result after the fact?

## Rebuttal



**Crux:** Can a tradition's loosening operation be described without reference to what it loosens toward? If vipassana loosening, self-inquiry loosening, and centering-prayer loosening are genuinely different operations shaped by their destinations, then grain is structural. If they share a common attentional operation that only diverges at the custody moment, then the custody model is stronger. The question is whether the verb already contains the direction or whether the direction is supplied by the sentence the verb appears in.

## Counter-Rebuttal

The revision became transformed. It is stronger in local accuracy, especially because it stops treating grain as a uniform feature of all traditions, but it is no longer the same thesis as the original. The original claim said preparatory capacities are never neutral and already run toward the dissolution they serve. The revised claim says direction may be deep, shallow, or supplied at custody. That is a better model, but it shifts from a single structural law to a comparative typology. I accept that shift. The proponent's Vivekachudamani argument is the strongest evidence: discrimination between eternal and non-eternal is not a neutral skill later aimed at Brahman. It is aim-shaped from the first exercise. I am less convinced that MN 10 proves the same depth, because non-appropriative framing may be strong without proving that the felt capacity is already directionally constrained before doctrinal interpretation. Pure Land remains the cleanest warning that not every path builds the receiving shape through prior self-generated exercise. The final standard should be stricter than either side's first formulation. Ask where direction enters, define the coding rules before reading the case, and then test whether practitioners actually report the predicted residue. If the typology can do that, the grain model survives as a useful research program. If not, it becomes an elegant comparative vocabulary with too much room to protect itself.

## What Emerged

**Outcome:** candidate_transcendence

The exchange did not select a winner. It transformed the dispute between capacity grain and custody of attention into a layered model of where direction enters contemplative practice. The proponent conceded that some traditions fit custody-only or shallow grain, while the challenger conceded that some instructions show deep grain from the first exercise. The main product is a new testable synthesis: direction may be carried by lexical definition, affective posture, attentional operation, bodily routine, teacher authorization, community interpretation, or post-insight integration.

**Convergence claim:** Contemplative capacities should not be presumed neutral, but direction does not always enter at the same point. Some practices build direction into the capacity itself, some frame a transferable capacity, and some assign direction at a handoff or authorization moment.

**Unresolved crux:** Can investigators distinguish capacity-internal direction from doctrinal framing and handoff authorization in practitioner experience, using prior coding rules and blind assessment?

**Next frontier question:** Where, exactly, does a practice place direction before, during, and after loosened ownership, and can practitioners feel that placement before doctrine explains it?
