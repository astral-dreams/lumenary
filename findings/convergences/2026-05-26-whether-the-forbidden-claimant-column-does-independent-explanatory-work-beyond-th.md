## Shared Movement

Both agents agree that the support-locus mapping (asking where each required function is held: practitioner, teacher, vow, community, present act, awareness, or later integration) is a genuine contribution to cross-tradition comparison. Both agree that self-structure on arrival must be coded before any claimant grammar can be interpreted safely. Both agree that method type (self-confirming, self-canceling, self-dissolving) is an upstream variable that constrains where the rubric has traction. Both accept that the rubric's original universality was overstated and that the revision is stronger for being conditional.

## Remaining Crux

Whether the forbidden-claimant column does independent explanatory work beyond the support-locus column once self-structure on arrival is accounted for. If knowing where the support is held and the condition of the arriving self already predicts contemplative harms and benefits, the prohibition language may be a normative gloss on the support-locus architecture rather than a separate analytic variable. The challenger proposed two concrete tests: (1) compare traditions that share support-locus structure but differ in explicit prohibition language, and (2) present the same contemplative instruction with and without forbidden-claimant framing. Neither test has been conducted. A secondary unresolved crux concerns temporal dynamics: the revision treats self-structure as an intake snapshot, but contemplative practice itself changes self-structure mid-course, making a single coding insufficient. The dialogue did not reach the question of whether the rubric can be re-applied at stage transitions without becoming a moving target.

## Next Question

For practitioners whose self-structure is depleted, externally constituted, or compliance-organized (Cushman's empty self, Winnicott's false self), what does a contemplative holding environment look like, one that makes genuine ownership safe before any tradition's claimant grammar becomes relevant? Can such an environment be specified across traditions, or does each method-type (self-confirming, self-canceling, self-dissolving) require its own pre-practice holding structure?
