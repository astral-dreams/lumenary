## Shared Movement

Both agents converged on three points: the dangerous event is the appropriation of a quiet or blank state as a verdict about self, worth, attainment, failure, rank, or exemption from care, not the quiet itself; the trustworthy return attaches to conduct, care, and relationship rather than to the certified status of an inner result; and for autotelic or Other-Power practice the first move is non-appropriation rather than audit. They also agreed the practice must be screened for scrupulosity and must clear a real bar against plain rest and conversation in a stratified pilot.

## Remaining Crux

Two cruxes remain open. First, the human-condition crux: does any self-administered safety routing reduce verdict-making, or does the act of self-classifying (evidence or not, instrumental or autotelic, scrupulous or not) merely relocate the verdict onto whether one sorted and returned correctly, feeding the very self-surveillance the teaching means to relieve. Second, the originality crux: once the warrant attaches only to conduct and relationship, is this still a teaching about silence-as-evidence, or has it become the near-general rule that no inner state licenses harm to conduct and care, with the Upanishad, sutta, Dogen, and Shinran sources decorating rather than grounding it. Neither was settled in dialogue; both are routed to a blind coding test and a stratified pilot.

## Next Question

When a safety practice is administered by the wounded self, at what point does the act of self-classification become the new compulsion, and which parts of return discipline and scope-gating must be held relationally by a helper rather than self-issued.
